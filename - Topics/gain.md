## Source [^1]
- A measure of the ability of an electronic circuit, device, or apparatus to increase the magnitude of a given electrical input parameter. In a power amplifier the gain is the ratio of the power output by the amplifier to the power input to it. For a voltage amplifier that supplies relatively little power to the load, the gain is the ratio of the voltage developed across a specified load impedance to the input voltage. For a directional antenna the gain is the ratio of voltage generated in the direction of maximum sensitivity to that produced by the same signal in an omnidirectional antenna. Gain is often measured in decibels.
