## Source [^1]
- $n$. a potent opioid analgesic (see OPIATE) used mainly to relieve severe and persistent pain, particularly in terminally ill patients; it also induces feelings of euphoria. Common side-effects are nausea and vomiting, constipation, and drowsiness. With regular use, tolerance develops and dependence may occur.
## Source[^2]
- An alkaloid present in opium (see opiate). It is an analgesic and narcotic, used medically for the relief of severe pain.
## Source[^3]
- $n$. A bitter crystalline alkaloid, being a narcotic analgesic extracted from opium, in which it occurs in amounts up to 15 per cent, used as a narcotic analgesic and sedative. See also ADDICTION, DEPENDENCE (2), OPIOID-RELATED DISORDERS, SUBSTANCE DEPENDENCE, SUBSTANCE-INDUCED DISORDERS, SUBSTANCE-RELATED AND ADDICTIVE DISORDERS, SUBSTANCE USE DISORDERS. \[From Greek Morph(eus) the god of sleep and dreams + -ine indicating an organic compound]
