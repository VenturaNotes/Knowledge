## Source [^1]
- a white or greyish line in the periphery of the cornea, concentric with but separated from the edge by a clear zone. It begins above and below but may become a continuous ring. It consists of an infiltration of fatty material and is common in the elderly (arcus senilis). When it occurs in younger people (arcus juvenilis) it may indicate hyperlipidaemia. It does not affect vision.
