## Source [^1]
- the membranous structures that surround the embryo and contribute to the placenta and umbilical cord. They include the amnion, chorion, allantois, and yolk sac. In humans the allantois is always very small and by the end of pregnancy the amnion and chorion have fused into a single membrane and the yolk sac has disappeared.
## Source[^2]
- (embryonic membranes) The tissues produced by an animal embryo for protection and nutrition but otherwise taking no part in its development. The four membranes, which are called fetal membranes in humans, are the chorion, amnion, allantois, and yolk sac.
