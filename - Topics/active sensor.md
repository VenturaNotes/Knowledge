## Source [^1]
- An active sensor is a device with a transmitter that sends out a signal, light wavelength or electrons to be bounced off a target, with data gathered by the sensor upon their reflection.
## Source[^2]
- A sensor (see TRANSDUCER) that provides its own energy illumination source for whatever it is trying to observe. It sends pulses or steady beams out towards a target and then uses the reflections coming back to sense or image it. Some systems use time of flight to get an estimate of target distance. Examples include sonar, radar, and flash photography. See also PASSIVE SENSOR.
