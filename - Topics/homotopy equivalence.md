## Source [^1]
- Two topological spaces (or metric spaces) $X, Y$ are homotopy equivalent if there exist continuous functions $f: X \to Y$ and $g: Y \to X$ such that $g \circ f$ is homotopic to the identity map on $X$, and $f \circ g$ is homotopic to the identity map on $Y$. This is an equivalence relation, weaker than homeomorphic; for example, all convex subsets (see CONVEX SET) of $\mathbb{R}^n$ are homotopy equivalent to a point. Homotopy equivalent spaces have isomorphic fundamental groups.
