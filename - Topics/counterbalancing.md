## Source [^1]
- $n$. 
- (1) In experimental design, a method of controlling for the effects of an extraneous variable by ensuring that its effects are equal in all treatment conditions. For example, order effects can be counterbalanced by administering the various procedures in different sequences. 
- (2) In questionnaire design, a method of controlling for acquiescence response set by wording a test so that an affirmative answer is scored in the direction of the attribute being measured in half of the questions and in the opposite direction in the rest. See also BALANCED SCALE.
