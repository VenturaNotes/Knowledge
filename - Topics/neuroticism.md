## Source [^1]
- $n$. a dimension of personality derived from questionnaires and psychological tests. People with high scores in neuroticism are anxious and intense, emotionally unstable, and more prone to develop neurosis.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. A psychological condition or state characterized by neurosis. Also, one of the Big Five personality factors, ranging from one extreme of neuroticism, including such traits as nervousness, tenseness, moodiness, and temperamentality, to the opposite extreme of emotional stability. See also alPHA PERSONALITY FACTOR, CORE SELF-EVALUATION, GENERAL FACTOR OF PERSONALITY.
