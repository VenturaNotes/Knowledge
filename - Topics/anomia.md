## Source [^1]
- $n$. a form of aphasia in which the patient is unable to give the names of objects, although retaining an understanding of their use and the ability to put words together into speech. It sometimes occurs after a stroke or in certain types of dementia.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. Inability to name common objects or pictures of common objects; another name for anomic aphasia. \[From Greek $a$- without + onomia a name + -ia indicating a condition or quality]
