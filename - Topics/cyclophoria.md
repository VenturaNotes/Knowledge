## Source [^1]
- $n$. a type of squint (see STRABISMUS) in which the eye, when tested, tends to rotate slightly clockwise or anticlockwise.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. A form of heterophoria in which the vertical axis of one eye rotates to the left or right because of a weakness of one of the oblique muscles of the eye. Compare esotropia, exophoria, heterophoria, HYPERPHORIA, HYPOPHORIA. \[From Greek kyklos a circle, ring, or wheel + -phoros carrying, from pherein to bear]
