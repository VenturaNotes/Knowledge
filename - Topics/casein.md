## Source [^1]
- $n$. a milk protein. Casein is precipitated out of milk in acid conditions or by the action of rennin: it is the principal protein of cheese. Casein is very easily prepared and is useful as a protein supplement, particularly in the treatment of malnutrition.
## Source[^2]
- One of a group of phosphate-containing proteins (phosphoproteins) found in milk; it is also the principal protein of cheese (see CURD). Caseins are easily digested by the enzymes of young mammals and represent a major source of phosphorus. See RENNIN.
