## Source [^1]
- A type of input to a computer where the meaning depends on the time-related positions input from the device. For example, using a dataglove, the user might beckon with a finger to indicate a zoom in on the display
## Source[^2]
- $n$. 
- (1) A bodily movement, usually of the hands or the head, that has a communicative function in kinesics. See also amimia, apraxia, asemia, copropraxia, echopraxia, paramimia, Rett's disorder. 
- (2) Something that is said or done as a formality or without conviction. $v b$. 
- (3) To make a gesture (1). \[From Latin gestura bearing, from gestus carried, from gerere to carry or behave]
