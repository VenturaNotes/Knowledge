## Source [^1]
- $n$. A technique of do-it-yourself counseling or psychotherapy in which people work in pairs, taking turns at playing the role of counselor and client. It was developed in the 1950s by the US counselor (Carl) Harvey Jackins (1916-99). US co-counseling. Also called re-evaluation counsel(l)ing. Compare CO-FACILITATOR.
