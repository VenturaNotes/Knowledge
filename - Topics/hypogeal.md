## Source [^1]
- (1) Describing seed germination in which the seed leaves (cotyledons) remain below ground. Examples of hypogeal germination are seen in oak and runner bean. Compare EPIGEAL. 
- (2) Describing fruiting bodies that develop underground, such as truffles and peanuts.
