## Source [^1]
- $n$. In DSM-5, a sexual dysfunction in men characterized by marked delay in ejaculating or marked infrequency or absence of ejaculation following the excitement phase of the male sexual response cycle, causing clinically significant distress for at least six months. Occasional delayed ejaculation is common: according to DSM-5, 25\% of men report not always ejaculating during sex. Also called orgasmic impotence, ejaculatory impotence, or impotence . See also DE-REFLECTION, IMPOTENCE (2). Compare ERECTILE DISORDER.
