## Source [^1]
- US consumer protection legislation, dating from 1969, that requires lenders to state how they calculated interest on loans and other charges and to express them as an annual percentage rate. The Act also allows the borrower a 'cooling off' period, authorizing withdrawal from a financial agreement on a mortgage, within three days of signing a consumer credit agreement.
