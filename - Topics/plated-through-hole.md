## Source [^1]
- (PTH) A printed circuit board (PCB) technique where holes in the board for insertion of the components make metallic contact to both sides (and all layers in a multilayer PCB), so that the component is electrically connected to all the appropriate interconnection layers. Early double-sided PCBs without this technology could only make contact to the components on one side of the board, and connections to the other side had to make explicitly using via (through) pins.
