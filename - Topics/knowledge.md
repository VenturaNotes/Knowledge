## Source [^1]
- Information that can be expressed as a set of facts and is known to an agent or program. Knowledge can be distinguished from information or data by its embodiment in an agent; for example, an agent might receive information that increases its knowledge.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. Anything that is known. The three major classes of knowledge are declarative knowledge (knowing that), procedural knowledge (knowing how), and acquaintanceship knowledge (knowing people, places, and things). See also information (1). Compare belief, opinion. \[From Latin noscere to come to know]
