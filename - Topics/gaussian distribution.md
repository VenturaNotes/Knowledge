## Source [^1]
- (normal distribution) A continuous distribution having a density given by$$f(x) = (2\pi\sigma^2)^{-1/2} \exp\left(-1/2 \frac{(x - \mu)^2}{\sigma^2}\right)$$where $\mu$ is the mean and $\sigma$ is the standard deviation of the distribution. The distribution is symmetric about the mean. A random variable having this distribution, i.e. having this set of possible values, is said to have a Gaussian or normal distribution.
