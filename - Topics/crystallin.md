## Source [^1]
- Any of the principal structural proteins of the lens of the vertebrate eye. They are responsible for maintaining the transparency and structural organization essential for the refractive properties of the lens. Minor changes in crystallin structure can lead to cataract or blindness. The term 'crystallin' is also applied to certain transparent proteins found in the cornea.
