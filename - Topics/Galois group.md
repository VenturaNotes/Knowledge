## Source [^1]
- The Galois group $\text{Gal}(K:F)$ of a field extension $F \subseteq K$ is the group $\text{Aut}_F(K)$ of field automorphisms of $K$, which pointwise fix the base field $F$; the group operation is composition. The Galois group $\text{Gal}(\mathbb{C}:\mathbb{R})$ has order 2 and is generated by complex conjugation.
- If $f$ is a polynomial over $F$, then the Galois group of $f$ is the Galois group of its splitting field. The Galois group of $f$ acts (see ACTION) on the set of roots of $f$. A polynomial $f$ over $\mathbb{Q}$ is solvable by radicals if and only if its Galois group is solvable. The Galois group of $x^5 - x - 1$ is the symmetric group $S_5$, which is not solvable. See GALOIS CORRESPONDENCE.
