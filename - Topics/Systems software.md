## Source[^2]
- (system software) The totality of software required to produce a system acceptable to end users. See also SYSTEMS PROGRAMMING.
## Source[^3]
- Computer programs that control the functioning of the computer itself, rather than directly meeting the user's computing needs (compare APPLICATIONS SOFTWARE). Examples are operating systems and utility programs, which perform such tasks as copying files, checking the integrity of magnetic disks, etc. These programs are usually supplied with the computer and may even be built into it (see FIRMWARE).
