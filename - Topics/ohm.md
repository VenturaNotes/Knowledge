## Source[^2]
- (symbol: $\Omega$) The SI unit of electrical resistance, reactance, and impedance. It is defined as the resistance between two points on a conductor when a constant current of one ampere flows as a result of an external potential difference of one volt applied between the points. This unit replaced the international ohm ($\Omega_{\text{int}}$)as the standard of resistance: one $\Omega_{\text{int}}$ equals 1.000 49 $\Omega$.
