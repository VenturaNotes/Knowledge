## Source [^1]
- An individual who is trained in basic biological skills to enable them to support professional biologists, e.g. in a wildlife conservation programme. Training of indigenous people as parabiologists is increasingly seen as a key ingredient for the long-term success of conservation efforts. Skills might include wildlife monitoring, identification, sampling, and recording techniques, as well as knowledge of the principles of conservation.
