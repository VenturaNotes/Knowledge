## Source[^2]
- (Alphameric) Alphanumeric, sometimes referred to as alphameric, is a term used to describe all of the letters and numerals that exist within a specific language. For those who use the English language, alphameric characters are composed of both the 26 letters of the alphabet (A thru Z) and the 10 arabic numerals (0 to 9). Typically, operating systems only allow computer users to name files using alphanumeric characters. Most file naming conventions are usually limited to 26 alphabetic characters and 10 numerals.
