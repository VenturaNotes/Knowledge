## Source [^1]
- n. 
- (1) The medium or route through which a message is communicated to its recipients, the most common channels being face-to-face communication, telephone, films, internet, television, radio, and the printed media, including newspapers, books, and magazines. 
- (2) Another name for sensory modality.
## Source[^2]
- (1) The physical medium over which a signal is sent. This may be free space in the case of radio, cables using a metal conductor, or optical fibre connections for light transmission. 
- (2) A subdivision, based on frequency, of the available spectrum on a physical medium; division into frequency-based channels enables the simultaneous transmission of different messages or data streams.
