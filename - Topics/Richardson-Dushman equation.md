## Source [^1]
- The fundamental equation describing thermionic emission of electrons from the surface of a metal as a function of the temperature of the metal:$$J = A T ^ {2} \exp \left(- \frac {\Phi}{k T}\right)$$where $J$ is the emitted electron current, $A$ is the Richardson constant (a function of the surface condition of the material), $\Phi$ is the work function of the metal (the height of the potential barrier to electron emission), $k$ is the Boltzmann constant, and $T$ the thermodynamic temperature.
