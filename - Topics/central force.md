## Source [^1]
- A force acting on a particle, directed towards or away from a fixed point. $O$, the centre. A conservative central force $\mathbf{F}$ on a particle $P$ is given by $\mathbf{F}=f(r) \mathbf{r}$, where $\mathbf{r}$ is the position vector of $P$ and $r=|\mathbf{r}|$.
- Examples are the gravitational force $\mathbf{F}=-(G M m / r^{3}) \mathbf{r}$, and the force $\mathbf{F}=-(k(r-1) / r) \mathbf{r}$ due to an spring of unit natural length (see Hooke's Law).
