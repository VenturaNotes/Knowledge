## Source [^1]
- $n$. In psychoanalysis, the three personality traits of orderliness, parsimony (or meanness), and obstinacy, first identified by Sigmund Freud (1856-1939) as the distinctive adult traits associated with imperfect negotiation of the anal stage of development ('Character and Anal Erotism', 1908, StandardEdition, IX, pp. 169-75). The traits are sometimes called the three Ps: pedantry, parsimony, and petulance. See also anal CHARACTER, FIXATION (2), OBSESSIVE-COMPULSIVE PERSONALITY DISORDER.
