## Source [^1]
- (portacaval shunt) 
- (1) a surgical technique in which the hepatic portal vein is joined to the inferior vena cava. Blood draining from the abdominal viscera is thus diverted past the liver. It is no longer widely used in the treatment of portal hypertension. 
- (2) any of the natural communications between the branches of the hepatic portal vein in the liver and the inferior vena cava.
