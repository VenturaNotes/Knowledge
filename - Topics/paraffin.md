## Source [^1]
- $n$. one of a series of hydrocarbons derived from petroleum. Paraffin wax (hard paraffin), a whitish mixture of solid hydrocarbons melting at $45-60^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$, is used in medicine mainly as a base for ointments and in microscopy for embedding specimens. Liquid paraffin is a mineral oil formerly used as a laxative; combined with soft paraffin (white or yellow), it is used as an emollient, as a lubricating ointment for treating dry eyes, and to impregnate gauze dressings to prevent the fibres from sticking.
