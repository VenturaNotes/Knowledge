## Source [^1]
- Making a communication signal look more random, often by multiplying the signal by a random waveform, so that the original signal can be readily recovered at the receiver. Scrambling is used for preventing eavesdropping or for allowing signal-processing circuits to work more efficiently (some equalizers and synchronization techniques rely on the incoming signal appearing to be random).
