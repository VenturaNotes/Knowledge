## Source [^1]
- $n$. Imaginative thinking, characterized by the generation of multiple possible solutions to a problem, often associated with creativity. The concept was introduced in 1946 by the US psychologist J(oy) P(aul) Guilford (1897-1987) and is one of the six different types of mental operations in Guilford's cube. See CONVERGENCE-DIVERGENCE.
