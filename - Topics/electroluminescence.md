## Source [^1]
- (Destriau effect) The emission of light by certain phosphorescent substances under the influence of an applied electric field. For example, if a slab of dielectric material within which phosphorescent power is dispersed is sandwiched between transparent plate electrodes, light is produced when voltages of 400 to 500 volts are applied across the plates.
