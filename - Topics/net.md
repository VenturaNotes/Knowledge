## Source [^1]
- Denoting an amount remaining after specific deductions have been made. For example, net profit before taxation is the profit made by an organization after the deduction of all business expenditure but before the deduction of the taxation charge.
## Source[^2]
- The quantity obtained after a component has been subtracted from a broader concept. The word appears in economics in a variety of contexts. Net investment is total, or gross, investment less an estimate of capital consumption. Net national product is equal to gross national product, the total of all value added in the economy, less capital consumption. Net exports are total exports less total imports. The net assets of a firm are its total, or gross, assets less its liabilities. Net price is price after subtracting any discounts, for prompt payment or otherwise. Net weight is the weight of a product excluding packaging.
