## Source [^1]
- (yttrium-iron-garnet) A ferrite that is widely used for microwave applications. The magnetic properties are altered by the amount of trace elements in the material. The most common trace elements are calcium, vanadium, and bismuth. Calcium-germanium YIGs may be easily grown on a substrate. This material is used as a thin magnetic film on a nonmagnetic substrate, such as $G^3$, for the production of solid-state magnetic circuits.
