## Source [^1]
- $n$. black tarry faeces discolored by the presence of digested blood. Melaena usually reflects significant bleeding in the upper gastrointestinal tract, but may be due to disease in the small bowel or proximal large bowel (such as carcinoma or angiodysplasia). It may be associated with vomiting blood (haematemesis) or coffee-ground vomit. See also HAEMORRHAGIC DISEASE OF THE NEWBORN.
