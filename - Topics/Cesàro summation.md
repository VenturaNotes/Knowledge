## Source [^1]
- A means of summing series which generalizes the usual definition of convergence. Given a series $\sum_1^\infty a_k$ then define $s_n = \sum_1^n a_k$. The Cesàro sum of the series is $S$ if $S = \lim \limits_{n \to \infty} \frac 1n \sum_1^n s_k$. If a series is convergent to $S$, then its Cesàro sum is also $S$, but some divergent series have Cesàro sums; for example, $1-1+1-1+\ldots$ has a Cesàro sum of $\frac 12$. The Abel sum generalizes the Cesàro sum still further.
