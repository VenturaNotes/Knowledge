## Source [^1]
- $n$. In psychoanalysis, the combination of processes that determine which particular neurosis a patient develops. The notion that a patient in some sense chooses a neurosis was first mentioned by Sigmund Freud (1856-1939) in a letter to his friend, the German physician Wilhelm Fliess (1858-1928) in 1896 (Standard Edition, I, pp. 220-1) and was developed fully in 1913 in an article on 'The Disposition to Obsessional Neurosis' (Standard Edition, XII, pp. 317-26). See also COMPromise FORMATION, RETURN OF THE REPRESSED, SYMPTOM FORMATION.
