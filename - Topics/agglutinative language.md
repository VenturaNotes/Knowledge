## Source [^1]
- $n$. A language such as Finnish, Japanese, Turkish, or Swahili in which grammatical relationships are indicated by building up words out of long sequences of units, each of which indicates a particular grammatical meaning. Also called an agglutinating language . Compare INFLECTING LANGUAGE, ISOLATING LANGUAGE. \[From Latin agglutinare to glue together, from ad to + gluten glue]
