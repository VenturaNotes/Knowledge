## Source [^1]
- A policy an incumbent firm can use to discourage entry by a new firm into its market. Limit pricing involves charging a low enough price$\textemdash$the limit price$\textemdash$for entry to appear unprofitable to other firms. Limit pricing is not a credible threat: charging the limit price is not the optimal strategy after entry has occurred, and it is always best for the incumbent firm to accommodate the entrant.
