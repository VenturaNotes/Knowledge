## Source[^2]
- $n$. The theory of knowledge, especially the enquiry into what is to count as knowledge, the validity of knowledge, what distinguishes mere belief from knowledge, what kinds of things are knowable, and whether anything can be known for certain. See also GENETIC EPISTEMOLOGY, SOCIOLOGY OF KNOWLEDGE. \[From Greek episteme knowledge + logos word, discourse, or reason]
