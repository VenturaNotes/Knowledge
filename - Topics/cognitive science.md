## Source[^2]
- $n$. An umbrella term for an interdisciplinary enterprise, embracing cognitive psychology, psychobiology, anthropology, computer science, artificial intelligence, linguistics, and philosophy, concerned with information acquisition and processing. It includes research into language, learning, perception, thinking and problem solving, and knowledge representation. The term was introduced in 1973 by the English theoretical chemist and computer scientist H (ugh) Christopher Longuet-Higgins (1923-2004) in a review of artificial intelligence included in a document entitled Artificial Intelligence: A Paper Symposium published by the Science Research Council of the UK.
