## Source [^1]
- $n$. an antidepressant drug that acts by prolonging the action of the neurotransmitter serotonin (5-hydroxytryptamine) in the brain (see SSRI). It is taken by mouth for the treatment of depression, obsessivecompulsive disorder, panic disorder, post-traumatic stress disorder, and some other anxiety disorders; side-effects may include nausea, indigestion, abdominal pain, diarrhoea, and rash. Because of its short half-life paroxetine is often associated with discontinuation syndrome upon stopping.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. An antidepressant drug with anxiolytic effects belonging to the group of selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs), used especially to treat social anxiety disorder and shyness in general. Also called Paxil or Seroxat (trademarks).
