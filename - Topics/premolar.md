## Source [^1]
- (bicuspid) $n$. either of the two teeth on each side of each jaw behind the canines and in front of the molars in the adult dentition. There are no premolars in the primary (deciduous) dentition.
## Source[^2]
- A broad ridged tooth in mammals that is situated behind the canine teeth (when present) and in front of the molars. Premolars are adapted for grinding and chewing food and are present in both the deciduous and permanent teeth.
