## Source [^1]
- (diurnal rhythm) Any 24-hour periodicity in the behavior or physiology of animals or plants. Examples are the sleep/activity cycle in many animals and the growth movements of plants. Circadian rhythms are generally controlled by biological clocks. In 2017 the US scientists Jeffrey Hall, Michael Rosbash, and Michael Young won the Nobel Prize for physiology or medicine for their work on these topics.
