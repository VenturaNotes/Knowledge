## Source [^1]
- In communications theory, a theorem giving the relationship between the capacity of a channel when the channel bandwidth and noise are known. It states that the channel capacity in bits/second is equal to$$W \log_ {2} \left(1 + \frac {P _ {\mathrm {S}}}{P _ {\mathrm {N}}}\right)$$where $W$ is the bandwidth (in hertz) and $P_{\mathrm{S}} / P_{\mathrm{N}}$ is the signal-to-noise power ratio.
