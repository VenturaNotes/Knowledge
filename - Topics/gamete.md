## Source [^1]
- A gamete is a reproductive cell of an animal or plant. 
- In animals, female gametes are called ova or eggs and male gametes are called sperm
- Ova and sperm are haploid cells, with each cell carrying only one copy of each chromosome
- During fertilization, a sperm and ovum unite to form a new [[diploid]] organism
## Source[^2]
- A sex cell; either an egg or a sperm
## Source[^3]
- $n$. a mature sex cell: the ovum of the female or the spermatozoon of the male. Gametes are haploid, containing half the normal number of chromosomes.
