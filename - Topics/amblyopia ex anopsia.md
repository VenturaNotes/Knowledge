## Source [^1]
- n. Amblyopia caused by disuse of an eye, especially monocular blindness associated with strabismus and consequent suppression of the image from one eye through binocular rivalry, the most common cause of amblyopia and of blindness in general. Also spelt amblyopia ex anoopsia. Also called suppression amblyopia. \[From Greek, literally dull vision from sightlessness]
