## Source [^1]
- Let $y=f(x)$ be the graph of a function $f$ such that $f^{\prime}$ is continuous on $[a, b]$. The length of the arc, or arc length, of the curve $y=f(x)$ between $x=a$ and $x=b$ equals$$\int_{a}^{b} \sqrt{1+\left(f^{\prime}(x)\right)^{2}} \mathrm{~d} x$$
- Parametric form
	- For the curve $x=x(t), y=y(t)(t \in[\alpha, \beta])$, the arc length equals$$\int_{\alpha}^{\beta} \sqrt{\left(\frac{\mathrm{~d} x}{\mathrm{~d} t}\right)^{2}+\left(\frac{\mathrm{d} y}{\mathrm{~d} t}\right)^{2}} \mathrm{~d} t$$
- Polar form
	- For the curve $r=r(\theta)(\alpha \leq \theta \leq \beta)$, the arc length equals$$\int_{\alpha}^{\beta} \sqrt{r^{2}+\left(\frac{\mathrm{d} r}{\mathrm{~d} \theta}\right)^{2}} \mathrm{~d} \theta$$
