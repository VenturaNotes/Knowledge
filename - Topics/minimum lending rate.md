## Source [^1]
- The immediate successor of bank rate. From 1971 to 1981 MLR was the minimum rate at which the Bank of England would lend to UK discount houses; it was used as a benchmark for other interest rates. MLR was replaced in 1981 by the base rate.
## Source[^2]
- (MLR) Between 1971 and 1981, the minimum rate at which the Bank of England would lend to the discount houses. This was a published figure; the present more informal base rate does not have the same status.
