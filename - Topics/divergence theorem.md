## Source [^1]
- For a bounded region $R$ in $\mathbb{R}^3$ with smooth boundary $\Sigma$, and for a continuously differentiable vector field $\mathbf{F}$ on $R$, $$\iiint_R \text{div}\mathbf{F} dV = \iint_\Sigma \mathbf{F} \cdot d\mathbf{S}.$$ Here $d\mathbf{S} = \mathbf{n}~ dS$, where $S$ denotes surface area and $\mathbf{n}$ is the outward-pointing unit normal. As the divergence $\text{div}\mathbf{F}$ represents the local expansion/contraction of the field $\mathbf{F}$, then the theorem states that the sum of such expansions equals the flux of $\mathbf{F}$ over the boundary $\Sigma$. See STOKES' THEOREM (GENERALIZED FORM).
