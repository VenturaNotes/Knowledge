## Source [^1]
- (pl. probosces or proboscides or proboscises) 
- (1) A muscular and very flexible elongation of the nose, such as the trunk of an elephant, which has a finger-like extremity and is capable of picking up and moving objects, taking in water, collecting food, etc. 
- (2) The elongated mouthparts of certain invertebrates, such as the two-winged flies (Diptera).
