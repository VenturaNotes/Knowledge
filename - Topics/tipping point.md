## Source [^1]
- The point at which a new idea, behavior, or product suddenly breaks out from being a minority or elite interest and becomes a mass trend. The term originated in epidemiology but was widely popularized by Malcolm Gladwell's 2000 book The Tipping Point, which analyzed why some trends achieve exponential growth while others fail to do so.
