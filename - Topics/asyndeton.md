## Source [^1]
- $n$. A form of sentence construction in which a conjunction is omitted for economy of expression or rhetorical effect. A double asyndeton occurs in the usual English translation of Julius Caesar's sentence: I came, I saw, I conquered. \[From Greek $a$- not or without + syndetos bound together, from syn together + detos bound or tied]
