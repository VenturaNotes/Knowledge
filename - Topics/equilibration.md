In the theories of the Swiss psychologist Jean Piaget (1896-1980) and his followers, a process of adaptation (3) to the environment that occurs mainly through assimilation (2) and accommodation (2) and that tends to maintain a state of cognitive equilibrium or balance when an individual acquires new information.
