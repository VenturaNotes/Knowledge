## Source [^1]
- $n$. an involuntary contraction of the muscles producing contortion of the body and limbs. Rhythmic convulsions of the limbs are a feature of major epilepsy. Febrile convulsions are provoked by fever in otherwise healthy infants and young children.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. An episode, which may be recurrent and episodic, of violent involuntary contractions of a group of muscles of the body, as in a convulsive disorder, or acute, as in a convulsion following a head injury and concussion. See CLONIC CONVULSION, SEIZURE, TONIC-CLONIC, TONIC CONVULSION. See also ANTICONVULSANT, AUDIOGENIC SEIZURE, DRAVET SYNDROME, PSEUDOSEIZURE. \[From Latin convulsio cramp, from convolvere to roll together, from con- together + volvere to roll + -ion indicating an action, process, or state]
