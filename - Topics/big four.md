## Source [^1]
- A term that was used to describe the four largest UK 'high street' banks, namely Barclays, Lloyds, HSBC (formerly the Midland), and NatWest. The term is now more commonly applied to the four largest accounting firms: PricewaterhouseCoopers, Deloitte, Ernst and Young, and KPMG.
## Source[^2]
- (1) The major high-street or commercial banks in the UK: Barclays (incorporating the Woolwich), Lloyds, HSBC (formerly Midland), and the Royal Bank of Scotland Group (incorporating NatWest). In terms of market capitalization the four were joined in the 1990s by Abbey National (now part of Santander) and the Halifax (now HBOS and a wholly owned subsidiary of Lloyds), which changed their status from building societies to banks. 
- (2) The four largest firms of accountants in the world, i.e. Deloitte, Ernst and Young, KPMG, and PricewaterhouseCoopers. 
- (3) In Japan, the four largest securities houses: Daiwa, Nikko, Nomura, and Yamaichi.
