## Source [^1]
- $n$. (pl. foramina) an opening or hole, particularly in a bone. The apical foramen is the small opening at the apex of a tooth. The foramen magnum is a large hole in the occipital bone through which the spinal cord passes. The foramen ovale is the opening between the two atria of the fetal heart, which allows blood to flow from the right to the left side of the heart by displacing a membranous valve.
## Source[^2]
- (pl. foramina) An aperture in an animal part or organ, especially one in a bone or cartilage. For example, the foramen magnum is the opening at the base of the skull through which the spinal cord passes.
