## Source [^1]
- The replacement of one legal agreement by a new obligation, with the agreement of all the parties. For example, on financial exchanges using a clearing house, transactions between members are novated so that matching contracts are created between the buyer and the clearing house and between the clearing house and the seller (see COUNTERPARTY RISK).
