## Source [^1]
- $n$. pathological repetition of the words spoken by another person. It may be a symptom of language disorders, autism, severe mental illness (e.g. catatonic schizophrenia), dementia, or Tourette's syndrome.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. A pathological parrot-like repetition of overheard words or speech fragments, often with a mocking intonation, symptomatic of some mental disorders, including catatonic schizophrenia, latah, pibloktoq, and some tic disorders. Compare echopraxia, palilalia. \[From Greek echo a sound + lalia speech]
