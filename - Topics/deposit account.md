## Source [^1]
- A deposit with a bank in which the deposit is held for a fixed term or which requires notice for withdrawal, and where interest is paid. This is UK terminology; the corresponding US term is time deposit. This is contrasted with a current account (UK; in US, checking account) which is repayable on demand and bears relatively low or no interest.
## Source[^2]
- (DA) An account with a bank or building society from which money cannot be withdrawn without notice and on which interest is paid. See also SAVINGS ACCOUNT.
