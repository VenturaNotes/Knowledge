## Source [^1]
- n. a basic compound important in the synthesis of phosphatidylcholine (lecithin) and other phospholipids and of acetylcholine. It is also involved in the transport of fat in the body. Choline is sometimes classed as a vitamin but, although it is essential for life, it can be synthesized in the body.
## Source[^2]
- An amino alcohol, $\mathrm{CH}_{2} \mathrm{OHCH}_{2} \mathrm{~N}\left(\mathrm{CH}_{3}\right)_{3} \mathrm{OH}$. It occurs widely in living organisms as a constituent of certain types of phospholipids$\textemdash$the lecithins and sphingomyelins$\textemdash$and in the neurotransmitter acetylcholine. It is sometimes classified as a member of the vitamin B complex.
