## Source [^1]
- A four-arm bridge that is used for the measurement either of capacitance or frequency. A typical network is shown in the diagram. At balance, when a null response is obtained from the indicating instrument,$$\frac {C _ {\mathrm {x}}}{C _ {\mathrm {s}}} = \left(\frac {R _ {\mathrm {b}}}{R _ {\mathrm {a}}}\right) - \left(\frac {R _ {\mathrm {s}}}{R _ {\mathrm {x}}}\right)$$$$C _ {\mathrm {s}} C _ {\mathrm {x}} = \frac {1}{\omega^ {2} R _ {\mathrm {s}} R _ {\mathrm {x}}}$$where $\omega$ is the angular frequency. For the measurement of a frequency $f$, it is convenient to make $C_{\mathrm{s}} = C_{\mathrm{x}}$, $R_{\mathrm{s}} = R_{\mathrm{x}}$, and $R_{\mathrm{b}} = 2R_{\mathrm{a}}$ so that$$f = (2 \pi C R) ^ {- 1}$$
