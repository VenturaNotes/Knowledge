## Source [^1]
- An individual is risk-loving if they prefer a risky prospect with an expected pay-off of $M$ to a certain pay-off of $M$. This will be true whenever the marginal utility of wealth is increasing, so the utility function is strictly convex. A risk-loving individual will be willing to pay a fee to enter an actuarially fair gamble.
