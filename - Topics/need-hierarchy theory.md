## Source [^1]
- $n$. The proposition put forward by the US psychologist Abraham Maslow (1908-70) in his book Motivation and Personality $(1954,1970)$ that human needs fall into five categories that form the following hierarchy. The strongest and most imperative are the physiological needs for food, water, oxygen, and sex; if the physiological needs are gratified, the needs for safety emerge, and the person begins to seek out safe environments; next to emerge are needs for love and belonging, and the person yearns for a friend, a lover, a place in a group; then needs for esteem come to the fore, and the person seeks self-respect, respect from others, status, and achievement; then last, and usually only if all the others have been reasonably well satisfied, there emerges a need for self-actualization.
