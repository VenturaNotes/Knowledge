## Source [^1]
- $n$. An enzyme that hydrolyses acetylcholine to acetic acid and choline. By destroying acetylcholine after its release from the terminal boutons of presynaptic neurons and from postsynaptic receptors after a nerve impulse has been triggered, it stops further postsynaptic nerve impulses. Also called cholinesterase . See also NERVE GAS, PHYSOSTIGMINE. AChE abbrev. \[From acetylcholine + ester + -ase denoting an enzyme, from Greek diastasis separation]
