## Source [^1]
- A commodity produced to uniform specifications, so that different units are interchangeable. Standardized goods make possible lower costs of production through economies of scale, but place some restriction on consumer choice. Only standardized commodities are suitable for being traded on forward and futures markets, for example, wheat or crude oil, often with specified grade.
