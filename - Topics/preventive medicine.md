## Source [^1]
- the branch of medicine whose main aim is the prevention of disease. This is a wide field, in which workers tackle problems ranging from the immunization of persons against infectious diseases, such as diphtheria or whooping cough, to finding methods of eliminating vectors, such as malaria-carrying mosquitoes. See PRIMARY PREVENTION; SECONDARY PREVENTION; TERTIARY PREVENTION. See also World Health Organization.
