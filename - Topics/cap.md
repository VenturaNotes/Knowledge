## Source [^1]
- The operation $\cap$ (See intersection) is read as 'cap'. Compare CUP.
## Source[^2]
- A ceiling on a charge; for example, an interest-rate cap would set a maximum interest rate to be charged on a loan, regardless of prevailing general interest-rate levels. A lender would charge a fee for including a cap at the outset to offset this risk. Caps may also limit annual increases to a certain level. Compare FLOOR. See also COLLAR.
---
- CAP: Abbreviation for Common Agricultural Policy.
## Source[^3]
- n. 
- (1) a covering or a cover-like part. The duodenal cap is the superior part of the duodenum as seen on X-ray after a barium swallow and meal. 
- (2) (in contraception) see DIAPHRAGM.
