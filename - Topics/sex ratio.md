## Source [^1]
- the proportion of males to females in a population, usually expressed as the number of males per 100 females. The primary sex ratio, at the time of fertilization, is in theory $50 \%$ male. The secondary sex ratio, found at birth, usually indicates slightly fewer girls than boys.
## Source[^2]
- The ratio of the number of females to the number of males in a population. Because the mortality rates in the two sexes may be different, the sex ratios in different age classes may differ.
