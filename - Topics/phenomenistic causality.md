An important aspect of phenomenistic thought, according to the Swiss psychologist Jean Piaget (1896-1980), in which events or processes that co-occur are interpreted as being causally linked, as when a child believes that trains go fast because they are big. \[From Greek phainomenon a thing shown, from phainein to show + istikos like]
