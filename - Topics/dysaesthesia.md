## Source [^1]
- $n$. the abnormal and sometimes unpleasant sensation felt by a patient with partial damage to sensory nerve fibres when the skin is stimulated. Compare PARAESTHESIA.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. A loss of feeling, usually experienced as numbness or paraesthesia, often resulting from spinal injury and experienced below the level of the lesion. US dysesthesia. \[From Greek dys- bad or abnormal + aisthesis sensation + -ia indicating a condition or quality]
