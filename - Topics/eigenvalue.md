## Source [^1]
- $n$. In statistics, a quantity often used in multivariate statistics to indicate the relative contribution of a factor (2) or independent variable to the variance in a dependent variable. \[From German eigen own + English value]
## Source[^2]
- Given an $N \times N$ matrix $A$, an eigenvalue of $A$ is a number $\lambda$ with the property $Ax = \lambda x$, where$$x = (x_1, x_2, \dots, x_N)^{\mathrm{T}}$$and is called an eigenvector of $A$. Eigenvalues are the possible values for a parameter of an equation for which the solutions will be compatible with the boundary conditions.
