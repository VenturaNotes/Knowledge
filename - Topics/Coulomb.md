## Source[^2]
- (Symbol C). The SI unit of electric charge. It is equal to the charge transferred by a current of one ampere in one second. The unit is named after Charles de Coulomb.
## Source[^3]
- coulomb (symbol: C) The SI unit of electric charge, defined as the charge transported through any cross section of a conductor in one second by a constant current of one ampere. Charge, $Q$, can then be given as$$Q = \int I \, d t$$where $I$ is the current.
