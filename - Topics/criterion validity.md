## Source [^1]
- $n$. In psychometrics, any measure of validity based on determining the strength of the relationship between scores on the test and an independent criterion that is accepted as a standard against which the test may be judged. For example, in validating a test to measure depression, the criterion might be diagnosis of depression by clinical psychologists or psychiatrists, or in validating a test to measure scholastic aptitude, the criterion might be scores on tests of school performance. See also CONCURRENT VALIDITY, CRITERION-REFERENCED TEST, PREDICTIVE VALIDITY.
