## Source [^1]
- $n$. A technique of behavior therapy based on operant conditioning in which therapists and family members ignore behavior that is symptomatic of the disorder being treated and actively reward behaviors that are incompatible with the disorder. In the first decade of the 21st century, contingency management began to be applied more widely in the US, especially in drug treatment programmes and company health initiatives. As a consequence, more homeless people with HIV started remembering to take their medication when they were paid small incentives for doing so, and recidivism rates of drug offenders reduced markedly.
