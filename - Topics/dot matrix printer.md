## Source [^1]
- A printer that creates each character from an array of dots that are usually formed by transferring ink by mechanical impact. It may be a serial printer, printing a character at a time, or a line printer. Once the most widely used type of printer, dot matrix printers are now mainly used where multipart stationery is required.
## Source[^2]
- A device that prints text or graphical images onto paper or plastic film. The image is created by pins striking an ink-carrying ribbon that contacts the paper or film. Each pin strike makes a coloured dot on the paper or film; the combination of the dots forms the character or image. The printer head comprises a column of pins, often 8, which produces a line of up to 8 dots. Dots are printed across the page before the paper or film is advanced ready for the next line.
