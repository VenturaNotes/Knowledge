## Source [^1]
- $n$. an artificial sweetener (E951) 200 times sweeter than sugar. Aspartame is metabolized by the body into its constituents - aspartic acid, phenylalanine, and methanol - and is therefore not suitable for people with phenylketonuria. It can be used in diabetic foods.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. The intensely sweet non-carbohydrate crystalline compound $\mathrm{C}_{14} \mathrm{H}_{18} \mathrm{~N}_{2} \mathrm{O}_{5}$ formed from the amino acids phenylalanine and aspartic acid, used as an artificial sweetener that is 200 times sweeter than sucrose (table sugar). \[From aspart(ic) (acid) + (phenyl)a(lanine) + me(thyl) (ester)]
