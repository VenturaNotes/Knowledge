## Source [^1]
- $n$. A type of genetic polymorphism characterized by a relatively permanent equilibrium with different alleles of a gene present at fixed relative frequencies in the population. Also called a Hardy$\textendash$Weinberg equilibrium . See also Hardy-Weinberg law, heterosis. Compare TRANSIENT POLYMORPHISM.
