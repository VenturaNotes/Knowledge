## Source [^1]
- $n$. A substance-related and addictive disorder in DSM-5 (the only one that is not substance-related), characterized by persistent and maladaptive gambling, causing clinically significant impairment or distress, indicated by such signs and symptoms as needing increasing amounts of money to achieve the desired level of excitement; becoming restless or irritable while refraining from gambling; attempting repeatedly and unsuccessfully to cut down or to stop gambling; frequent preoccupation with gambling; using gambling as an escape from worry or unhappiness; attempting to recoup gambling losses by further gambling; jeopardizing a significant job, personal relationship, or educational opportunity by gambling; and relying on others to relieve financial crises caused by gambling. Compare INTERNET GAMING DISORDER, MANIC EPISODE.
