## Source [^1]
- A solid, liquid, or gaseous material that can sustain an electric field and act as an insulator. A perfect dielectric is one in which no energy is lost from an electric field applied across the dielectric. An imperfect dielectric is one in which electric hysteresis losses occur. The displacement, $D$, lags behind the applied field, $E$, resulting in a typical hysteresis curve (see MAGNETIC HYSTERESIS) and in energy losses from the applied field, which appear in the form of heat.
