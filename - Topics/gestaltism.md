## Source [^1]
- (gestalt psychology) n. a school of psychology, originating in Germany, that regards mental processes as wholes (gestalt, from the German meaning 'form' or 'body') that cannot be broken down into constituent parts. From this was developed gestalt therapy, which aims at achieving a suitable gestalt within the patient that includes all facets of functioning.
