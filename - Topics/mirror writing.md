## Source [^1]
- $n$. Handwriting that runs backwards and looks normal when viewed in a mirror. It is sometimes deliberate, a famous exponent being the Italian artist and polymath Leonardo da Vinci (1452-1519), but unintentional mirror writing is more common in young children and braindamaged adults, and as a frequent transient symptom following lefthemisphere stroke, about a quarter of patients experiencing it. Contrary to popular belief, it is not more common in left-handed than right-handed people and is not related to intelligence in neurologically undamaged people. Also called palingraphia.
