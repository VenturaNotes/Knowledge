## Source [^1]
- $n$. In the topological psychology of the German-US psychologist Kurt Lewin (1890-1947), a special form of topological geometry in which paths and vectors are defined psychologically, the distance between one hodological region and another being not the shortest path but the path of least effort given the attractive and repulsive valences of the regions making up the space. See also principle of least effort. \[From Greek hodos a way + logos word, discourse, or reason + Latin -al from -alis of or relating to]
