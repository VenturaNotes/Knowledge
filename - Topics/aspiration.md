## Source [^1]
- $n$. the withdrawal of fluid from the body by means of suction using an instrument called an aspirator. There are various types of aspirator: some employ hollow needles for removing fluid from cysts, inflamed joint cavities, etc.; another kind is used to suck debris and water from the patient's mouth during dental treatment. See also VACUUM ASPIRATION.
## Source[^2]
- n. 
- (1) A hope, desire, or ambition to achieve a specified goal. Hence an aspiration level or level of aspiration is a goal or target that a person sets for future achievement. 
- (2) Audible breath that may accompany the articulation of a speech sound. For example, in most accents (1) of English, the initial consonants in the words pen, ten, and ken are followed by a brief sound like $/ \mathrm{h} /$ before the voicing of the vowel begins, but when $/ \mathrm{p}/, / \mathrm{t} /$, or $/ \mathrm{k} /$ is preceded by $/ \mathrm{s} /$, as in $s p y$, sty, and $s k y$, it is not aspirated. See also Grassmann's law (linguistics). 
- aspirate $v b$. 
- (1) To pronounce a speech sound with aspiration. n. 
- (2) A speech sound pronounced with aspiration. \[From Latin aspirare to aspire to achieve something, from ad to + spirare to breathe + -ation indicating a process or condition]
