## Source [^1]
- A way of writing numbers using two symbols only. The symbols are usually written as 0 and 1 , and called binary digits or bits. These bits are usually grouped in eights, called bytes. Numbers written in binary notation are generally much longer than their decimal equivalents. For example, the binary number 1111 is equivalent to the decimal number 15. In a binary number the columns from the right represent 'units', 'twos', 'fours', 'eights', and so on. Binary notation is used in computing because digital computers represent numbers in terms of the presence (1) or absence (0) of an electrical pulse.
## Source[^2]
- A method of numerical representation with two as the base and thus having only two digits (0 and 1). These symbols may be easily represented electronically by two voltage levels in a circuit (see LOGIC CIRCUIT) and binary notation is therefore used in computers. The radix point in a binary system is the binary point and is analogous to the decimal point in decimal notation. For example,$$101.0101_2 = 2^2 + 2^0 + 2^{-2} + 2^{-4} = 5.3125_{10}$$
