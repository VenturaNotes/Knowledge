## Source [^1]
- $n$. (in immunology) the extent to which a sample of blood serum containing antibody can be diluted before losing its ability to cause agglutination of the relevant antigen. It is used as a measure of the amount of antibody in the serum.
## Source[^2]
- (1) The number of infectious virus particles present in a suspension. 
- (2) A measure of the amount of antibody present in a sample of serum, given by the highest dilution of the sample that results in the formation of visible clumps with the appropriate antigen (see AGGLUTINATION).
