## Source [^1]
- A neuron that has one axon and several dendrons extending from its cell body in different directions. Most vertebrate motor neurons and interneurons are multipolar neurons. Compare BIPOLAR NEURON; UNIPOLAR NEURON.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. A neuron with more than one neurite, invariably a single axon and one or more dendrites, projecting from its soma (1). Also called a multipolar cell. Compare BIPOLAR CELL, UNIPOLAR NEURON.
