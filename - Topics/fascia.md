## Source [^1]
- $n$. (pl. fasciae) connective tissue forming layers of variable thickness in all regions of the body. It is divided into superficial fascia, found immediately beneath the skin, and deep fascia, which envelops organs and tissues. Deep fascia includes the sheets of fibrous tissue that enclose muscles and muscle groups and separate them into layers.
## Source[^2]
- (pl. fasciae) A sheet of fibrous connective tissue occurring beneath the skin and also enveloping glands, vessels, nerves, and forming muscle and tendon sheaths.
