## Source [^1]
- Any of a class of chemical elements, including copper and iron, that are usually solids and are good conductors of electricity and heat. Alloys, such as brass or steel, containing one or more metals are also considered as metals. The atoms in a metal or alloy crystal are held together by a covalent bond known as metallic bond: a regular lattice of positive ions held together by a cloud of free electrons that move through the lattice. See also ENERGY BANDS.
