## Source [^1]
- The networks, institutions, relationships, and social customs that determine the quality of social interactions. It has been argued that a high stock of social capital leads to many desirable outcomes including lower crime rates, better health, increased longevity, improved educational achievement, and less corrupt government. Social capital has proved a difficult concept to define precisely and has defied direct measurement.
