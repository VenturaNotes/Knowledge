## Source [^1]
- (Boeck's disease) n. a chronic disorder of unknown cause in which the lymph nodes in many parts of the body are enlarged and small fleshy nodules (see GRANULOMA) develop in the lungs, liver, and spleen. The skin, nervous system, eyes, and salivary glands are also commonly affected (see uVEOPARotitis), and the condition has features similar to tuberculosis. Recovery is complete with minimal after-effects in twothirds of all cases.
