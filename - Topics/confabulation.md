## Source [^1]
- $n$. the invention of circumstantial but fictitious detail about events supposed to have occurred in the past. Usually this is to disguise an inability to remember past events. It may be a symptom of any form of loss of memory, but typically occurs in Korsakoff's syndrome.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. A memory disorder related to amnesia but involving the generation of fabricated accounts of events, experiences, or facts, either deliberately or without conscious intent, to compensate for memory loss. Some authorities think of it as 'honest lying'. It is a common sign of Korsakoff's psychosis. \[From Latin con- with + fabula a story + -(a)tion indicating a process or condition]
