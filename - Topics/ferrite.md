## Source [^1]
- A sintered ferromagnetic material plus ceramic that combines the high magnetic permeability of the former with the high electric resistance of the latter. It was used in core store memory.
## Source[^2]
- A low-density ceramic material with composition $\mathrm{Fe}_2\mathrm{O}_3.\mathrm{XO}$ , where X is a divalent metal, such as cobalt, nickel, manganese, or zinc. These magnetic materials have very low eddy-current loss and cores made from them are used for high-frequency circuits.
