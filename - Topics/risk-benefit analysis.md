## Source [^1]
- an analytical process used to weigh up the probability of foreseeable risks and benefits of an action or policy. In medical ethics, it provides a means of reconciling the principles of beneficence and nonmaleficence where a particular intervention has dangers as well as benefits. Compare COST-BENEFIT ANALYSIS. See also CONSEQUENTIALISM.
