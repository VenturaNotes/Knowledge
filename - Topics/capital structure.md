## Source [^1]
- (financial structure) 
- (1) The balance between the assets and liabilities of a company, the nature of its assets, and the composition of its borrowings. The assets may be fixed (tangible or intangible) or current (stock, debtors, or creditors); the borrowings may be long- or short-term, fixed or floating, secured or unsecured. The term is used particularly of the debt-equity ratio of a company. See also GEARING. 
- (2) The mix of differently rated classes of debt in a structured finance instrument. See TRANCHE.
