## Source [^1]
- (1) A US word, now increasingly used in the UK, for gearing. 
- (2) The use by a company of its limited assets to guarantee substantial loans to finance its business.
## Source[^2]
- The ratio of a company's debt to its equity, that is, to the part of its total capital owned by its shareholders. High leverage means high reliance on debt financing. The higher a company's leverage, the more of its total earnings are absorbed by paying debt interest, and the more variable are the net earnings available for equity shareholders. The UK term for leverage is gearing.
