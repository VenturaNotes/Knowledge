## Source [^1]
- adj. In linguistics, of or relating to speech characteristics that are considered in terms of their function within a particular language. For example, an emic approach to intonation focuses on those features of pitch pattern that signal differences in meaning, whereas an etic approach analyses pitch movements regardless of meaning. In sociology and social psychology, of or relating to an account of a social phenomenon made from the perspective or viewpoint of a participant in the social situation, in contrast to an etic account given by an outside observer such as a researcher. \[Coined in 1967 by the US linguist Kenneth Lee Pike (1912-2000), from (phon)emic]
