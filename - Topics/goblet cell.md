## Source [^1]
- a column-shaped secretory cell found in the epithelium of the respiratory and intestinal tracts. Goblet cells secrete the principal constituents of mucus.
## Source[^2]
- A goblet-shaped cell, found in the epithelium of the intestine and respiratory system in mammals and in the epidermis of fish, that secretes mucus. Goblet cells have a wide top and constricted base and possess glycoprotein-containing vesicles.
