## Source [^1]
- $n$. The principle attributed to the Greek philosopher Heraclitus (?535-?475 BC) according to which everything eventually changes into its opposite. In analytical psychology, Carl Gustav Jung (1875-1961) described it as 'the principle which governs all cycles of natural life, from the smallest to the greatest' (Collected Works, 7, paragraph 112). \[From Greek enantios opposite + dromos course + -ia indicating a condition or quality]
