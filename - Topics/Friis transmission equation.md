## Source [^1]
- An equation giving the variation in power between a transmitter and receiver in a telecommunication system; this variation is known as path loss. In a system with a transmitter of power $P_{\mathrm{T}}$, gain $G_{\mathrm{T}}$, and a receiver of gain $G_{\mathrm{R}}$, received power $P_{\mathrm{R}}$, separated by a distance $R$ and operating at a wavelength $\lambda$, the Friis transmission equation gives the received power as$$P _ {\mathrm {R}} = P _ {\mathrm {T}} G _ {\mathrm {T}} G _ {\mathrm {R}} \left(\frac {\lambda}{4 \pi R}\right) ^ {2}$$This specifies the relation in free space; modifications have to be made if the transmission is influenced by other objects, such as the earth's surface.
