## Source [^1]
- A term used (by analogy with, e.g., genome or proteome) to mean essentially phenotype in its broadest sense, i.e., all the measurable physical and biochemical attributes of an organism. Hence phenomics is the study of the interactions of genes, environment, and lifestyle to establish how these determine the phenotype and how these change throughout the life of the organism.
