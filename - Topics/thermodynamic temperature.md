## Source [^1]
- (absolute temperature) (symbol: $T$) Temperature that is measured as a function of the energy possessed by matter and as such is a physical quantity that can be expressed in units, termed kelvin. In the thermodynamic temperature scale changes of temperature are independent of the working substance used in a thermometer. The zero of the scale is absolute zero. The triple point of water is defined as 273.16 kelvin.
