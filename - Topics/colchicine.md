## Source [^1]
- $n$. a drug obtained from the meadow saffron (Colchicum autumnale), used to relieve pain in attacks of gout and in the prevention of attacks of gout and hereditary polyserositis. Common side-effects are nausea, vomiting, diarrhoea, and stomach pains.
## Source[^2]
- An alkaloid derived from the autumn crocus, Colchicum autumnale. It inhibits spindle formation in cells during mitosis so that chromosomes cannot separate during anaphase, thus inducing multiple sets of chromosomes (see polyploID). Colchicine is used in genetics, cytology, and plant breeding research and also in cancer therapy to inhibit cell division.
