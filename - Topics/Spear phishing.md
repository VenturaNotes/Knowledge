## Source [^1]
- Targets specific individual in organization to steal their login credentials
- Before starting attack, information about person is gathered such as
	- name
	- position
	- contact details

### Example
- An attacker targeted an employee of [[NTL World]] which is part of the [[Virgin Media]] company
	- Attacker claimed victim needed to sign a new employee handbook. Lured victim to clicking a link which would then ask to submit private information

## Source [^2]
- When an attacker is trying to gain access to a target's login credentials, this is known as [[credential phishing]]
- [[Tessian Defender]]
	- Five examples of spear phishing attacks are shown and are protected/prevented by the program above
	- Able to provide in-the-moment warnings to explain why an email has been flagged as suspicious
### Example 1
- Attacker encouraging target to sign an "updated employee handbook"
	- Irregularities of email
		- Sender's email address not often seen on company's network
		- Email contains unusual link matching patterns seen in previous attacks
		- Creates a sense of urgency
