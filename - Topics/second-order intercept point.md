## Source [^1]
- (IP2; second-order intercept point) The $\mathrm{IP}_2$ is used to predict mixer performance in terms of the half-IF response, which is generated by mixing of internally generated local oscillator and RF second harmonics:$$\text {half-IF rejection} = \frac {1}{2} (IP _ {2} - S - CR) (\mathrm {dB})$$where $S$ is the sensitivity of the radio receiver, and $CR$ is the cochannel rejection or capture ratio. Only the mixer $IP_{2}$ needs to be considered, as it is this stage that generates the half-IF response. See also INTERMODULATION.
