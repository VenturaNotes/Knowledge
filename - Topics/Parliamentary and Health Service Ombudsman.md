## Source [^1]
- (in England) an official responsible to Parliament and appointed to protect the interests of patients in relation to administration of and provision of health care by the National Health Service. He or she can investigate complaints about the NHS when they cannot be resolved locally. In Scotland, and in Wales, this role is undertaken by a Public Services Ombudsman.
