## Source [^1]
- $n$. The effect on an individual's task performance of the presence of other individuals engaged in the same activity. Depending on the nature of the task, the effect on performance may be positive or negative. See also drive theory of social facilitation, social facilitation. Compare AUDIENCE EFFECT, SOCIAL LOAFING. \[From Latin co- together + agere, actum to do]
