## Source [^1]
- $n$. an extract from the poppy Papaver somniferum, which contains a number of alkaloids, principally morphine (which accounts for its analgesic, euphoric, and addictive effects: see OPIATE) with smaller amounts of codeine, papaverine, and others.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. A narcotic drug extracted as a bitter-tasting, yellow-brown milky juice from the unripe seed pods of the opium poppy Papaver somniferum, containing alkaloids such as morphine and codeine, used medicinally to induce analgesia, also smoked as an intoxicant with euphoric effects, often leading to dependence (2). See also Dover's POWDER, LAUDANUM, OPIATES, OPIOID-RELATED DISORDERS, PAREGORIC, SUBSTANCE DEPENDENCE. \[From Latin opium poppy juice, from Greek opion diminutive of opos vegetable juice or sap]
