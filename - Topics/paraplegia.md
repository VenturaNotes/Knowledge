## Source [^1]
- $n$. paralysis of both legs, usually due to disease or injury of the spinal cord. It is often accompanied by loss of sensation below the level of the injury and disturbed bladder function. $\textemdash$paraplegic adj., $n$.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. Paralysis of the lower limbs, usually as a result of lesions in the middle or lower part of the spinal cord. Compare DIPLEGIA, HEMIPLEGIA, MONOPLEGIA, OPHTHALMOPLEGIA, QUADRIPLEGIA, TRIPLEGIA. paraplegic adj. \[From Greek para beside or beyond + plege a blow + -ia indicating a condition or quality]
