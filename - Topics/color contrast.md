## Source [^1]
- $n$. The change in perceived color resulting from its juxtaposition with a different color, as occurs when complementary colors such as blue and yellow are juxtaposed, making the blue look bluer and the yellow yellower, or when red and cyan (blue-green) are juxtaposed, making the red look redder and the cyan more intensely cyan, simultaneous contrast occurring when a color is viewed alongside another and tends to shift towards the complementary color of the other color, and successive contrast occurring when one color and then another is viewed, the second tending to shift towards the complementary color of the first. US color contrast. See also appurtenance, contrast (2), VISUAL INDUCTION. Compare COLOR ASSIMILATION.
