## Source [^1]
- A frieze pattern is a planar pattern that is repeating in one direction. As such they are common in decorations and architecture. Formally a frieze pattern is such that its group of symmetries—called a frieze group—has a subgroup of translations that is isomorphic to the integers.
- ![[Pasted image 20260627184022.png]]
	- An example of a frieze pattern
- The frieze group of the pattern in the figure is generated by $x$, a translation two tiles to the right, and $y$, a half-turn, and is isomorphic to$$\langle x, y \mid y^2 = e, yx = x^{-1}y \rangle,$$and its subgroup of translations is $\langle x \rangle$. It can be shown that, up to isomorphism, there are seven frieze groups. Compare CRYSTALLOGRAPHIC GROUPS.
