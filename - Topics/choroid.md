## Source [^1]
- $n$. the layer of the eyeball between the retina and the sclera (see also BRUCH's MEMBRANE). It contains blood vessels and a pigment that absorbs excess light and so prevents blurring of vision. See EYE. $\textemdash$choroidal adj.
## Source[^2]
- A pigmented layer, rich in blood vessels, that lies between the retina and the sclerotic of the vertebrate eye. At the front of the eye the choroid is modified to form the ciliary body and the iris.
## Source[^3]
- $n$. The vascular membrane between the outer sclera and the retina of the eye. \[From Greek chorion the membrane surrounding an embryo or foetus]
