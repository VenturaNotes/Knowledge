## Source [^1]
- (FDNR) A one port device realized using a GIC (general impedance converter) whose impedance $Z_{\mathrm{in}}$ is given by $1 / (s^2 D)$, where $s = \mathrm{j}\omega$ and $D$ is a constant. A circuit realization is shown in the diagram, where $D = C^{2}R_{2}R_{4} / R_{5}$. If, for example, $C = 1$ farad and $R_{2} = R_{5} = 1$ ohm and $R_{4} = R$, then $Z_{\mathrm{in}}$ becomes equal to $-1 / (\omega^{2}R)$, i.e. a frequency-dependent negative resistor. This device is used extensively to design active filters directly from passive LC prototype filters.
