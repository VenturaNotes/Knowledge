## Source [^1]
- A load impedance diagram plotted on polar coordinates: the radial coordinate represents the reflection coefficient measured in the transmission line that connects the active device to the load; the angular coordinate represents the angular distance of the minimum standing-wave voltage from a designated reference plane on the output terminal. Lines of constant power output, frequency, voltage, and efficiency may be drawn on the diagram.
