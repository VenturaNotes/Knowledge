## Source [^1]
- $n$. a drug administered by mouth to treat depression (see ANTIDEPRESSANT) and, in children, bedwetting. Common side-effects include dry mouth, blurred vision, constipation, sweating, and rapid heartbeat.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. A commonly prescribed tricyclic antidepressant drug that acts by blocking the reuptake mechanism of amine neurotransmitters, allowing them to accumulate at synapses (1) in the central nervous system. Also called Amitril, Elavil, Endep, Laroxyl, Saroten, Tofranil, and Triptafen (trademarks). Formula $\mathrm{C}_{19} \mathrm{H}_{24} \mathrm{N}_{2}$.
