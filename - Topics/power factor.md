## Source [^1]
- The ratio of the actual power in watts developed by an a.c. system, as measured by a wattmeter, to the apparent power indicated by voltmeter and ammeter readings and usually measured in volt-amperes (VA) rather than watts. If the voltage and current are sinusoidal the power factor, $P / VI$, is equal to the cosine of the phase angle between them. The power factor is also equal to the ratio of the resistance, $R$, to the impedance, $Z$, and thus indicates the dissipation in an insulator, inductor, or capacitor.
