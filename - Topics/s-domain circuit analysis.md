## Source [^1]
- A mathematical technique that simplifies the study of the transient behavior of circuits by using the Laplace transform, which results in a transformation into the s-domain. This technique avoids the formulation of differential equations in the time domain in favor of much simpler algebraic manipulations in the s-domain. The function $F(s)$ given by the Laplace transform can be expressed as the ratio of two factored polynomials given by:$$F(s) = \frac{K \left[ (s + a_1)(s + a_2) \dots (s + a_n) \right]}{\left[ (s + b_1)(s + b_2) \dots (s + b_n) \right]}$$where $s = \sigma + j\omega$, the complex frequency or complex operator.
- The roots of the denominator polynomial, $-b_1, -b_2, \ldots, -b_n$, are called the poles of $F(s)$ and at these values of $s$, $F(s)$ tends to infinity. The roots of the numerator polynomial, $-a_1, -a_2, \ldots, -a_n$, are called the zeros of $F(s)$ and at these values of $s$, $F(s)$ becomes zero. It is often convenient to visualize the poles and zeros of $F(s)$ as points on the complex plane called the s-plane.
