## Source [^1]
- Gold held in bulk, usually in the form of gold bars. Bullion is largely held by central banks, as part of their countries' foreign exchange reserves.
## Source[^2] 
- Gold, silver, or some other precious metal used in bulk, i.e. in the form of bars or ingots rather than in coin. Central banks use gold bullion in the settlement of international debts. In the London bullion market, bullion brokers act as agents for both buyers and sellers and also trade as principals.
