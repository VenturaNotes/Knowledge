## Source [^1]
- An approach in economics, predominant in mainstream economics since the 1950s and developed primarily by John Hicks (1904-1989) and Paul Samuelson (1915-2009), that combines neoclassical microeconomics, in particular, consumer theory of individual demand, and Keynesian macroeconomics.
