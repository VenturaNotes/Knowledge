## Source [^1]
- A form of market segmentation developed to improve the discriminating power of demographic classifications. It combines life-cycle, income, and socio-economic information. The underlying idea is that as people pass through the various stages of their lives they have different aspirations and patterns of behavior, which are reflected in their consumption of goods and services.
