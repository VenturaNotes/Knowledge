## Source [^1]
- The use of electrostatic fields produced between two metal electrodes for deflecting an electron beam. The electrodes used are called deflection plates and two pairs of plates, at right angles to each other, are used in electron-beam devices, such as cathode-ray tubes, to provide deflection in two orthogonal directions. Compare ELECTROMAGNETIC DEFLECTION.
