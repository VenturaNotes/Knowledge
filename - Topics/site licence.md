## Source [^1]
- The practice of granting an extended licence to a business to allow it to make and use a number of copies of a particular computer program at a particular location or site. This has been a popular way of getting a large company to standardize on particular software packages. The software house thereafter obtains its regular income from support and upgrade fees rather than individual licenses on a per workstation basis.
