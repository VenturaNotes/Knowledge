## Source [^1]
- (atypical facial pain, chronic idiopathic facial pain) a chronic pain in the face that has no known cause and does not fit the classic presentation of other cranial neuralgias. It may be stressrelated, and in some cases appears to be associated with defective metabolism of tyramine. Treatment may involve the use of antidepressants.
