## Source [^1]
- $n$. In signal detection theory, an index of the detectability or discriminability of a signal, given by the difference between the means (or separation between the peaks) of the signal-plus-noise and the noise-only probability distributions, divided by the standard deviation of the noise-only distribution. See also noise (2), RECEIVER OPERATING CHARACTERISTIC, TWO-ALTERNATIVE FORCED-CHOICE TASK. Symbol: $d^{\prime}$.
