## Source [^1]
- $n$. In DSM-5, a disruptive, impulse-control, and conduct disorder of childhood characterized by recurrent negativistic, defiant, disobedient, or hostile behavior towards authority figures, including such signs and symptoms as temper tantrums, arguing with adults, actively defying rules, deliberately annoying people, unfairly blaming others for mistakes or misbehavior, being touchy or easily annoyed, angry, resentful, spiteful, or vindictive, with the pattern of behavior leading to significant impairment in social, academic, or occupational functioning. Compare DISRUPTIVE MOOD DYSREGULATION DISORDER, INTERMITTENT EXPLOSIVE DISORDER. ODD abbrev.
