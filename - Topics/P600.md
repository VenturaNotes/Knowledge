## Source [^1]
- abbrev. An event-related potential elicited when a person encounters a grammatical anomaly, either when reading text or listening to speech. It is also called the syntactic positive shift. It was first reported in 1992 by the US psychologists Lee Osterhout (born 1959) and Philip J. Holcombe (born 1951) in an article in the journal Psychophysiological Brain Research. \[From p(ositive) 600 (milliseconds)]
