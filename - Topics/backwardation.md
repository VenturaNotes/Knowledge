## Source [^1]
- A situation in which the futures price (or forward price) of a commodity is lower than the spot price. See also CONTANGO; FORWARDS AND FUTURES.
## Source[^2]
- (1) The situation in which (or the extent to which) the spot price of a commodity, plus any carrying costs, is higher than the forward price. Compare CONTANGO. 
- (2) A situation that occasionally occurs on a stock exchange when a market maker quotes a buying price for a share that is lower than the selling price quoted by another market maker. This can only occur in a quote-driven system.
