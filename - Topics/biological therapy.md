## Source [^1]
- any treatment that facilitates the ability of the immune system to fight disease, as opposed to acting directly against the disease (compare CHEMOTHERAPY; RADIOTHERAPY). Such treatments, most commonly used for cancer and rheumatic disease, include biological response modifiers, immunotherapy, monoclonal antibodies, cytokine inhibitors and modulators, and targeted agents.
