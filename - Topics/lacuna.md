## Source [^1]
- n. (pl. lacunae) 
- (1) (in anatomy) a small cavity or depression; for example, one of the spaces in compact bone in which a bone cell lies. 
- (2) (in ethics) a crucial gap, for instance in laying out the arguments for or against a controversial approach or treatment, that may invalidate the whole approach.
## Source[^2]
- (pl. lacunae or lacunas) A gap or cavity in the tissues of an organism; for example, the hollow centre of certain plant stems or any of the small cavities in bone in which the bone-forming cells are found.
