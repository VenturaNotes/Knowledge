## Source [^1]
- $n$. a nitrogen-containing compound with a two-ring molecular structure. Examples of purines are adenine and guanine, which form the nucleotides of nucleic acids, and uric acid, which is the end-product of purine metabolism.
## Source[^2]
- An organic nitrogenous base (see formula), sparingly soluble in water, that gives rise to a group of biologically important derivatives, notably adenine and guanine, which occur in nucleotides and nucleic acids (DNA and RNA).
