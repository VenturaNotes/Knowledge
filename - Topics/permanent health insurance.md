## Source [^1]
- (PHI) A form of health insurance that provides an income (maximum $75 \%$ of salary) up to normal retirement age (or pension age) to replace an income lost by prolonged illness or disability in which the insured is unable to perform any part of his or her normal duties. Premiums are related to age and occupation and normally are fixed; benefits, which are not paid for the first $4\text{-}13$ weeks of disability, are tax free for one year and thereafter are taxed. Compare SICKNESS AND ACCIDENT INSURANCE.
