## Source [^1]
- a condition in which a person struggles to discard useless or worn-out possessions, acquires an excessive number of such items, and stores them in a chaotic manner resulting in unmanageable clutter. The items are nearly always of little or no monetary value, although valuable items are often found mixed indiscriminately with the rest. Hoarding disorder was included in DSM-5 in 2013 as a new disorder and has been considered for introduction in ICD-11.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. A mental disorder introduced in DSM-5 characterized by persistent difficulty in discarding or parting with possessions regardless of their intrinsic value, and distress when forced to discard them, causing significant impairment in social, occupational, or everyday functioning. People with this condition generally accumulate objects that clutter their living space until it can no longer be used comfortably. According to DSM-5, the prevalence in the US and Europe is between 2 per cent and 6 per cent. Compare obsessive-compulsive DISORDER. \[From Old English hord to collect or store]
