## Source [^1]
- (hemeralopia) comparatively good vision in poor light but poor vision in good illumination. The condition is usually congenital and associated with poor visual acuity and defective color vision. Acquired cases occur when the cones (light-sensitive cells) are selectively destroyed by disease. Compare NIGHT BLINDNESS.
