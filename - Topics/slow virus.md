## Source [^1]
- one of a group of infective disease agents that resemble viruses in some of their biological properties but whose physical properties (e.g. sensitivity to radiation) suggest that they may not contain nucleic acid. They are now more commonly known as prions.
## Source[^2]
- Formerly, a quasi-viral or subviral agent thought to be responsible for the diseases scrapie in sheep and bovine spongiform encephalopathy (BSE) in cattle. These diseases are now generally regarded as being caused by abnormal prion proteins.
