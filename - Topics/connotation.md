## Source [^1]
- n. 
- (1) An association or idea suggested or implied by a word or expression; for example, the word mother has connotations of home, family, maternal love, and so on. This meaning should be carefully distinguished from sense 2. See also SEMANTIC DIFFERENTIAL. Compare DENOTATION (1). 
- (2) In philosophy, the set of attributes or properties that determine the thing or things to which a word or expression refers; hence the connotation of the word mother is the following set of attributes or properties: \{female, human or animal, parent\}. See also intension. Compare denotation (2). connotative $a d j$. connote $v b$.
- \[From Latin connotare to connote, from con- with + notare to mark, from nota a mark or sign + -ation indicating a process or condition]
