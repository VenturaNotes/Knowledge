## Source [^1]
- $n$. A visual contour in the absence of a lightness or color gradient in the stimulus, computed in Area V2 of the visual cortex, generating form perception without a corresponding retinal image, the best-known example being the contour of a Kanizsa triangle. Also called an imaginary contour, a Kanizsa contour, or a subjective contour. See also anorthoscopic, cognitive invention, Ehrenstein's brightness illusion, RANDOM-DOT KINEMATOGRAM, RANDOM-DOT STEREOGRAM.
