## Source [^1]
- The process of evaluating and scrutinizing the assets, liabilities, profitability, cash flow, policies, and compliance of a company prior to a potential transaction, such as the purchase of the business. Due diligence is typically conducted by the prospective buyer in conjunction with legal, accountancy, and other relevant experts to ensure that all material facts are declared and understood. The term is sometimes used in an older legal sense to mean the care a reasonable person should take before entering into an agreement.
