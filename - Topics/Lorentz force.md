## Source [^1]
- The force experienced by an electron or other charged particle moving in a region of electric field, $\pmb{E}$, and magnetic flux density, $\pmb{B}$. The force due to the magnetic flux acts in a direction that is perpendicular both to the direction of particle motion and of the flux density and is given by the vector product $q\mathbf{v} \times \mathbf{B}$, where $\mathbf{v}$ is the particle velocity and $q$ the charge. The total force due to both fields is given by $\pmb{F} = q\pmb{E} + q\mathbf{v} \times \mathbf{B}$.
