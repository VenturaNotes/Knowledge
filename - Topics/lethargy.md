## Source [^1]
- $n$. mental and physical sluggishness: a degree of inactivity and unresponsiveness approaching or verging on the unconscious. The condition results from disease (such as sleeping sickness) or hypnosis.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. Lack of energy. See also encephalitis lethargica, hebetude, IRRITABLE MALE SYNDROME, STUPOR, WATER INTOXICATION. \[From Latin lethargia sluggishness, from Greek lethargos drowsy, from lethe oblivion]
