## Source [^1]
- $n$. The property of a word or lexeme of having several different meanings, a typical English example being the word kind, which can mean type (a different kind of food); quality (a difference of kind rather than degree); goods or services (payment in kind); something similar (respond to the attack in kind); compassionate (a kind gesture); or cordial (kind regards). Also called polysemia. See also heteronyms, lexical ambiguity. Compare MONOSEMY, POLYONYMY. polysemous adj. \[From Greek polys many + sema a sign]
