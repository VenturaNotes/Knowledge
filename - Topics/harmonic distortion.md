## Source [^1]
- The distortion of a waveform caused by the nonlinear effect of a system or component that creates harmonic frequencies that would not otherwise be present at the output. The extent to which a particular harmonic component distorts a signal is given by the ratio of its amplitude $A_{\mathrm{n}}$ to the amplitude of the fundamental component $A_{\mathrm{f}}$, expressed as a percentage:$$\% \text{age } n \text{th harmonic distortion} = \% \mathrm{Dn} = \left(A_{\mathrm{n}} A_{\mathrm{f}}\right) \times 100\%$$
- The total harmonic distortion (THD) is the square root of the sum of the squares of all the individual harmonic distortions:$$\% \mathrm{THD} = \sqrt{\left(D_2^2 + D_3^2 + \dots + D_n^2\right)} \times 100\%$$where the individual harmonic distortion $D_{\mathrm{n}}$ is specified by the ratio $A_{\mathrm{n}} / A_{\mathrm{f}}$.
