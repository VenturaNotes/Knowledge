## Source [^1]
- (CNS) the brain and the spinal cord, as opposed to the cranial and spinal nerves and the autonomic nervous system, which together form the peripheral nervous system. The CNS is responsible for the integration of all nervous activities.
## Source[^2]
- (CNS) The part of the nervous system that coordinates all neural functions. In invertebrates it may comprise simply a few nerve cords and their associated ganglia. In vertebrates it consists of the brain and the spinal cord. The vertebrate CNS contains reflex arcs, which produce automatic and rapid responses to particular stimuli.
## Source[^3]
- $n$. The part of the nervous system that in humans and other vertebrates comprises the brain and spinal cord. Compare PERIPHERAL NERVOUS SYSTEM. CNS abbrev.
