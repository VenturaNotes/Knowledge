## Source [^1]
- (1) The right that enables a bank to seize any bank-account balances of a guarantor or debtor if a loan has been defaulted upon. 
- (2) A code on the magnetic strip of a plastic card that, together with the personal identification number (PIN), verifies that the user of the card is entitled to use it. 
- (3) To cancel a futures contract or options contract by entering into a reverse agreement.
