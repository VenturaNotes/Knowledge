## Source [^1]
- Nominally another name for cartridge; in practice the term is normally reserved for the type of cassette originally introduced by Philips for audio purposes under the trademark Compact Cassette and used as backing store on some early microcomputers.
- The digital audio tape cartridge is sometimes referred to as a cassette.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. (in radiography) a thin light-proof box in which a piece of photographic film is placed. It usually contains special screens, which fluoresce on exposure to X-rays, intensifying the image formed on photographic film when a radiographic exposure is taken. In computerized radiography the cassette may contain an electrically charged plate.
