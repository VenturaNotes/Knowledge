## Source [^1]
- Integers $a$ and $b$ are coprime if their greatest common divisor is 1. Two ideals $I$ and $J$, in a commutative ring $R$, are coprime if $I + J = R$. By Bézout's lemma $a\mathbb{Z}$ and $b\mathbb{Z}$ are coprime ideals in $\mathbb{Z}$ if and only if $a$ and $b$ are coprime integers.
