## Source [^1]
- (1) (in animal behavior) A stimulus that produces a more vigorous response than the normal stimulus eliciting that particular response. For example, a female herring gull will brood a giant egg in preference to its own eggs, which are smaller. A supernormal stimulus is an exaggerated sign stimulus. 
- (2) (in neurophysiology) A stimulus that is more intense than a normal stimulus and is capable of inducing a response in a nerve fiber during the relative refractory period.
