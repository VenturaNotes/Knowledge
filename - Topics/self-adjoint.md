## Source [^1]
- A linear map $T:V \to V$ on an inner product space $V$ is self-adjoint if $T$ equals its adjoint $T^*$, that is, $\langle Tv, w\rangle = \langle v, Tw\rangle$ for all $v, w \in V$. If $V$ is finite-dimensional, then the matrix for $T$, with respect to an orthonormal basis, is symmetric (or Hermitian over $\mathbb{C}$). See spectral theorem.
