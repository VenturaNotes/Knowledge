## Source [^1]
- An analysis of business sectors that aims to characterize particular industries in terms of the stage they have reached in their natural rise and fall; the usual purpose is to clarify investment decisions. An industry life cycle is normally divided into four stages: the emergence of a new industry at a time of rapid change; its growth; its maturity, at which point the market is saturated; and finally its decline.
