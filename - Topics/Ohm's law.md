## Source [^1]
- The electric current, I, flowing in a conductor or resistor is linearly proportional to the applied potential difference, V, across it. From the definition of resistance, R, Ohm's law can be written:$$V = I R$$Any electrical component, circuit, or device that maintains such a linear relationship between current and voltage can be described as ohmic.
- The form of Ohm's law at a particular point is given by$$J = \sigma E$$which relates the current density $J$ at a point to the electric field $E$ at that point and the conductivity $\sigma$ of the material.
