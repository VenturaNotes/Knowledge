## Source [^1]
- $n$. a widely distributed cell in connective tissue that is responsible for the production of both the ground substance and of the precursors of collagen, elastic fibres, and reticular fibres.
## Source[^2]
- A cell that secretes fibres in the extracellular matrix of connective tissue. The cells are long, flat, and star-shaped and lie close to collagen fibres. Fibroblasts are often grown in cell cultures and are used to study genetic reprogramming for cell therapy or stem cell production. See also FILOPODIUM; LAMELLIPODIUM.
