## Source [^1]
- $n$. 
- (1) The color or appearance of the skin, originally believed to be indicative of a person's temperament. See complexion (2). 
- (2) In medieval physiology, the combination of qualities or humours (3) believed to determine a person's temperament or personality, as in the Prologue to Chaucer's Canterbury Tales: 'Of his complexion he was sangwyn' (line 333). See also complex (2).
- \[From Latin complexio, complexionis a combination, from com- together + plectere to plait]
