## Source [^1]
- (ISI) In a communications system, interference caused to each transmitted symbol resulting from the previous and subsequent symbols overlapping. This interference can be controlled by appropriate filtering (for example, the popular root-raised cosine transmit filter), although when the characteristics of the channel are unknown, there is often a substantial amount of ISI, which must be reduced by an equalizer at the receiver.
