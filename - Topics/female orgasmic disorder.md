## Source [^1]
- $n$. A sexual dysfunction in DSM-5 characterized by a persistent or recurrent delay or absence of orgasm following the excitement phase of the female sexual response cycle on all or almost all occasions ($75\text{-}100$ per cent), causing significant distress or interpersonal problems, persisting for at least six months, and not being attributable to a drug, another medical condition, or relationship distress, such as partner violence. Compare PERSISTENT SEXUAL AROUSAL SYNDROME.
