## Source [^1]
- Reduction in amount, degree, or intensity of an activity. In environmental economics abatement is used to describe a reduction in the emission of pollutants. Technical abatement refers to measures involving the production activities of firms, such as the introduction of new, or amendment of existing, technologies or processes. Behavioral abatement refers to consumption activities, or changes in the lifestyle of consumers. See also ABATEMENT COST.
