## Source [^1]
- $n$. (in neurophysiology) the triggering of a conducted impulse in the membrane of a muscle cell or nerve fiber. During excitation a polarized membrane becomes momentarily depolarized and an action potential is set up.
## Source[^2]
- (1) The act or process of stimulating a cell, especially a nerve cell. 
- (2) The response of a cell that has been stimulated.
## Source[^3]
- $n$. The action of a stimulus $(1,5)$ in evoking a response from an organism, or from a cell, tissue, or organ, or the disturbed or altered state resulting from such arousal. See also excitatory, excitatory postsynaptic POTENTIAL. Compare inhibition (2, 3). excite $v b$. \[From Latin ex- out + ciere to set in motion + -ation indicating a process or condition]
