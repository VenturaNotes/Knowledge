## Source [^1]
- Symbol kDa. A non-SI unit of mass used to express molecular mass, especially for large molecules, such as proteins and polysaccharides. It is equal to 1000 daltons. A dalton is equal to one twelfth of the mass of an atom of carbon-12, or $1.66033 \times 10^{-27} \mathrm{~kg}$. It is named after British chemist and physicist John Dalton (1766-1844).
