## Source [^1]
- (1) Any of various accounting ratios used to measure a company's longer-term solvency. One of the most widely used is: net assets / (long-term liabilities + short-term liabilities), expressed as a percentage; sometimes net after-tax profit is substituted for net assets. See also LIQUID RATIO. 
- (2) A ratio used by the UK regulatory authorities to evaluate the stability of insurance companies; it is the ratio of the company's net assets to its non-life premium income. 
- (3) See CAPITAL ADEQUACY RATIO.
