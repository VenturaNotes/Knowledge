## Source [^1]
- $n$. In the doctrine of associationism, a law introduced by the English philosopher John Stuart Mill (1806-73), according to which mental elements that frequently occur together tend to become associated. It was influential in the early development of behaviourism. See also GROUPING LAW. Compare CONTIGUITY LAW, SIMILARITY LAW.
