## Source [^1]
- $n$. a viscous fluid secreted by mucous membranes. Mucus acts as a protective barrier over the membranes, a lubricant, and a carrier of enzymes. It consists chiefly of glycoproteins, particularly mucin. $\textemdash$ mucous, mucoid adj.
## Source[^2]
- The slimy substance secreted by goblet cells onto the surface of a mucous membrane to protect and lubricate it and to trap bacteria, dust particles, etc. Mucus consists of water, various mucoproteins (collectively called mucin), cells, and salts. Mucus in saliva is essential for swallowing food, facilitating the smelling and tasting of food, and protecting the mouth and pharynx against abrasion.
