## Source [^1]
- A bank which is a member of the clearing-house. Most large banks in the UK are clearing-house members; banks which are not members have to use a clearing bank as their agent to get their cheques cleared.
## Source[^2]
- (1) In the UK, a bank that is a member of the central clearing system, to enable passage and clearance of cheques. It is often used as an alternative description for the major high-street banks. 
- (2) In the USA, a bank that settles federal, agency, and corporate securities for its customers.
