## Source [^1]
- $n$. absence or loss of the voice caused by disease of the larynx or mouth or by disease of the nerves and muscles involved in the generation and articulation of speech. If loss of speech is due to a language defect in the cerebral hemispheres, the disorder is aphasia.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. Loss of the voice resulting from damage to the vocal tract. If the inability to speak is of psychological origin or caused by a lesion in the central nervous system, then the condition is not properly called aphonia. \[From Greek $a$- without + phone voice + -ia indicating a condition or quality]
