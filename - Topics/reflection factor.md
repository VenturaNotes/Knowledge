## Source [^1]
- The vector ratio of the current, $I$, delivered to a load of impedance $Z_{\mathrm{B}}$ by a source of impedance $Z_{\mathrm{A}}$ when the impedances are not matched, to the current, $I_0$, delivered to the load when the impedances are matched by an impedance matching network of image transfer constant, $\theta$, equal to zero (see diagram). The reflection factor is thus the ratio of the current delivered to an unmatched load to the current that would be delivered to a perfectly matched load. It is given by$$\frac {I}{I _ {0}} = \frac {\sqrt {\left(4 Z _ {\mathrm {A}} Z _ {\mathrm {B}}\right)}}{\left(Z _ {\mathrm {A}} + Z _ {\mathrm {B}}\right)}$$The ratio of the electrical powers in $Z_{\mathrm{B}}$ for these matched and unmatched cases is also given by $I / I_0$. The reflection loss between $Z_{\mathrm{A}}$ and $Z_{\mathrm{B}}$ is the ratio of the powers in $Z_{\mathrm{B}}$ expressed in decibels, i.e. it is given by$$10 \log_ {1 0} \left(\frac {I}{I _ {0}}\right)$$If the reflection loss is negative it represents a reflection gain in the system.
