## Source [^1]
- (symbol: $G$; unit: siemens) The real part of the admittance, $Y$, which is given by$$Y = G + jB$$where $B$ is the susceptance and $j = \sqrt{-1}$. In d.c. circuits the conductance is the reciprocal of the resistance. The d.c. conductance of a sample of material of active area $A$ and length $l$, and that has a conductivity $\sigma$, is given by$$G = \frac{\sigma l}{A}$$
