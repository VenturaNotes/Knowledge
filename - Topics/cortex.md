## Source [^1]
- n. (pl. cortices) the outer part of an organ, situated immediately beneath its capsule or outer membrane; for example, the adrenal cortex (see ADRENAL GLANDS), renal cortex (see KIDNEY), or cerebral cortex. $\textemdash$cortical adj.
## Source[^2]
- (pl. cortices) 
- (1) (in botany) The tissue between the epidermis and the vascular system in plant stems and roots. It is composed of parenchyma cells and shows little or no structural differentiation. Cortex is produced by activity of the apical meristem. See also ENDODERMIS. 
- (2) (in zoology) The outermost layer of tissue of various organs, including the adrenal glands (adrenal cortex), kidneys (renal cortex), and cerebral hemispheres (cerebral cortex).
## Source[^3]
- $n$. The outer layer of any organ or part, such as the cerebrum, the cerebellum, or adrenal gland. Often used as a shorthand for the cerebral cortex. cortical adj. \[From Latin cortex bark or outer layer]
