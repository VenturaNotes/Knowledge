## Source [^1]
- $n$. visible scaling from the surface of the scalp. It is extremely common, occurring in about $50 \%$ of the population, and is associated with the presence of the yeast Malassezia furfur. It is the precursor of seborrhoeic eczema of the scalp, in which there is a degree of inflammation in addition to the greasy scaling. Dandruff can be controlled by shampoos containing tar, selenium sulphide, pyrithione zinc, or imidazole antifungals. Medical name: pityriasis capitis.
