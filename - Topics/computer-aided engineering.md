## Source [^1]
- An umbrella term that covers all uses of computers in engineering applications. Thus computer-aided design, computer-aided planning, and computer-aided manufacturing, are all branches of computer-aided engineering. The subject area is not usually taken to include software engineering.
## Source[^2]
- Computer-aided engineering (CAE) refers to multiple computer applications needed to carry out engineering tasks, such as design, testing, and simulation. CAE software typically falls into one of four categories: Computer-aided design (CAD), computational fluid dynamics (CFD), finite element analysis/method (FEA/FEM), and multibody simulation (MBS).
## Source[^3]
- (CAE) The design of software, products, or components with the assistance of computers and computer programs. Examples in electronics include the use of schematic capture to simplify the drawing of circuit diagrams and simulation theoretically to test the circuit before it is constructed. Computer-aided engineering is often used to describe the overall computerization of the design and development process of engineering and it embraces computer-aided design (CAD).
