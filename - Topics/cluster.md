## Source [^1]
- (1) A group of similar devices, such as processors, storage units, or peripheral devices, brought together to provide enhanced performance, security, or resilience to failure. 
- (2) A unit of storage, usually on a disk, that comprises a contiguous area made up from a number of basic units of storage.
- (3) See CONCEPT LEARNING.
## Source[^2]
- The term cluster refers to a group of computers that act as a single system to perform a similar function. The individual computers within a cluster are called nodes. Clusters can range from either a two-node system or hundreds of individual nodes. Computer clusters are often used by businesses to maximize computer processing speed and increase data storage capacity.
