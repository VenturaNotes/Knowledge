## Source [^1]
- (1) An increase in the nominal price of a company's shares, by combining a specified number of lower-price shares into one higher-priced share. In most cases this can be done by an ordinary resolution at a general meeting of the company. 
- (2) The inclusion of one or more subsidiary companies in a parent company's financial statements. See CONSOLIDATED FINANCIAL STATEMENTS.
