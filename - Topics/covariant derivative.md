## Source [^1]
- Let $\mathbf{v}$ be a smooth vector field on a surface $X$ in $\mathbb{R^3}$ and let $\gamma(t)$ be a parameterized curve in $X$. Then the covariant derivative $D\mathbf{v}/dt$ is the orthogonal projection of $d(\mathbf{v}(\gamma(t))/dt$ into the tangent space at $\gamma(t)$. The covariant derivative is in fact intrinsic to the surface and can be expressed in terms of Christoffel symbols. See parallel transport.
