## Source [^1]
- The condition of tissues or other structures within an organism of being perforated by window-like slits. For example, the endothelium of the capillaries in the glomerulus within the kidneys is fenestrated in order to facilitate the filtration of substances.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. 
- (1) (in dentistry) a 'window' of bone loss on the facial or lingual aspect of a tooth that places the exposed root surface directly in contact with the gum or the alveolar mucosa. 
- (2) a surgical operation in which a new opening is formed in the bony labyrinth of the inner ear as part of the treatment of deafness due to otosclerosis. It is rarely performed today, having been superseded by stapedectomy.
