## Source [^1]
- $n$. A form of agnosia involving an impaired ability to identify parts of one's own body, often indicative of a lesion in the pathway between the thalamus and the parietal lobe. Often misspelt autopagnosia. See also agnosia, finger agnosia. Compare asomatagnosia (see agnosia), topagnosia. \[From Greek autos self + topos place + $a$- without + gnosis knowing + -ia indicating a condition or quality]
