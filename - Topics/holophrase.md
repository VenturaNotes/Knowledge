## Source [^1]
- $n$. A grammatically unstructured speech segment, usually a single word, characteristic of the earliest stage of language acquisition in children, such as mama or allgone, according to some authorities analysable as one-word sentences (as in mama $=$ There is mama or allgone $=$ It is all gone) and sometimes found in the language of adults in utterances such as thanks and sorry. Compare PIVOT GRAMMAR, TELEGRAPHIC SPEECH. holophrastic adj. \[From Greek holos whole + phrazein to express]
