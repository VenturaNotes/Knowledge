## Source [^1]
- $n$. an enzyme that catalyses the breakdown of nucleic acids by cleaving the bonds between adjacent nucleotides. Examples are ribonuclease, which acts on RNA, and deoxyribonuclease, which acts on DNA.
## Source[^2]
- Any enzyme that breaks down nucleic acids to nucleotides. Nucleases are found in the small intestine. See DNASE; ENDONUCLEASE; EXONUCLEASE.
