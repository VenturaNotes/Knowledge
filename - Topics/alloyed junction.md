## Source [^1]
- A semiconductor junction formed by bonding metal contacts onto a wafer of semiconductor material and then heating to produce an alloy: it is a method commonly used for germanium diodes and transistors and in early silicon devices, the devices being termed alloyed transistors or diodes. It has been generally superseded by the planar process for all but special-purpose items, although it is a useful technique for gallium arsenide devices.
