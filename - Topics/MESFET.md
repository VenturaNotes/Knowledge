## Source [^1]
- (metal-semiconductor field-effect transistor; Schottky-gate field-effect transistor) A type of junction field-effect transistor that has a Schottky barrier as the gate electrode rather than a semiconductor junction. The current-voltage characteristic is similar to that of a junction FET. The Schottky barrier gate electrode has the advantage that it can be made at much lower temperatures than are required to form a p-n junction.
