## Source [^1]
- The small volume generated at a given 3D point when all its coordinates are subject to small changes. As those changes shrink to zero, this becomes the differential element $dV$ that can be used as the basis for a volume integral. With Cartesian coordinates, the volume element is simply the product of the changes along each axis: $dV = dx\,dy\,dz$.
