## Source [^1]
- When a system of particles or a rigid body with a total mass $m$ experiences a uniform gravitational force, the total effect on the system or body as a whole is equivalent to a single force acting at the centre of gravity. This point coincides with the centre of mass. The centre of gravity moves in the same way as a single particle of mass $m$ would move under the uniform gravitational force. When the gravitational force is given by the inverse square law of gravitation, the same is not generally true.
