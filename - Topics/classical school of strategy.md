## Source [^1]
- An approach to business strategy that emphasizes the role of rational, long-term planning by senior executives with the goal of establishing competitive advantage and thus maximizing profits. It draws on traditions of military strategy and assumes that markets are efficient, managers rational, and organizations hierarchical. The classical school emerged in the 1960s and is exemplified by the work of the US mathematician and management guru Igor Ansoff. See also STRATEGIC MANAGEMENT.
