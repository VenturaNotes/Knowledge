## Source [^1]
- The behavior of firms or individuals that is aimed at influencing the structure of a market. In traditional economics, such situations as monopoly or oligopoly were seen as the outcome of technological conditions and the state of demand. More recently, it has been observed that a particular firm or individual can influence its competitors in the market in various ways, for example by threatening a price war if other firms attempt to enter the market. See INDUSTRIAL ORGANIZATION ECONOMICS. See also GAME THEORY; MARKETING WARFARE STRATEGY; PREDATORY PRICING STRATEGY.
## Source[^2]
- Making a choice of action with an awareness of how the pay-off depends on the choices of other agents, and taking into account how the choice of action will influence the choices of others. See also STRATEGIC INTERACTION.
