## Source [^1]
- $n$. A whitish solidified ester found in many vegetable fats and oils, especially oil of nutmeg; specifically glyceryl trimyristate, formula: $\left(\mathrm{C}_{14} \mathrm{H}_{27} \mathrm{O}_{2}\right)_{3} \mathrm{C}_{3} \mathrm{H}_{5}$. Also, a psychedelic and hallucinogenic drug obtained from nutmeg or mace. \[From Latin Myristica fragrans, the tropical evergreen tree the seeds of which contain nutmeg, from myristicus fragrant, from Greek myron an ointment or perfume + -in(e) indicating an organic compound]
