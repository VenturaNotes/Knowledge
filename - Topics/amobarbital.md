## Source [^1]
- $n$. Amylobarbital sodium, a short-acting barbiturate drug and central nervous system depressant, used as a sedative and hypnotic drug, and sometimes taken as a street drug. Also called amylobarbitone. Amytal (trademark). Formula $\mathrm{C}_{11} \mathrm{H}_{18} \mathrm{~N}_{2} \mathrm{O}_{3} \mathrm{Na}$. \[From amyl, from Greek amylon starch + hyle matter, amyl having been originally synthesized from starch + barbit(uric) (acid) + -al indicating a pharmaceutical product]
