## Source [^1]
- A method of multivariate analysis popular in France and developed by J. Benzecri. The purpose of the technique is to rearrange the rows and columns of a data matrix so that units and variables with similar properties appear together giving rectangular blocks of similar values. The results may also be displayed graphically.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. A technique of data analysis applicable to categorical scores in two-way contingency tables, usually involving the depiction of the interrelationships between categories of row and column variables in two-dimensional maps. It is sometimes considered a form of factor analysis and sometimes as principal-components analysis of qualitative data.
