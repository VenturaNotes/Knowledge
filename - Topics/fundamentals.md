## Source [^1]
- (1) In microeconomic theory, the preferences, endowments, and technology that describe an economy.
- (2) In macroeconomics, the aggregate indicators of a country's economic performance. Most commonly used are the growth rate of gross domestic product, the exchange rate, the inflation rate, the interest rate, and the unemployment rate.
- (3) In finance, basic data about the financial characteristics and market situation of a firm.
