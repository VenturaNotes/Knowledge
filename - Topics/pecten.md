## Source[^2]
- Any of various comblike structures in animals. The pecten in the eyes of birds is a heavily pigmented structure with a network of blood vessels attached to the optic nerve and projecting into the vitreous humor. It is thought to supply the retina with nutrients and oxygen, and maintain acid-base balance inside the eye. A simple form of this structure is found in the eyes of reptiles.
