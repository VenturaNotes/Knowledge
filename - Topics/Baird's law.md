## Source [^1]
- A rule that determines whether the lowest excited triplet state of a planar cyclic molecule is aromatic. It is the reverse of the HÃ¼ckel rule (see aromatic compound), as it states that the lowest excited state for such molecules has $4n$ pi-electrons for aromaticity, where $n$ is a positive integer, and $(4n+2)$ pi-electrons for pseudo-aromaticity. The rule was stated by the Canadian chemist Colin Baird in 1972 on the basis of quantum mechanics.
