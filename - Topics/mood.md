## Source [^1]
- n. 
- (1) A temporary but relatively sustained and pervasive affective state, often contrasted in psychology and psychiatry with a more specific and short-term emotion. See also AFFECT, DEPRESSION, DYSPHORIC MOOD, ELEVATED MOOD, EUTHYMIC MOOD, EXPANSIVE MOOD, IRRITABLE MOOD. 
- (2) In linguistics, a category of verb expressing a mode or manner of action or being, typical grammatical moods being indicative, subjunctive, and imperative. 
- (3) In logic, the form of a syllogism, depending on the nature of its three constituent propositions (1). \[Sense 1 from Old English mod mind or feeling; senses 2 and 3 from Latin modus manner]
