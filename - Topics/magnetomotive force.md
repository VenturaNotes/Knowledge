## Source [^1]
- (m.m.f.) (symbol: $F_{\mathrm{m}}$; unit: ampere; ampere-turn) The line integral of magnetic flux density, $B$, for a closed path, divided by the magnetic constant $\mu_0$:$$F _ {\mathrm {m}} = \mu_ {0} ^ {- 1} \oint \boldsymbol {B}. \mathrm {d} \boldsymbol {l}$$It is equal to the total conduction current linked, during one traverse of the closed path. If the path encloses a current $I$ the value of $F_{\mathrm{m}}$ increases by $I$ for every traverse, i.e. $F_{\mathrm{m}}$ is multivalued unlike electromotive force, which is single-valued.
