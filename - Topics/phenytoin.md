## Source [^1]
- $n$. an anticonvulsant drug used to control tonic-clonic and partial epileptic seizures and status epilepticus. Side-effects include nausea and vomiting, confusion, dizziness, headache, tremor, insomnia, and (more rarely) gum hypertrophy, hirsutism, and skin rashes. Overdosage causes unsteadiness and slurred speech.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. An anticonvulsant drug that is used in the treatment of epilepsy and that acts by stabilizing neuron cell membranes, decreasing the intracellular concentration of sodium and thereby causing hyperpolarization and preventing action potentials. It is also taken as a supposedly smart drug to improve IQ, concentration, verbal performance, and long-term memory. \[A contraction of its chemical name, (di)pheny(lhydan)-toin]
