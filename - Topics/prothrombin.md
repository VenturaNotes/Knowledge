## Source [^1]
- $n$. a substance, present in blood plasma, that is the inactive precursor from which the enzyme thrombin is derived during the process of blood coagulation. See also COAGULATION FACTORS.
## Source[^2]
- (Factor II) One of the blood clotting factors. It is the precursor of the enzyme thrombin, which catalyses the formation of the fibrin matrix of the blood clot. Prothrombin synthesis occurs in the liver and is dependent on adequate supplies of vitamin K. See also Blood Clotting.
