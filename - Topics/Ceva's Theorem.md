## Source [^1]
- Due to Giovanni Ceva (1648-1734), the theorem states: let $L$, $M$, and $N$, be points on the sides $BC, CA, \text{and } AB$ of a triangle (possible extended). Then $AL, BM, \text{and } CN$ are concurrent if and only if $$\frac{BL}{LC}\cdot\frac{CM}{MA}\cdot\frac{AN}{NB}=1$$
