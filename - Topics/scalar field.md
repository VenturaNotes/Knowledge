## Source [^1]
- A scalar field is a real-valued function defined on $\mathbb{R}^3$ or some subset of $\mathbb{R}^3$, though the notion can easily be extended to other dimensions. A field will usually be assumed to have continuous derivatives (see ). Scalar fields are common in physical applied mathematics (examples include temperature, pressure, density, gravitational potential) but may also be studied in their own right in multivariable calculus. Compare vector field.
