## Source [^1]
- adj. Experienced as consistent or harmonious with the total personality. The term was introduced by Sigmund Freud (1856-1939) in 1914 in an article 'On Narcissism: An Introduction' (Standard Edition, XIV, pp. 73-102, at p. 99), and discussed again in 1923 in an encyclopedia article (Standard Edition, XVIII, pp. 235-59, at p. 246). Compare EGO-DYSTONIC. ego syntony $n$. \[From Latin ego I + Greek syn- together + tonos tone + -ikos of, relating to, or resembling]
