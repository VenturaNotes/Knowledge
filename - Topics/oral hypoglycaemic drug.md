## Source [^1]
- (oral antihyperglycaemic drug) one of the group of drugs that reduce the level of glucose in the blood and are taken by mouth for the treatment of type 2 diabetes mellitus. They include the sulphonylurea group (e.g. glibenclamide, gliclazide), metformin (a biguanide), alpha-glucosidase inhibitors, meglitinides, thiazolidinediones, DPP-IV inhibitors, and SGLT-2 inhibitors.
