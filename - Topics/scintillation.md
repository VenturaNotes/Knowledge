## Source [^1]
- (1) A flash of light produced in certain materials (scintillators) when exposed to ionizing radiation. The frequency of the light emitted in the crystal is a function of the energy of the incident radiation. Each incident ionizing event produces one flash. 
- (2) A rapid fluctuation of the image of the target of a radar system about its mean position on the radar display. 
- (3) A small random fluctuation of the received signal in a radio transmission system about the mean signal value. This effect is analogous to the twinkling of light from a star and is caused by small variations in the density of the atmosphere.
