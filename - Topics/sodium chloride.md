## Source [^1]
- common salt: a salt of sodium that is present in all tissues and is important in maintaining the electrolyte balance of the body. Sodium chloride infusions are the basis of fluid replacement therapy after operations and for conditions associated with salt depletion, including shock and dehydration (see SALINE). Sodium chloride is also a basic constituent of oral rehydration therapy and is used to irrigate the eye (e.g. for intraocular surgery) and to treat infections of the bladder (being instilled) and mouth (as a mouthwash).
