## Source [^1]
- A general solution to a linear homogeneous differential equation with constant coefficients, obtained from its characteristic equation. The characteristic equation is a polynomial in which the $i$th derivative is replaced by $r^i$ , which can then be solved for $r$ to obtain all the roots of the polynomial. These roots are used to form the general solution. For example, the characteristic equation of $a_2y'' + a_1y' + a_0y = 0$ is $a_2r^2 + a_1r^1 + a_0 = 0$ . See also PARTICULAR INTEGRAL.
