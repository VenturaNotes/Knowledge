## Source [^1]
- A function of a speech communication system, such as a telephone system, in which it is more important for the received information to be intelligible than for the input to be flawlessly reproduced. The intelligibility is measured by the syllable articulation score, which is the percentage of correctly perceived monosyllabic nonsense words uttered in an uncorrelated sequence.
