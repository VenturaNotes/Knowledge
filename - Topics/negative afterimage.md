## Source [^1]
- $n$. 
- (1) An afterimage in which the lightness contrasts are reversed, light areas of the original stimulus appearing dark and dark areas appearing light. See also complementary afterimage. Compare positive afterimage. 
- (2) Another name for a complementary afterimage, though this usage undermines an important distinction and is avoided in careful usage.
