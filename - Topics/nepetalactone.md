## Source [^1]
- $n$. A chemical compound, mildly sedative to humans but strongly psychotropic to most cats, first isolated in 1941 in catnip. Formula: $\mathrm{C}_{10} \mathrm{H}_{14} \mathrm{O}_{2}$. \[From Latin nepeta catnip + English lactone an organic compound with carbon atoms forming part of a ring, from Latin lac, lactis milk + -one indicating a chemical compound, from -one a feminine name suffix]
