## Source [^1]
- (alpha-adrenergic blocker) a drug that prevents the stimulation of alpha adrenoceptors at the nerve endings of the sympathetic nervous system by noradrenaline and adrenaline: it therefore causes relaxation of smooth muscle, including widening of arteries (vasodilatation) and a drop in blood pressure. Alpha blockers include doxazosin, phentolamine, phenoxybenzamine, indoramin, prazosin, alfuzosin, and tamsulosin.
