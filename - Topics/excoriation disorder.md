## Source [^1]
- $n$. A mental disorder introduced in DSM-5 characterized by recurrent, habitual skin-picking, resulting in skin lesions, most commonly on the face, arms, or hands, despite repeated attempts to eliminate the behavior, the skin-picking causing significant distress or impairment of social, occupational, or everyday life. Compare obsessive-COMPULSIVE DISORDER, TRICHOTILLOMANIA. \[From Latin excoriare to strip or flay, from ex- out + corium a skin or hide]
