## Source [^1]
- (symbol: $h$) A universal constant arising from Planck's law, which states that the energy of electromagnetic radiation is confined to discrete packets or photons. The energy of each photon is given by $h\nu$, where $\nu$ is the frequency of the radiation. Planck's law is fundamental to quantum theory. The value of $h$ is$$6.6260755 \times 10^{-34} \text{ joule second}$$The rationalized Planck constant, $h / 2\pi$ (symbol $\hbar$) has the value$$1.0545726 \times 10^{-34} \text{ Js}$$
