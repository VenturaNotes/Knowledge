## Source [^1]
- $n$. An eating disorder in DSM-5 characterized by persistent eating of inappropriate substances such as paint, plaster, string, hair, cloth, animal droppings, sand, insects, leaves, pebbles, clay, soil, or other non-nutritive matter, but without any aversion to conventional foods, the behavior not occurring as part of a culturally sanctioned practice. See also coprophagia, GEOPHAGIA, HYALOPHAGIA, PAGOPHAGIA. \[From Latin pica a magpie, referring to its fondness for inedible objects]
