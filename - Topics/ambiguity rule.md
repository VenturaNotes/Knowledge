## Source [^1]
- A US legal rule which requires judges to construe ambiguities (expressions that can be given more than one acceptable interpretation) in insurance contracts against the insurer and therefore in favor of the insured. To prevail in a coverage dispute an insured party need only demonstrate that their interpretation of the contract is reasonable to establish that the contract is ambiguous.
