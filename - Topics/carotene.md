## Source [^1]
- $n$. a yellow or orange plant pigment $\textendash$ one of the carotenoids $\textendash$ that occurs in four forms: alpha ($\alpha$), beta ($\beta$), gamma ($\gamma$), and delta ($\delta$). The most important form is $\beta$-carotene, which is an antioxidant and can be converted in the body to retinol (vitamin A). Good sources include yellow and green vegetables, such as carrots, sweet potato, and kale.
## Source[^2]
- A member of a class of carotenoid pigments. Examples are $\beta$-carotene and lycopene, which color carrot roots and ripe tomato fruits respectively. $\alpha$ - and $\beta$-carotene yield vitamin A when they are broken down during animal digestion; $\beta$-carotene has antioxidant properties.
