## Source [^1]
- The emission of electrons from a surface as a result of the impact of other charged particles, especially as a result of bombardment with (primary) electrons. As the number of secondary electrons can exceed the number of primary electrons, the process is important in photomultipliers. See also AUGER EFFECT.
## Source[^2]
- The emission of electrons from the surface of a material, usually a metal, as the result of bombardment by high-velocity electrons or positive ions. The total energy of the incident primary electrons is often sufficient to liberate several secondary electrons per incident particle. The secondary emission ratio, $\delta$, is the number of secondary electrons emitted per incident particle. Secondary emission is mainly used in the electron multiplier.
