## Source [^1]
- Information about speed combined with the direction of movement, typically expressed as a vector quantity. It is formally defined as the derivative with respect to time of a position vector. A velocity is only considered as constant when both the speed and direction are fixed. An example of motion that has constant speed but changing direction is circular motion at a constant angular speed.
