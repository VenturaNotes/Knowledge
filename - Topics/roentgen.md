## Source [^1]
- $n$. a unit of exposure dose of X- or gamma-radiation equal to the dose that will produce $2.58 \times 10^{-4}$ coulomb on all the ions of one sign, when all the electrons released in a volume of air of mass 1 kilogram are completely stopped.
## Source[^2]
- The former unit of dose equivalent (see RADIATION UNITS). It is named after the discoverer of X-rays, W. K. Roentgen (1845-1923).
