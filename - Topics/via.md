## Source [^1]
- A plated hole used to provide low-inductance connections on an integrated circuit. A via may be formed through the dielectric layer on the top of a wafer to interconnect two metallization patterns. A via may also be formed though the back of a wafer containing FETs where a low-inductance earth is required; this is especially useful for microwave or power FETs, and metal plating is used on the back of the wafer to provide a heat sink and earth plane simultaneously (see diagram).
