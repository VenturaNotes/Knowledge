## Source [^1]
- $n$. An index of the difference between the luminance of an image $\left(L_{i}\right)$ and the luminance of its background $\left(L_{b}\right)$ usually expressed as a percentage of the background luminance: $100 \times\left(L_{i}-L_{b}\right) / L_{b}$. Compare CONTRAST (4).
## Source[^2]
- The ratio between the brightest and darkest light levels emitted by pixels in a display. Contrast ratio is often used as a measure of the quality of a display, along with its resolution. The higher the ratio, the deeper the black parts of the image will appear, making dark scenes look much better. See also HIGH DYNAMIC RANGE.
