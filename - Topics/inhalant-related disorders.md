## Source [^1]
- $n$. Substance-related and addictive disorders in DSM-5 induced by inhaling the aliphatic and aromatic hydrocarbons in petrol or gasoline, glue, paint, and some aerosols or, less commonly, the halogenated hydrocarbons in cleaning fluids and other volatile substances in esters, ketones, and glycols. DSM-5 distinguishes between inhalant use disorder and inhalant intoxication. See also SUBSTANCE-INDUCED DISORDERS, SUBSTANCE-RELATED AND ADDICTIVE DISORDERS, SUBSTANCE USE DISORDERS. See GLUE-SNIFFING, SOLVENT ABUSE, SUBSTANCE ABUSE, SUBSTANCE DEPENDENCE, SUBSTANCE-INDUCED DISORDERS, SUBSTANCE USE DISORDERS.
