## Source [^1]
- (LCOS) A liquid-crystal display made by layering liquid crystal directly onto a silicon substrate. Its main advantage over conventional LCD displays is that the circuitry controlling a given pixel is packed underneath it, rather than surrounding it on the surface. This results in a much better image quality. It also allows much finer control of the resulting light, such as its phase or polarization.
