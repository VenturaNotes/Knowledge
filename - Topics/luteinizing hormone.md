## Source [^1]
- (LH) a hormone (see GONADOTROPHIN), synthesized and released by the anterior pituitary gland, that stimulates ovulation, corpus luteum formation, progesterone synthesis by the ovary (see also MENSTRUAL CYCLE), and androgen synthesis by the interstitial cells of the testes. Also called: interstitial cell stimulating hormone (ICSH).

## Source[^2]
- (LH; interstitial-cell-stimulating hormone; ICSH) A hormone, secreted by the anterior pituitary gland in mammals, that stimulates in males the production of sex hormones (androgens) by the interstitial cells of the testes and in females ovulation, progesterone synthesis, and corpus luteum formation. See ovarian CYCLE.
