## Source [^1]
- (involuntary admission) (in Britain) the entry and detention of a person within an institution without his or her consent, either because of mental illness (see Mental Health Act) or severe social deprivation and self-neglect (see SECTION 47 REMOVAL). Compare VOLUNTARY ADMISSION.
