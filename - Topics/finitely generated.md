## Source [^1]
- A group $G$ is finitely generated if there is a finite subset $S$ which generates $G$; that is, the only subgroup of $G$ which contains $S$ is $G$. A cyclic group is generated by a single element; $\mathbb{Q}$ is not finitely generated as an additive group. The term similarly applies to other algebraic structures; a finitely generated vector space is called finite-dimensional.
