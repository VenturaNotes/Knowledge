## Source [^1]
- $n$. A frequently used laboratory measure of aggression in response to provocation, developed by the US psychologist Joel D. Leiberman (born 1967) and three colleagues and published in the journal Aggressive Behavior in 1999. The task is disguised as a food-tasting study. After tasting and rating a sample of food, participants are asked to prepare a food sample for a fictitious participant who has previously provoked them. The amount of hot sauce that the participant chooses to add to this person's food is taken as an indicator of aggression in response to provocation. Compare point Subtraction Aggression Paradigm, Taylor Aggression PARADIGM. HST abbrev.
