## Source [^1]
- $n$. A form of depression (included in the DSM-5 classification) with similar signs and symptoms to major depressive disorder or persistent depressive disorder but considered less severe and more chronic, with the major depressive episodes being less discrete and the depression less severe and more persistent than in major depressive disorder. Also called depressive neurosis, minor depressive disorder , or neurotic depression . See also antidepressant, Borna disease virus, COGNITIVE THERAPY, DYSTHYMIA, INOSITOL. \[From Greek dys- bad or abnormal + thymos spirit + -ikos of, relating to, or resembling]
