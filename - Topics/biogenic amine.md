## Source [^1]
- Any amine that is produced by living organisms, especially the physiologically active amines that serve as neurotransmitters in animals. These include adrenaline (epinephrine), noradrenaline (norepinephrine), dopamine, serotonin (5hydroxytryptamine), and histamine.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. Any member of a group of amines that are produced in the body and that play important roles in nervous system functioning, including catecholamines such as dopamine, adrenalin (epinephrine), and noradrenalin (norepinephrine); indoleamines such as serotonin; and imidazoleamines such as histamine. See also neurotransmitter. \[From Greek bios life + genein to produce, referring to the fact that they are produced naturally in the body, unlike amines such as amphetamine]
