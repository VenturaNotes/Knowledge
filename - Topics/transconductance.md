## Source [^1]
- (symbol: $g_{\mathrm{m}}$) The ratio of output current of a circuit or network to its input voltage, when both are real. This is also the small-signal gain of a transistor:$$g _ {\mathrm {m}} = \frac {\partial I _ {\mathrm {o u t}}}{\partial V _ {\mathrm {i n}}}$$where the output current is the drain or collector current, and the input voltage is the gate-source or base-emitter voltage, for field-effect transistors and bipolar junction transistors, respectively. The symbol $g_{\mathrm{m}}$ is derived from the earlier term for transconductance, mutual conductance.
