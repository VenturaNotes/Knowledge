## Source [^1]
- The constant $\upomega$ in the equation $\ddot x = -\omega^2x$ for simple harmonic motion. The angular frequency $\upomega$ is usually measured in radians per second. The frequency of the oscillations is equal to $\omega / 2 \pi$ and the period is $2 \pi / \upomega$.
## Source[^2]
- (symbol: $\omega$) The frequency of a periodic phenomenon expressed in radians per second. It is equal to the frequency in hertz times $2\pi$.
