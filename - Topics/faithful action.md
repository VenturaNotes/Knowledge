## Source [^1]
- The group action of a group G on a set X is faithful if the only g $\in$ G satisfying $g.x = x$ for all $x \in X$ is the group's identity. Equivalently the homomorphism $G \to \text{Sym}(X)$ is injective, and so every group element is identifiable by its action on X
