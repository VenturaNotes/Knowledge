## Source [^1]
- Logic circuit, usually an integrated circuit, that will convert an asynchronous serial data stream into a byte-parallel form and vice versa. The normal application is in the interfaces for data transmission lines and peripherals
## Source[^2]
- (universal asynchronous receiver/transmitter) An input/output device that sends and receives information in bit-serial fashion. Most computers are equipped with at least one serial port for sending and receiving characters through a cable linked to a corresponding port on a terminal, a modem, a printer, or another computer. A UART within the computer provides the software and hardware interface to the serial port and can be programmed to interrupt the processor when it receives a character from the serial port.
