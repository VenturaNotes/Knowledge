## Source [^1]
- (attenuation coefficient) (symbol: $\alpha$) The rate of exponential decrease in amplitude of voltage, current, or field-component in the direction of propagation of a plane progressive wave, at a given frequency. If $I_{2}$ and $I_{1}$ are the currents at two points a distance $d$ apart ($I_{1}$ being nearer the source of the wave), then$$I _ {2} = I _ {1} \exp (- \alpha d)$$$\alpha$ is usually expressed in nepers or decibels.
