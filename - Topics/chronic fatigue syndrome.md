## Source [^1]
- $n$. A cluster of disorders first identified and named in an article by the US physician Gary P(aul) Holmes (born 1956) and 14 co-authors in the Annals of Internal Medicine in 1988 and redefined in an influential article in the same journal in 1994, characterized by persistent or recurrent fatigue (1) experienced for at least four to six months, not resulting from unusual exertion or any identifiable medical cause and not significantly relieved by rest, resulting in a marked decline in occupational, educational, social, and everyday activities. Some authorities believe that it is often triggered by a viral infection. See also actigraphy, Borna disease virus, myalgic encephalomyelitis, post-viral fatigue SYNDROME. CFS abbrev.
