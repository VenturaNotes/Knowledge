## Source [^1]
- The time that will elapse before an item in stock runs out. The concept is essential for scheduling the reordering of a range of goods. The run-out time for a product $i$ is defined as: $$r_{\mathrm{i}}=l_{\mathrm{i}} / d_{\mathrm{i}}$$where $r_{\mathrm{i}}$ is run-out time in weeks, $l_{\mathrm{i}}$ is units in stock, and $d_{\mathrm{i}}$ is demand in units per week.
