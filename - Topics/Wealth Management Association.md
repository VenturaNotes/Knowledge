## Source [^1]
- (WMA) A representative body for private-client investment managers and stockbrokers, formed as the Association of Private Client Investment Managers and Stockbrokers (APCIMS) in 1990. It aims to improve the environment and expand the market in which private investors' business is transacted. The association represents $95 \%$ of eligible firms with a network of over 550 offices throughout the UK and Ireland. It publishes a directory of private-client stockbrokers, with details of members and the services they provide. The association, which adopted its current name in 2013, is run by an elected committee with a small permanent secretariat based in London, assisted by specialist practitioner committees.
