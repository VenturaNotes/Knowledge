A fundamental result in game theory, establishing that every finite, strictly competitive (two-person zero-sum) game has what later came to be called a Nash equilibrium, provided that mixed strategies may be used, and that the corresponding strategies are minimax strategies. The theorem was first proved by the Hungarian-born US mathematician John von Neumann (1903-57) and published in the journal Mathematische Annalen in 1928.
