## Source [^1]
- $n$. An organic compound formed from ammonia by replacing one or more of the hydrogen atoms with one or more hydrocarbon radicals (2). Amines such as adrenalin (epinephrine), noradrenalin (norepinephrine), dopamine, histamine, serotonin, and tryptamine are of special importance in neurobiology because of their function as neurotransmitters. See also biogenic amine, catecholamine, imidazoleamine, indoleamine, monoamine. \[From am(monia) + -ine indicating an organic compound]
