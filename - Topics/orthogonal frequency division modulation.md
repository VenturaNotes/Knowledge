## Source [^1]
- (OFDM) A modulation technique in which a large number of symbols are sent at the same time on closely spaced carrier frequencies. This allows the symbol period to be dramatically extended, while not requiring a reduction in the bit rate; it is widely used for digital radio and television broadcasting.
