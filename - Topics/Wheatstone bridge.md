## Source [^1]
- A four-arm bridge used for measuring resistance. Each arm contains a resistance (see diagram), with the unknown and reference resistances, $R_{1}$ and $R_{2}$, being connected at a point. At balance, when a null response is obtained from the indicating instrument,$$\frac {R _ {1}}{R _ {2}} = \frac {R _ {3}}{R _ {4}}$$The two arms $R_3$ and $R_4$ are known as the ratio arms and may take the form of a wire of uniform resistance, which is tapped by a sliding contact. $R_3$ and $R_4$ are proportional to the lengths of wire, $l_1$ and $l_2$, on each side of the contact, so that$$\frac {R _ {1}}{R _ {2}} = \frac {l _ {1}}{l _ {2}}$$
