## Source [^1]
- (dividing network) A type of filter circuit that divides the frequency range passed between a number of paths. Frequencies in a specified range pass through one path and those outside that range through other paths. The frequency at which the output passes from one channel to another is the crossover frequency and at that frequency the outputs are equal. Such networks are widely used with loudspeakers to separate the bass, mid-range, and treble components.
