## Source [^1]
- $n$. A proposition formulated in 1911 by the US psychologist Edward Lee Thorndike (1874-1949) according to which repetition tends to strengthen the association between a stimulus (1) and a response, making the response more likely to occur on the next presentation of the stimulus. Compare LAW OF EFFECT.
