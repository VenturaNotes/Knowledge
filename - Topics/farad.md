## Source [^1]
- $n$. the SI unit of capacitance, equal to the capacitance of a capacitor between the plates of which a potential difference of 1 volt appears when it is charged with 1 coulomb of electricity. Symbol: F.
## Source[^2]
- (symbol: F) The SI unit of capacitance. A capacitor has a capacitance of one farad when a charge of one coulomb increases the potential difference between its plates by one volt. The farad is too large for most practical purposes, the submultiples microfarad, nanofarad, and picofarad being more convenient.
