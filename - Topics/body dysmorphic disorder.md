## Source [^1]
- a fixed distressing belief that one's body or part of one's body is deformed, or an irrational fear that it might become so. Previously known as dysmorphophobia, it is usually considered to be a phobic rather than a delusional disorder. Treatment consists of cognitive behavioral therapy and occasionally SSRIs.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. A mental disorder in DSM-5 characterized by a pathological preoccupation with an imagined or exaggerated defect in physical appearance. Also called dysmorphophobia. See also Adonis COMPLEX, MUSCLE DYSMORPHIA, SOMATIC DELUSION, TAIJIN KYOFUSHO. BDD abbrev. \[From Greek dys- bad or abnormal + morphe form]
