## Source [^1]
- (Joule effect) If a current, $I$, is passed through a circuit of resistance $R$, electrical energy is dissipated as the current flows from a higher to a lower potential. The rate of dissipation of energy equals $I^2 R$. This is known as $I^2 R$ loss. The energy appears as heat; if all the electrical energy is converted into a quantity of heat $Q$, then$$Q = I^2 R t$$where $t$ is the time for which the current flows. The temperature does not increase indefinitely since a stable state is reached when the rate of emission from the surface of the resistor just equals the rate of generation of heat.
