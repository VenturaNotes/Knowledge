## Source [^1]
- The magnetic flux density, $B$, at a point distance $r$ from a current-carrying conductor in free space when a current $I$ flows in an element of length $ds$ is given by$$\mathrm{d} \boldsymbol{B} = \frac{\mu_0 I (\mathrm{d} s \times r)}{4 \pi r^3} = \mu_0 I \mathrm{d} s \sin\theta / 4 \pi r^2$$where $\mu_0$ is the permeability of free space (see diagram). Integration of the Biot-Savart law gives Ampere’s circuiting theorem (see AMPERE’S LAW).
