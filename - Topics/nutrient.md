## Source [^1]
- $n$. a substance that must be consumed in order to provide the essential components needed for growth and the maintenance of life. Nutrients include carbohydrates, fats, proteins, minerals, and vitamins. See also ESSENTIAL AMINO ACID; ESSENTIAL FATTY ACID; TRACE ELEMENT.
## Source[^2]
- Any substance that is required for the nourishment of an organism, providing a source of energy or structural components. In animals nutrients form part of the diet and include the major nutrients, i.e. carbohydrates, proteins (see also ESSENTIAL AMINO ACID), and lipids (see also ESSENTIAL FATTY ACIDS), as well as vitamins and certain minerals (see ESSENTIAL ELEMENT). Plant nutrients, derived from carbon dioxide in the atmosphere and water (containing minerals) absorbed from the soil by the roots, are divided into macronutrients and micronutrients.
