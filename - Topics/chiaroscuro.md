## Source [^1]
- $n$. The disposition of areas of light and shade in an image; specifically one of the monocular cues of visual depth perception, patterns of shadow and highlight providing information about depth (see illustration). The human visual system usually interprets such information on the unconscious assumption that the light source is above. Also called shading. See also anAGLYPtoscope, hOLlow-FACE Illusion. \[From Italian chiaro clear + oscuro obscure or dark]

	- Chiaroscuro. Most of the shapes in the upper panel appear convex and most of those in the lower panel concave, because the visual system assumes by default that the light source is above.
