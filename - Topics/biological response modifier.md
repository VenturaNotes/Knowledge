## Source [^1]
- (BRM) a therapeutic agent, such as interferon or interleukin, that influences the body's defence mechanisms to act against infection and disease. In small amounts, these substances are produced naturally by the body; larger doses have been genetically developed for use against rheumatoid arthritis, hepatitis, and cancer (especially melanoma and renal cancer).
