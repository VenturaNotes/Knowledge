## Source [^1]
- n. 1-1-Phenycyclohexylpiperidine, or phencyclidine hydrochloride for short, a central nervous system depressant developed in the 1950s as an anaesthetic (1) and used in veterinary medicine to produce trancelike anaesthetic states in animals, adopted in the 1960s as a psychedelic street drug capable of inducing vivid imagery. Street names include hog, peace pill, tranq, and most commonly angel dust. Formula $\mathrm{C}_{17} \mathrm{H}_{25} \mathrm{~N}$. See also hallucinogen, phantasticant, phencyclidine-related DISORDERS. Compare KETAMINE. PCP abbrev.
