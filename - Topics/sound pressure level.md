## Synthesis
- While "sound pressure level" is often expressed in decibels (dB), they are not the same thing
- Sound pressure level refers to the measurement of [[sound pressure]] itself
## Source [^1]
- Historically called the [[decibel]] (dB)
- Absolute amount of pressure change exerted in the air by sound
## Source[^2]
- (SPL) The root-mean-square (r.m.s.) pressure of a sound wave at a particular instant $(P_{\mathrm{actual}})$, usually expressed in decibels (dBSPL) with respect to the average threshold of hearing at 1 kilohertz $(P_{\mathrm{ref}})$ of 20 micropascal:$$\mathrm{dBSPL} = 20 \log_{10} \left(\frac{P_{\mathrm{actual}}}{P_{\mathrm{ref}}}\right)$$
