## Source [^1]
- n. 
- (1) In cognitive psychology, a mental representation or image occurring without simultaneous sensation or perception of the corresponding object or event, though frequently based on memories of earlier perceptions. 
- (2) Any conception, thought, or mental content. 
- (3) In the philosophy of the Greek philosopher Plato (?427-?347 BC), a supposed ideal form or archetype (1) of a class of things, individual things being imperfect copies of archetypal ideas and deriving their existence from them. See also ideation, innate idea. \[From Greek idea a model, pattern, or form, from idein to see]
