## Source [^1]
- $n$. In DSM-5, a trauma- and stressor-related disorder of infancy or childhood, characterized by a pattern of behavior in which a child displays indiscriminate sociability to unfamiliar adults, acting in an overly familiar and trusting manner, together with evidence that the child has experienced pathogenic care, assumed to be responsible for the disturbed social relating, such as neglect or persistent lack of adult care, repeated changes of carers with limited opportunity to form stable attachments, or rearing in institutions with insufficient numbers of carers or other circumstances of severely limited opportunities for forming attachments to carers. See also attachment theory, trauma- and STRESSOR-RELATED DISORDERS. Compare ATTENTION-DEFICIT/HYPERACTIVITY DISORDER, REACTIVE ATTACHMENT DISORDER.
