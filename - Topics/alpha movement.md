## Source [^1]
- $n$. A form of apparent movement that is produced when stimuli of different sizes, such as bars of different lengths, are presented in an alternating pattern with an interstimulus interval of about 60 milliseconds, the visual illusion being created of a single object increasing and decreasing in size. Also called alpha motion . See also Korte's laws. Compare BETA MOVEMENT, DELTA MOVEMENT, GAMMA MOVEMENT.
