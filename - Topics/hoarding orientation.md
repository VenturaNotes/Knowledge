## Source [^1]
- $n$. In neo-Freudian theory, a term used by the German-born US psychoanalyst Erich Fromm (1900-80) to denote a character type in which security is obtained by holding on to possessions, spending is felt to be threatening and anxiety-arousing, and miserliness extends to feelings as well as material things. Compare EXPLOITATIVE ORIENTATION, MARKETING ORIENTATION, PRODUCTIVE ORIENTATION, RECEPTIVE ORIENTATION.
