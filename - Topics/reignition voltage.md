## Source [^1]
- The voltage required to re-establish the discharge in a gas-discharge tube after the tube has ceased conducting. The reignition voltage is often applied during the period of deionization of the tube, i.e. the interval during which the ions in the tube recombine to form gas molecules, and can therefore be less than the voltage required to initiate the first discharge.
