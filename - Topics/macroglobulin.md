## Source [^1]
- n. 
- (1) (immunoglobulin M, IgM) a protein of the globulin series that is present in the blood and functions as an antibody, forming an effective first-line defence against bacteria in the bloodstream. See also IMMUNOGLOBULIN. 
- (2) an abnormal form of IgM (see PARAPROTEIN) produced by lymphoma cells or in other plasma-cell disorders, such as multiple myeloma.
