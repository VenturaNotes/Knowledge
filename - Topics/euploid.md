## Source [^1]
- Describing a nucleus, cell, or organism that has an exact multiple of the haploid number $(n)$ of chromosomes. For example, diploid (2n), triploid (3n), and tetraploid (4n) nuclei or cells are all euploid. Compare aneuploid.
