## Source [^1]
- Having the same variance. A set, or a vector, of observations is homoscedastic if the variance of the random error is the same for all observations. This is one of the classical linear regression assumptions underlying the Gauss-Markov theorem. Violation of this assumption is called heteroscedasticity.
