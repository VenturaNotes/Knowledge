## Source [^1]
- (1) The dividends due on a security. With a bearer security, the holder claims payments due by using a physical coupon attached to the documents of title.
- (2) Tokens, usually paper, used as evidence of entitlement under rationing schemes.
## Source[^2]
- (1) One of several dated slips attached to a bond, which must be presented to the agents of the issuer or the company to obtain an interest payment or dividend. They are usually used with bearer securities; the coupon yield is the yield provided by a bearer bond. 
- (2) The rate of interest paid by a fixed-interest bond. The annual payment is a percentage of the bond's nominal value. 
- (3) (coupon security) A bond or note on US Treasury Markets. 
- (4) A fixed-rate payment on an interest or currency swap.
