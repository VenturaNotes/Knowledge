## Source [^1]
- A sensory cell or group of cells that reacts to the presence of light. It usually contains a pigment that undergoes a chemical change when light is absorbed, thus stimulating a nerve. See EYE.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. A sensory receptor situated at the back of the retina, either a large cylindrical rod or a smaller tapering cone, specialized to convert light stimulation into electrical nerve impulses or action potentials. The human retina contains approximately 120 million rods and 6 million cones. Also called a visual receptor. \[From Greek phos, photos light + English receptor, from Latin recipere, receptum to receive, from re- back + capere, ceptum to take]
