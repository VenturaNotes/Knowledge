## Source [^1]
- $n$. A legal term denoting criminal intent and knowledge of the wrongness of a criminal act, the criminal act itself being called the actus reus. For a defendant to be convicted of a crime, both an actus reus and a mens rea must normally be proved. See also insanity, McNaghten rules. \[From Latin mens mind + rea guilty]
