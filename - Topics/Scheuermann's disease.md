## Source [^1]
- (adolescent kyphosis) a disorder of spinal growth in which a sequence of three or more vertebrae become slightly wedge-shaped. It arises in adolescence and usually occurs in the thoracic spine, causing poor posture, backache, fatigue, and exaggerated kyphosis. X-ray findings include Schmorl's nodes. \[H. W. Scheuermann (1877-1960), Danish surgeon]
