## Source [^1]
- The cost of a product that is not paid by its producer but is paid by society in general. An example would be the environmental cost involved in a production process that involves the discharge of pollutants into the environment (e.g. into the sea or the atmosphere). See also EXTERNALITY.
## Source[^2]
- The total cost of any activity. This includes private costs which fall directly on the person or firm conducting the activity, as well as external costs outside the price system which fall on other people or firms.
