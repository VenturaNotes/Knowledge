## Source [^1]
- $n$. In psychoanalysis, absorbing a relationship or an instinctual object into one's mental apparatus, as when a child's relationship with an authoritarian father is internalized as the relationship between an ego and a strict superego. In Kleinian analysis the term is used more broadly as simply another name for introjection: fantasizing the absorption of a whole object or a part object.
