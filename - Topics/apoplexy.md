## Source [^1]
- $n$. Abrupt loss of consciousness, often followed by paralysis, usually caused by a rupture or thrombosis of a blood vessel in the brain. apoplectic adj. Of, relating to, or resembling apoplexy; colloquially (arising from a misapprehension of the nature of the disorder), enraged or infuriated. \[From Greek apoplexia paralysis from a stroke, from apo away + plessein to strike]
