## Source [^1]
- The seven ways that wastage can occur in industrial processes, as enumerated by Shigeo Shingo, an engineer at Toyota and a noted authority on just-in-time techniques. They are:
	- waste of overproduction$\textemdash$make only what is needed now;
	- waste of waiting;
	- waste of transportation;
	- waste of processing$\textemdash$does this product or part need to be made?;
	- waste of stocks;
	- waste of motion$\textemdash$first improve, then mechanize or automate;
	- waste of making defective products$\textemdash$accept no defects and make no defects.
- See also lean production
