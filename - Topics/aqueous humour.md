## Source [^1]
- the watery fluid that fills the chamber of the eye immediately behind the cornea and in front of the lens. It is continually being formed - chiefly by capillaries of the ciliary processes $\textendash$ and it drains away into Schlemm's canal, at the junction of the cornea and sclera.
## Source[^2]
- The fluid that fills the space between the cornea and the lens of the vertebrate eye. In addition to supplying the cornea and lens with nutrients, the aqueous humour helps to maintain the shape of the eye. It is produced and renewed every four hours by the ciliary body.
