## Source [^1]
- the watery fluid that fills the chamber of the eye immediately behind the cornea and in front of the lens. It is continually being formed - chiefly by capillaries of the ciliary processes $\textendash$ and it drains away into Schlemm's canal, at the junction of the cornea and sclera.
## Source[^2]
- The fluid that fills the space between the cornea and the lens of the vertebrate eye. In addition to supplying the cornea and lens with nutrients, the aqueous humor helps to maintain the shape of the eye. It is produced and renewed every four hours by the ciliary body.
## Source[^3]
- $n$. The watery fluid in the space between the cornea and crystalline lens of the eye. US aqueous humor. See also Glaucoma. Compare vitreous humor. \[From Latin aqua water + -osus having or characterized by + umor bodily fluid, from umere to be moist]
