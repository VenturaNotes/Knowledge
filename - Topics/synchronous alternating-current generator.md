## Source [^1]
- (alternator; synchronous generator) An alternating-current generator that consists of one or more coils that are made to rotate in the magnetic fields produced by several electromagnets excited by a direct-current source. The frequency, $f$, of the alternating currents and emfs induced in the coils is equal to the product of the speed, $n_{\mathrm{s}}$, at which the coils rotate and the number of pairs of magnetic poles, $p$. This type of generator can operate and produce electrical power independently of any other source of alternating current and is the type most commonly employed in power stations.
