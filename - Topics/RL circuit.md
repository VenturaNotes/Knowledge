## Source [^1]
- (RL filter) A circuit built using resistors and inductors. The simplest are two-port networks that have one of each component—one in series with the input port and the other in parallel with the output. They are often used as filters. A high-pass filter will have the resistor in series with the input, and the capacitor in parallel with the output, while switching them around results in a low-pass filter. See also RC CIRCUIT; RLC CIRCUIT.
