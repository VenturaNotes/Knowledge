## Source [^1]
- (1) Having purchased more of a good than one needs or has orders for. 
- (2) Having purchased more securities or commodities than are covered by margins deposited with a broker or dealer. In a falling market, for example, a bull speculator can become overbought without having made a fresh purchase. 
- (3) Denoting a market that has risen too rapidly as a result of excessive buying. An overbought market is unstable and likely to fall if unsupported. Compare OVERSOLD.
