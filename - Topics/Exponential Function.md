## Source[^2]
- exponential function A mathematical function of the form$$f(z) = a \cdot b^{(z + c)}$$where $a$, $b$, and $c$ are constants and $z$ can be a real or complex number. Because of the laws of exponentiation, all such functions can be rewritten in the form$$f(z) = k \cdot e^{z}$$where $k$ is a constant depending on $a$, $b$, and $c$, and $e$ (2.71828 approx.) is the base of natural logarithms.
