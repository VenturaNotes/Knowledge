## Source [^1]
- A body set up in 1989 whose members include hardware and chip manufacturers, software houses, and system integrators. The PCMCIA defined two specifications for add-in cards of credit-card size: PC card (formerly [[PCMCIA card]]) and its successor, ExpressCard. Cards can contain such peripherals as hard disks, modems, flash memory, or network interfaces. The association was dissolved in 2009.
## Source[^2]
- (Personal Computer Memory Card International Association) An organization of approximately 500 companies that specifies a standard for small devices used for the expansion of portable computers. Originally the standard was used for memory, but it is now suitable for many types of devices. There are three types, all with the same plan size of $85.6 \times 54 \mathrm{~mm}$, but of different thicknesses:
	- Type I: primarily for ROM or RAM, these are $3.3 \mathrm{~mm}$ thick.
	- Type II: often used for communications cards, these are $5.5 \mathrm{~mm}$ thick. Type II slots can also hold one Type I card.
	- Type III: can accept portable disk drives and are 10.5 mm thick. Type III slots can also hold two Type I or II cards in any combination.
