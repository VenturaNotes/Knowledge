## Source [^1]
- (symbol: $k$) A fundamental constant having the value $1.380\,658 \times 10^{-23}$ joule per kelvin.
- In a system in which $n_1$ particles have an energy $E_1$ and $n_2$ particles have an energy $E_2$ then$$n_1 = n_2 \exp \left[ - \left(E_1 - E_2\right) / kT \right]$$where $T$ is the thermodynamic temperature. This is the Boltzmann distribution law.
