## Source [^1]
- (DTS) A six-channel digital audio coding format similar to Dolby digital 5.1. DTS uses less compression than Dolby digital 5.1 to attempt to produce better-perceived sound at the expense of significantly greater bandwidth requirements. In addition to film and video use, DTS can be used in the mixing and reproduction of music.
