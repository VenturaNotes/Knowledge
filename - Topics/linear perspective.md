## Source [^1]
- $n$. One of the monocular cues of visual depth perception, a form of perspective closely related to aerial perspective and texture gradient, arising from the fact that an object of a fixed size subtends a smaller visual angle when it is far away than when it is relatively close, so that parallel lines such as rail tracks appear to converge towards a vanishing point at the horizon. See also CARPENTERED WORLD, LEANING TOWER ILLUSION.
