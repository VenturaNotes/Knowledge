## Source [^1]
- nonallergic food hypersensitivity: a non-life-threatening reaction (often delayed) to a food, drink, food additive, or compound that results in symptoms in the respiratory system, gut, or skin. It can result from metabolic reactions, pharmacological reactions, or malabsorption; treatment uses elimination and exclusion diets and the formation of tolerance over a period of time.
