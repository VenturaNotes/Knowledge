## Source [^1]
- $n$. In stimulus generalization, a change in the strength or frequency of a response to a variable stimulus as a function of the degree to which it resembles a particular stimulus to which the response has been learned or conditioned. It is sometimes used to examine perceptual similarity and discrimination in infants and non-human organisms that cannot give verbal judgements.
