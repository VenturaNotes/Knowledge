## Source [^1]
- $n$. the production of offspring by parents who are not closely related. Compare INBREEDING.
## Source[^2]
- Mating between unrelated or distantly related individuals of a species. Outbreeding populations usually show more variation than inbreeding ones and have a greater potential for adapting to environmental changes. Outbreeding increases the number of heterozygous individuals, so disadvantageous recessive characteristics tend to be masked by dominant alleles.
## Source[^3]
- $n$. Producing offspring through mating between parents that are not closely related. Also called exogamy.
