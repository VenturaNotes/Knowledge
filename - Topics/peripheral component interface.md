## Source [^1]
- A standard, designed by Intel, for a local bus that connects peripherals to a personal computer, and published in 1993. The design is independent of the processor, handling 32 or 64 bits at up to 66 MHz . Although superseded by PCI Express in 2004 it is still widely used.
