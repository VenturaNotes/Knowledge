## Source [^1]
- In a topological space $X$, points $x$ and $y$ in $X$ are topologically indistinguishable if they have exactly the same neighborhoods. Otherwise, they are distinguishable. Generally, ‘distinguishable’ is stronger than ‘distinct’ but not as strong as being separated. Compare functionally separable, separation axioms.
