## Source [^1]
- $n$. Either of two wrinkled olive-shaped clumps of neurons in the upper part of the medulla oblongata, just below the pons, connected to the thalamus, cerebellum, and spinal cord, implicated in motor coordination. Also called an inferior olivary complex or an inferior olive. Compare SUPERIOR OLIVARY NUCLEUS. ION abbrev.
