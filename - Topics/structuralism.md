## Source [^1]
- A philosophy of mathematics, dating to the 1960s, which maintains that mathematical objects are entirely defined by their position in mathematical structures. As such, mathematical objects do not have any intrinsic properties. Structuralism contrasts with some philosophies that would place a primacy on foundational theory (e.g. set theory, category theory).
