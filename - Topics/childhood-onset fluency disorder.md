## Source [^1]
- n. A communication disorder introduced in DSM-5, characterized by disturbed fluency and rhythm of speech, involving repetitions of sounds or syllables, prolonged consonants and vowels, words broken by pauses, word substitutions to avoid problem words, and excessive physical tension in pronouncing words, the symptoms causing anxiety and affecting social, academic, or occupational functioning, with onset in early development (2 to 6 years in most cases), not attributable to deafness, neurological impairment, a side-effect of medication, general medical condition, or another mental disorder, such as Tourette's disorder. Also (informally) called stuttering or stammering .
