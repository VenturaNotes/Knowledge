## Source [^1]
- The marketing of related products as a single unit. The price of the bundle is set at less than the sum of the prices of the separate items. For example, a computer is usually supplied in a bundle with software. Bundling is an example of second-degree price discrimination and is employed to increase profit by extracting additional consumer surplus.
## Source[^2]
- The marketing ploy of giving away a relatively cheap product with a relatively expensive one to attract customers; for example, incorporating one or more CD-ROMs with the software package supplied with a computer. See PRICE-BUNDLING STRATEGY.
