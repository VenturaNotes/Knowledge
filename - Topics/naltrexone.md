## Source [^1]
- $n$. an opioid antagonist drug that is used to prevent relapse in formerly heroin- and other opioid-dependent patients. Possible side-effects include abdominal cramps, nausea and vomiting, sleep difficulties, and dizziness.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. A long-acting narcotic antagonist that blocks the action of opiates, including the euphoria that they induce, and that is thus used in the treatment of narcotic overdose and opioid-related disorders. Compare NALORPhINE, NALOXONE. \[A blend of naloxone and Trexan a trademark]
