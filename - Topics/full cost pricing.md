## Source [^1]
- An approach to setting selling prices that attempts to ensure that the price of a good or service is based on all the costs incurred in its supply, including overheads. It usually involves an absorption approach to the costing of units. Compare COST-PLUS PRICING; MARGINAL COST PRICING.
