## Source [^1]
- (Rogerian therapy) a method of psychotherapy in which the therapist refrains from directing clients in what they should do and instead concentrates on communicating understanding and acceptance. Frequently the therapist reflects the clients' own words or feelings back to them. The aim is to enable clients to solve their own problems.
