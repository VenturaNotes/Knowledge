## Source [^1]
- A tax that is levied to correct market failure when the production or consumption of a good creates an externality. A negative externality will require a positive Pigouvian tax to ensure efficiency; the converse holds for a positive externality. Congestion charges and effluent charges are examples of Pigouvian taxes.
