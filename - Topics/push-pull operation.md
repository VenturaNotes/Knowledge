## Source [^1]
- The use of two matched devices in such a way that they operate with a $180^{\circ}$ phase difference. The output circuits combine the separate outputs in phase (Fig. a). One common means of achieving the desired $180^{\circ}$ phase shift in the inputs is a transformer-coupled input circuit (Fig. b). Complementary transistors may also be used (Fig. c), in which case no phase shift is required in the inputs.

- Push-pull circuits are frequently used for class A and class B amplification and are then termed push-pull amplifiers. A push-pull amplifier that is suitably biased to give negligible output current when no input signal is present is termed a quiescent push-pull amplifier.
