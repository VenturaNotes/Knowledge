## Source [^1]
- n. a benzodiazepine drug with muscle relaxant properties, used for the short-term relief of anxiety and to lessen the severity of symptoms in alcohol withdrawal. Side-effects include drowsiness, lightheadedness, and shaky movements.

## Source[^2]
- $n$. A member of the benzodiazepine group of drugs, used as an anxiolytic, sedative, and muscle relaxant, and in the treatment of alcohol-related disorders. In its hydrochloride form it is usually called Librium (trademark). See also SEdative-, hypnotic-, or anxiolytic-Related DISORDERS.
