## Source [^1]
- A term used to describe the area represented by a definite integral. If an integrand $f(x)$ changes signs in an interval $a \le x \le b$, then the definite integral $\int_a^b f(x) dx$ equals the area above the $x$-axis and below the graph, minus the area below the $x$-axis and above the graph. Instead, the total area between the graph and the $x$-axis equals $\int_a^b |f(x)| dx$.
