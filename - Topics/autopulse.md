## Source [^1]
- $n$. a mechanical device designed to compress the chest (and thereby the heart) rhythmically, with the aim of maintaining the circulation during cardiac arrest. It is more efficient than manual chest compression and has the advantage of allowing percutaneous coronary intervention to be performed without interruption of chest compression.
