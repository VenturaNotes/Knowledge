## Source [^1]
- $n$. A change in perception of a visual stimulus resulting from prolonged (usually 30 seconds or more) exposure to a different visual stimulus, as when the contour of the second stimulus falling on the retina near the contour of the original stimulus appears displaced away from the position occupied by the original contour, or more generally any distortion in visual perception resulting from a prior visual stimulus, as occurs in successive contrast. See also aFtEREFFECT, CONTINGENT aFtEREFFECT, MOTION aFtEREFFECT, SPIRAL aFtEREFFECT, TILT aFtEREFFECT, VISUAL aFtEREFFECT. FAE abbrev.
