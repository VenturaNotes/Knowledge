## Source [^1]
- (on-line) (1) Connected to the system and usable. See also OFFLINE. 
- (2) Connected to a network, especially the Internet. 
- (3) In automaton theory, describing an automaton that, having read the first $k$ symbols of the input string, has already produced the first $k$ symbols of the output string. The concept of being online is analogous to the initial subwords-preserving property of gsm mappings.
