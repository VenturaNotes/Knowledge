## Source [^1]
- An approach to financial regulation that involves using a set of targeted rules to reduce levels of instability and systemic risk across the financial system as a whole. Such an approach has been adopted in many countries as a response to the financial crisis of 200708. In the UK the Bank of England's Financial Policy Committee, set up in 2013, is the authority charged with this type of regulation. Compare MICROPRUDENTIAL REGULATION.
