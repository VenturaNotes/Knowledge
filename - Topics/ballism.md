## Source [^1]
- $n$. violent repetitive involuntary movements particularly involving the proximal limbs. They occur following bilateral damage to the subthalamic nuclei of the basal ganglia, most commonly after stroke, haemorrhage, or trauma. In hemiballismus, only one side of the body is affected due to contralateral subthalamic dysfunction.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. A condition usually resulting from damage to the subthalamic nucleus characterized by involuntary violent flailing of the limbs, resembling throwing movements, which are exhausting and incapacitating. Compare hemiBALLISM. \[From Greek ballein to throw]
