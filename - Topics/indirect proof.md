## Source [^1]
- A theorem of the form $p \implies q$ can be proved by establishing instead its contrapositive, by supposing $\lnot q$ and showing that $\lnot p$ follows. Such a method is called an indirect proof. Another example of an indirect proof is the method of proof by contradiction
