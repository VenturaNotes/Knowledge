## Source [^1]
- $n$. a naturally occurring polypeptide consisting of nine amino acids. Bradykinin is a very powerful vasodilator and causes contraction of smooth muscle; it is formed in the blood under certain conditions and is thought to play an important role as a mediator of inflammation. See KININ.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. A kinin that is formed in injured tissue in human and animal bodies, causing contraction of most smooth muscles but dilation of blood vessels. \[From Greek bradys slow + kineein to move, so called because it causes slow muscle contractions]
