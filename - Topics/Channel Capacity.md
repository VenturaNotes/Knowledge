## Source [^1]
- Channel capacity refers to data traffic that can be handled by a channel, or the total amount of bandwidth that a channel may have.
## Source[^2]
- The amount of information that can be transmitted over a communication channel. Channel capacity cannot be exceeded without error but in theory can always be attained with an arbitrarily small probability of error. See also SHANNON-HARTLEY THEOREM; DIGITAL COMMUNICATIONS.
