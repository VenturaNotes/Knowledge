## Source [^1]
- (AAC) An MPEG standardized format for encoding audio with lossy compression, intended as a replacement for MP3. The main improvement is the use of a psychoacoustic model to aid the identification of signal components that are not audible and arrange for their removal without too much adverse effect on the remaining audible components. AAC is used on a wide variety of music players, smartphones, and gaming consoles as well as music playback software on personal computers.
