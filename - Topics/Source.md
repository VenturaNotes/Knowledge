## Source [^1]
- (1) The point in a vector field at which lines of flux originate; an example is a positive electric charge in an electrostatic field. A point at which lines of flux terminate is a sink. 
- (2) Any device that produces electrical energy, e.g. a current source. 
- (3) The electrode in a field-effect transistor that supplies charge carriers (holes or electrons) to the interelectrode space.
