## Source [^1]
- If a triangle's vertices have position vectors $\mathbf{a}, \mathbf{b}, \mathbf{c}$, the position vector of any point of the plane containing the triangle can be written uniquely as $\boldsymbol{\alpha} \mathbf{a}+\boldsymbol{\beta} \mathbf{b}+$ $\gamma \mathbf{c}$ where $\alpha+\beta+\gamma=1$. The point's barycentric coordinates are $\alpha, \beta, \gamma$. Points inside the triangle have positive barycentric coordinates. At the triangle's centroid $\alpha=\beta=\gamma=1 / 3$. Barycentric coordinates can similarly be defined for a simplex.
