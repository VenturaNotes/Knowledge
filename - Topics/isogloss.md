## Source [^1]
- $n$. A line on a map enclosing an area within which a particular linguistic feature (1) is found. Various types of isogloss are distinguished: an isophone is a feature of pronunciation, an isolex an item of vocabulary, an isomorph a feature of word formation, and an isoseme a particular word meaning. \[From Greek isos equal + glossa a tongue]
