## Source [^1]
- (MMDA) A type of high-yielding savings account introduced in the USA in 1982 to allow deposit-taking institutions to compete with the money markets for savers' funds. Typically, such accounts require a minimum balance of between $\$ 1,000$ and $\$ 10,000$ and restrict withdrawals to six a month.
