## Source [^1]
- (Symbol F). The electric charge carried by one mole of electrons or singly ionized ions, i.e. the product of the Avogadro constant and the charge on an electron (disregarding sign). It has the value 9.648 533 289(79) × 104 coulombs per mole. This number of coulombs is sometimes treated as a unit of electric charge called the faraday.

## Source[^2]
- (symbol: $F$) A fundamental constant having the value $9.648~5309 \times 10^{4}$ coulombs per mole. It is the quantity of electricity that is equivalent to one mole of electrons and that can deposit or liberate one mole of a univalent ion. It is the product of electron charge and Avogadro constant.
