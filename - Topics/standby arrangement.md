## Source [^1]
- An arrangement by central banks to lend one another reserves if necessary to resist speculative pressure on their exchange rates. The existence of standby arrangements between a group of central banks decreases the total of foreign exchange reserves that they need to hold between them, if speculative capital movements are likely to occur between their currencies.
