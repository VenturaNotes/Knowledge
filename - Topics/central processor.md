## Source [^1]
- (CPU, central processing unit) The principal operating part of a computer. It is usually defined as the ALU (arithmetic and logic unit) and the control unit (CU). It must be joined to a primary memory to form the processor-memory pair of the basic von Neumann machine.
## Source[^2]
- n. 
- (1) Another name for a central processing unit. 
- (2) In cognition, a hypothetical construct analogous to the central processing unit of a computer that receives information, allocates resources to subsystems, takes decisions, and controls voluntary behavior. See also FILTER THEORY.
