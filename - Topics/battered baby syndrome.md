## Source [^1]
- battered baby syndrome $n$. A term coined by the German-born US paediatrician C(harles) Henry Kempe (1922-84) in an article in the Journal of the American Medical Association in 1962 to denote the pattern of physical and psychological injuries inflicted on a baby by intentional neglect or repeated excessive beating by a parent or carer. See also battered CHILD SYNDROME, CHILD ABUSE. Compare BATTERED WIFE SYNDROME.
