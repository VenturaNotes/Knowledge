## Source [^1]
- $n$. Any of three distinct visual pigments in the normal (non-color-blind) human retina that are responsible for color vision and that have peak sensitivities at approximately 430 nanometres, 530 nm , and 560 nm , usually called blue, green, and red pigments, respectively, although light of these wavelengths actually looks violet, blue-green, and yellowish-green. See also IODOPSIN. Compare RHODOPSIN.
