## Source [^1]
- An anode is the electrode in a polarized electrical device through which current flows in from an outside circuit.
## Source[^2]
- The positive electrode of an electrolytic cell, discharge tube, valve, or solid-state rectifier; the electrode by which electrons leave (and conventional current enters) a system. Compare CATHODE.
