## Source [^1]
- $n$. Any theory of attention according to which information that is not attended to is processed, though at a shallower level of processing than the information receiving attention. The mechanism that implements this process is called an attenuating filter. The theory was formulated in 1960 by the US-based English psychologist Anne (Marie) Treisman (born 1935). See also Cocktail Party phenomenon, filter theory, SELECTIVE ATTENTION. Compare BOTTLENECK THEORY. \[From Latin attenuare to make thin or to weaken, from tenuis thin]
