## Source [^1]
- In growth theory, a characterization of the optimal, or welfare-maximizing, growth path for the economy. A turnpike was the name given to roads in medieval England. It was derived from the fact that access to the road was controlled by soldiers with pikes (a form of spear) which were turned aside to allow a traveller to progress; the name was adopted for major highways in the US that provided the quickest route between cities.
