## Source [^1]
- $n$. a variety of white blood cell, 16-20 $\mu \mathrm{m}$ in diameter, that has a kidney-shaped nucleus and greyish-blue cytoplasm (when treated with Romanowsky stains). Its function is the ingestion of foreign particles, such as bacteria and tissue debris. There are normally $0.2-0.8 \times 10^{9}$ monocytes per litre of blood. $\textemdash$monocytic adj.
