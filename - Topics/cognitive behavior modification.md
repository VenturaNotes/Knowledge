## Source [^1]
- $n$. A form of psychotherapy based on cognitive therapy and behavior modification, in which the client or patient learns to replace dysfunctional self-speech (such as I knew I'd never be able to cope with this job) with adaptive alternatives (The job's not going well, but I am capable of working out a plan to overcome the problems). Its applications include anger control, stress management, coping with anxiety, and developing social skills. US cognitive behavior modification. Also called cognitive behaviour therapy (CBT ) or cognitive behavioural therapy . See also ACCEPTANCE AND COMMITMENT THERAPY, DIALECTICAL BEHAVIOUR THERAPY, MINDFULNESS-BASED COGNITIVE THERAPY, THOUGHT STOPPING. Compare BEHAVIOUR THERAPY. CBM abbrev.
