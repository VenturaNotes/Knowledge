## Source [^1]
- $n$. Any method of psychophysical scaling in which an observer adjusts the differences between stimuli, or chooses stimuli from a set, so that the differences between the magnitudes of the stimuli appear equal, thereby producing an interval scale. A Thurstone scale is a special form of equal-appearing interval scale. See also METHOD OF BISECTION.
