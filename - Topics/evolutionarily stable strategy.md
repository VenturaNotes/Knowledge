## Source [^1]
- $n$. Any hereditary pattern of behavior that is fixed in the sense that, in a population in which most individuals adopt it, no alternative behavior pattern has a greater Darwinian fitness, and therefore none is favored over it by natural selection. The concept was introduced in 1973 by the English biologist John Maynard Smith (1920-2004) and the US physicist and chemist George R(obert) Price (1922-75). Often mistakenly written evolutionary stable strategy. See also Hawk-Dove game, Hawk-Dove-Retaliator game, strategy (2). ESS abbrev.
