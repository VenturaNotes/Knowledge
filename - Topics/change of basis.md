## Source [^1]
- (change of basis matrix) Given a linear map $T: V \to W$ between vector spaces, and a choice of bases $\mathcal{V}$ for $V$ and $\mathcal{W}$ for $W$, then the matrix of the linear map $T$ sends the coordinate vector of $v \in V$ to the coordinate vector of $Tv$ in $W$. Denote this matrix as ${}_{\mathcal{W}}T_{\mathcal{V}}$. If $\mathcal{V}'$ and $\mathcal{W}'$ are two other bases, then ${}_{\mathcal{W}'}T_{\mathcal{V}'} = ({}_{\mathcal{W}'}I_{\mathcal{W}}) ({}_{\mathcal{W}}T_{\mathcal{V}}) ({}_{\mathcal{V}}I_{\mathcal{V}'})$, where $I$ denotes the identity map (on $V$ or $W$). The matrices ${}_{\mathcal{W}'}I_{\mathcal{W}}$ and ${}_{\mathcal{V}}I_{\mathcal{V}'}$ are referred to as change of basis matrices. Respectively, they change the coordinates of a vector with respect to $\mathcal{W}$ (or $\mathcal{V}'$) to the coordinates of the same vector with respect to $\mathcal{W}'$ (or $\mathcal{V}$).
