## Source [^1]
- (EBD) A vehicle braking system that adjusts the braking force individually for each wheel to compensate for dynamic changes in wheel speed, slippage, and weight loading. It operates when the driver is braking the car, but can also come into play if it detects either understeer or oversteer, in which case applying braking force to an appropriately chosen single wheel can restore correct steering behaviour. See also ANTI-LOCK BRAKING.
