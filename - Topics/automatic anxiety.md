## Source [^1]
- $n$. In psychoanalysis, an infant's psychological feeling of helplessness, arising from biological helplessness, experienced as a form of anxiety, or a reproduction of such anxiety in an older child or an adult when confronted with a situation with which it is difficult to cope. The concept was introduced by Sigmund Freud (1856-1939) in his book Inhibitions, Symptoms and Anxiety (1926, Standard Edition, XX, pp. 77-175, at p. 138). See also anxiety.
