## Source [^1]
- A motor nerve responsible for suppling one of the extraocular muscles of the eye: the lateral rectus muscle
## Source[^2]
- $n$. Either of the sixth pair of cranial nerves controlling the lateral rectus muscles of the eyes, turning the eye outwards for a sidelong direction of gaze. Paralysis of this nerve causes convergent squint of the affected eye. Also called the pathetic nerve. \[From Latin abducere to lead away, from $a b$ away + ducere to lead or carry]
## Source[^3]
- the sixth cranial nerve (VI), which supplies the lateral rectus muscle of each eyeball, responsible for turning the eye outwards.
