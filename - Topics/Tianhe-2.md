## Source [^1]
- In 2014, the world's fastest supercomputer; it is situated at the National Super Computer Centre in China and was manufactured by the National University of Defence Technology. The first supercomputer to have more than 1 million cores (3 120000 Intel Xeon E-5 processors), it can reach a theoretical peak speed of 54902 teraflops, has 1024 TB of memory, and consumes nearly 18 MW of power. Its closest rival at the time of writing is the Titan Cray XK7, with 560,640 cores.
