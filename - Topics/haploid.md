## Source[^2]
- Describing a nucleus, cell, or organism with a single set of unpaired chromosomes. The haploid number is designated as $n$. Reproductive cells, formed as a result of meiosis, are haploid. Fusion of two such cells (see FERTILIZATION) restores the normal (diploid) number.
## Source[^3]
- adj. Of or relating to a cell that has a single set of unpaired chromosomes, as in the sex cells or gametes of humans and other sexually reproducing animals, rather than the full complement of paired chromosomes; also of or relating to an organism such as a fungus or a male bee whose body cells are haploid.
