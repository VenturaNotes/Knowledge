## Source [^1]
- Moving Picture Experts Group (MPEG) is a digital format that allows for the compression of video images. This compression can be done in a way that allows for the easy sending and downloading of MPEG files. Files can be exchanged over the internet while still maintaining their quality. These files can be used in a video or audio format.
## Source[^2]
- (Moving Picture Experts Group) 
- (1) The committee charged with the development of standards for coded representation of digital audio and video signal transmission. The standards as they are developed are accepted as ISO/IEC standards. These include: MPEG-1, the standard used for the coding of information on video CDs and in MP3 audio files; MPEG-2, the standard upon which digital television set top boxes and DVDs are based; MPEG-4, the standard for fixed and mobile web multimedia information transfer; and MPEG-7, the standard for the description and searching of audio and visual content of files, films, and audio tracks. The group has also developed MPEG-J, the framework for MPEG Java-based application programming interfaces, and is currently working on MPEG-21, a multimedia framework. 
- (2) A family of video compression algorithms widely used in digital broadcasting and storage.
