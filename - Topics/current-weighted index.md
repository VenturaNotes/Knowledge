## Source [^1]
- (Paasche index) A weighted average of prices or quantities, where the weights used are proportional to the quantities or prices of the most recent period. Where $p_{i j}$ and $q_{i j}$ are the prices and quantities of goods $i=1,2, \ldots, N$ in period $j, 0$ labels the base period, and $t$ the latest period, the current-weighted, or Paasche, price index is given by$$P_{c}=\Sigma_{i} p_{i t} q_{i t} / \Sigma_{i} p_{i o} q_{i t}$$and the current-weighted, or Paasche, quantity index is given by$$Q_{c}=\Sigma_{i} p_{i t} q_{i t} / \Sigma_{i} p_{i t} q_{i o}$$
