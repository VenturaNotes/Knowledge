## Source [^1]
- A period during which a company, in certain countries, is excused from paying corporation tax or profits tax (or pays them on only part of its profits) as an export incentive or an incentive to start up a new industry.
## Source[^2]
- A limited period of tax-free operation, or of specially reduced taxation. This may be used to induce foreign firms to invest in a country, or domestic firms to invest in an industry or area which the government especially wishes to encourage. A tax holiday scheme may be very expensive in terms of lost revenue if firms which take advantage of it would have invested in any case even without it.
