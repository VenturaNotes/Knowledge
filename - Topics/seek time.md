## Source [^1]
- The time taken for a particular track on a storage disk or drum to be located. The average seek time is defined as the sum of all single track seek times plus the sum of two track seek times and so on..., divided by the sum of all possible seeks. Typical average seek times for disks are in the range 4-12 milliseconds. See also ACCESS TIME, LATENCY.
## Source[^2]
- (1) The time it takes for the desired sector of a hard disk to rotate, and the drive's read-write head to move, so that the sector start is under the read-write head and the transfer of data to/from the sector can begin. 
- (2) More generally, the time it takes to access requested data on any storage medium.
