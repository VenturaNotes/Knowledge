## Source [^1]
- $n$. A higher-order factor arising from the Big Five personality factors, suggested by the US psychologist John M. Digman (1923-98) in an article in the Journal of Personality and Social Psychology in 1997. It combines extraversion and openness to experience, and Digman suggested that it may represent personal growth, or agency in the sense of striving for power and mastery. The US psychologist Colin G. DeYoung (born 1976) and others have suggested that it represents plasticity. See also alPHA PERSONALITY FACTOR.
