## Source [^1]
- (melanosis) $n$. an unusually pronounced darkening of body tissues caused by excessive production of the pigment melanin. For example, melanism may affect the hair, the skin (after sunburn, during pregnancy, or in Addison's disease), or the eye.
## Source[^2]
- Black coloration of the body caused by overproduction of the pigment melanin, often as a reaction to the environment. There are several species of melanic moths in industrially polluted areas (see INDUSTRIAL MELANISM) and the panther is a melanic form of leopard.
