## Source [^1]
- $n$. A tract of nerve fibres on each side of the hypothalamus involved in the control of hunger and predatory aggression, and functioning more generally as a major reward center through the action of the catecholamines dopamine and noradrenalin (norepinephrine). See also pleasure centre, quiet-biting attack. MFB abbrev. \[From Latin medialis situated in the middle, from medius the middle + English forebrain]
