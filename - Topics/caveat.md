## Source [^1]
- A proviso or qualification that limits liability by putting another party on notice. For example, a retailer may sell goods subject to the caveat that no guarantee of their suitability for a particular purpose is given. The purchaser in these circumstances has no remedy against the retailer should the goods in fact turn out to be unsuitable for that particular purpose.
