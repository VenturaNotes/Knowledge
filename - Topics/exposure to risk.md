## Source [^1]
- The extent to which lending institutions would lose if particular borrowers or classes of borrower were to default on their obligations. Exposure to risk can be kept down both by choosing relatively safe forms of investment, such as the government debt of countries considered unlikely to default, and by avoiding having too large a percentage of assets invested in the debt of any one borrower or class of borrower.
