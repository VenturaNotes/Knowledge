## Source [^1]
- PCI Express An interface for add-in cards released by Intel in 2004. It is intended to replace the accelerated graphics port and PCI standards. Each slot for add-in cards contains up to 32 full duplex serial links, called lanes. In version 1.1 each lane is capable of transferring 250 Mbytes per second; in version 3.0, introduced in 2010, this rate is increased to 985 Mbytes per second.
## Source[^2]
- (Peripheral Component Interconnect Express) The standard interface for add-in cards for personal computers. It involves slots mounted on the PC motherboard into which connectors on the edge of the card can fit. Slots come in four sizes, as do card-edge connectors, and a card will fit into any slot of its size or larger. The size denotes the number of independent parallel connections, or lanes, that the card or connector will support. The interface is designed for flexibility and high data-transfer speeds.
