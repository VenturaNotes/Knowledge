## Source [^1]
- $n$. An episode with characteristic cognitive and emotional features sometimes experienced by people close to death, typically involving a sensation of moving through a tunnel, feeling unnaturally calm, an out-of-body experience, and sometimes mystical or religious feelings. The term was introduced in 1975 by the US parapsychologist Raymond Moody (born 1944) in his book Life After Life: 'During the past few years I have encountered a large number of persons who were involved in what I shall call "near-death experiences"' (p. 18). NDE abbrev.
