## Source [^1]
- (image transfer coefficient) (symbol: $\theta$) A complex quantity, given by $(\alpha + \mathrm{j}\beta)$, of a two-port network terminated in its image impedances. It is half the natural logarithm of the complex ratio of the steady-state volt-amperes input to output of the network:$$\theta = \frac{1}{2} \log_{\mathrm{e}} \left(\frac{E_1 I_1}{E_2 I_2}\right)$$where $E_1, I_1$ and $E_2, I_2$ are the voltages and currents at the input and output terminals, respectively.
- The real part $(\alpha)$ of the image transfer constant is the image attenuation constant; the imaginary part $(\mathrm{j}\beta)$ is the image phase constant. Compare PROPAGATION COEFFICIENT.
