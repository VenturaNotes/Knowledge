## Source [^1]
- The use of bribery to influence the actions of a public official. More generally, corruption refers to obtaining private gains from public office through bribes, extortion, and embezzlement of public funds.
## Source[^2]
- (1) The introduction of errors into computer data through mechanical accident or malfunction. All forms of electronic or magnetic data storage are vulnerable to corruption, which can occur for no discernible reason. Corruption can also occur when data is sent over telephone lines between communicating computers. 
- (2) See BRIBERY.
