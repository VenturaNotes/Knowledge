## Source [^1]
- $n$. On a graph of points representing means, a line through a point indicating the margin of error (see illustration). Error bars on a line graph or histogram may indicate confidence intervals, standard deviations, or standard errors of the means, standard errors frequently being preferred because they provide a visual guide to statistical significance: if two SE error bars overlap, then the difference between the two means is non-significant (at $p<.05$ ), whereas if they do not overlap, then the difference is usually significant. Compare BOX-AND-WHISKER PLOT.
