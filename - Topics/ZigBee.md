## Source [^1]
- ZigBee is a wireless device that helps set up a low-rate wireless personal area network. It operates under the IEEE 802.15.4 protocols. The devices are meant to be used over a very small radius, usually no more than 100 meters, and devices using it must remain within line of sight. These devices do not consume extensive amounts of power. This makes the device ideal for setting up home or work networks.
## Source[^2]
- A specification for a wireless communication standard enabling low-power devices to link together and to relay messages from one to another. Because of the low-power requirement, it has a relatively low bandwidth of 250 kbits/s compared to standard Wi-fi or Bluetooth equipment. Key applications for ZigBee include personal area networks in homes (so-called 'smart homes'), wireless sensor networks, and industrial control.
