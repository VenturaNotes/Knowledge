## Source [^1]
- $n$. A field of applied psychology concerned with psychological aspects of physical health, including health promotion and the prevention and treatment of illness and the identification of psychological causes and correlates of health and illness. See also MEDICAL PSYCHOLOGY, OCCUPATIONAL HEALTH PSYCHOLOGY. A critical review of decades of research, published in the Annual Review of Psychology in 2014, concluded that happiness does not appear to be a cause of good health, nor are negative emotions such as worry or depression significant causes of ill health, but conscientiousness appears to be significantly linked to health and well-being across the lifespan.
