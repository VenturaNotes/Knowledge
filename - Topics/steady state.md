## Source [^1]
- In economics, a state of a dynamic economy where certain characteristics do not change over time. In neoclassical economics this is the state with a constant capital-labor ratio. In particular, this implies that per capita quantities of output and consumption are also constant, whereas the levels of capital stock, output, and consumption in the steady state grow at the rate of population growth.
## Source[^2]
- A state reached by a system under steady operating conditions after any transient effects resulting from a change in the operating conditions have died away.
