## Source [^1]
- $n$. one of a group of oral hypoglycaemic drugs, derived from a sulphonamide, that are used in the treatment of type 2 diabetes mellitus; they act by stimulating the islet cells in the pancreas to produce more insulin. The group includes glibenclamide, gliclazide, and tolbutamide. Side-effects, such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhoea, are usually mild.
