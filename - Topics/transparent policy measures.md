## Source [^1]
- Policy measures whose operation is open to public scrutiny. Transparency includes making it clear who is taking the decisions, what the measures are, who is gaining from them, and who is paying for them. The setting of the official UK interest rate by the Monetary Policy Committee is a transparent policy: the minutes of the meetings and the voting patterns are publicly released.
