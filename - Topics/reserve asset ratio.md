## Source [^1]
- A required minimum proportion between a bank's reserve assets and its deposits and other liabilities. A reserve asset ratio may be imposed or varied as part of a central bank's monetary policy, to enable it to control total bank lending by using open market operations to control the amount of reserve assets, or as part of the regulations imposed to safeguard the solvency of the banking system.
