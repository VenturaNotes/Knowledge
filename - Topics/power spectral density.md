## Source [^1]
- (power spectral density) A spectrum that gives the proportion of a signal's total power that is contained in each of its frequency components. Its usefulness is a consequence of Parseval's theorem, which enables calculations of power by squaring the voltage amplitude in either the time domain or the frequency domain. Knowing the power spectral density of both desired signals and noise is important in designing filters that will preserve the former while reducing the latter.
