## Source [^1]
- $n$. Developed skill, competence, or power to do something, especially (in psychology) existing capacity to perform some function, whether physical, mental, or a combination of the two, without further education or training, contrasted with capacity, which is latent ability. See also cognitive ability. Compare aptitude. \[From Latin habilitas ability, from habilis able, from habere to have or to hold]
