## Source [^1]
- (1) (GBP) The standard monetary unit of the UK, divided into 100 pence. It dates back to the 8th century AD when Offa, King of Mercia, coined 240 pennyweights of silver from 1 pound of silver. When sterling was decimalized in 1971 it was divided into 100 newly defined pence. The pound is also the currency unit of the Falkland Islands, Gibraltar, the Channel Islands, and the Isle of Man. See also Punt. 
- (2) The standard monetary unit of Egypt (EGP), Lebanon (LBP), South Sudan (SSP), Sudan (SDG), and Syria (SYP), divided into 100 piastres. 
- (3) Formerly, the standard monetary unit of Cyprus, divided into 100 cents. Cyprus adopted the euro in 2008.
