## Source [^1]
- The least distance, for a specified operating frequency, at which radiowaves are received in a given direction from the transmitter by reflection from the ionosphere. Reflection from the sporadic E-layer is customarily ignored. The area surrounding a particular transmitter swept out by a complete rotation of a radius vector equal in length to the skip distance is known as the skip area. This area is not necessarily circular as the skip distance is not always the same in all directions.
