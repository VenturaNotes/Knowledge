## Source [^1]
- An employee of a firm, who works in a factory or office and is the representative of a trade union. The shop steward is responsible for negotiating with the employers on behalf of the other members of the same union in the factory or office.
## Source[^2]
- A worker elected at shop-floor level to represent fellow-workers in discussions with management. Shop stewards are not trade-union officials, though they are usually union members. They can catch industrial relations problems early and help to prevent them from escalating into industrial disputes.
