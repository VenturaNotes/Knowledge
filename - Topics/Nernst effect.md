## Source [^1]
- If a conductor or semiconductor is placed in a magnetic flux density and a temperature gradient maintained across it at right angles to the magnetic flux density, an e.m.f. is developed in the material orthogonal to both the magnetic flux density and the temperature gradient. This is the converse of the Ettinghausen effect and is related to the Righi–Leduc effect.
