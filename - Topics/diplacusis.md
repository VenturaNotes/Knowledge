## Source [^1]
- $n$. perception of a single sound as double owing to a defect of the cochlea in the inner ear.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. A hearing impairment arising from a difference between the two ears, resulting in hearing a single tone as two tones of different pitch. Also spelt diplacousis . Also called diplacusia or diplacousia. \[From Greek diploos double + akusis hearing, from akouein to hear + -sis indicating a condition or state]
