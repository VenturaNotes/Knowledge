## Source [^1]
- $n$. Features of language that refer to personal, locational, or temporal characteristics of the situation in which an utterance occurs and whose meaning is therefore relative to the situation, such as this/that, here/there, now/then, I/you. The term is also sometimes applied to words that refer backwards or forwards in discourse, namely anaphora and cataphora. deictic adj. n. (Of or relating to) a verbal expression exhibiting deixis. \[From Greek deiknynai to show]
