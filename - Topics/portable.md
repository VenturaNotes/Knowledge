## Source [^1]
- (1) Another word for machine-independent. 
- (2) A word applied to software that can readily be transferred to other machines, although not actually machine-independent. 
- (3) A computer that can be simply carried from one place to another by one person. They cannot necessarily be used in transit. Examples include laptop computers.
## Source[^2]
- adj. (in radiography) referring to images obtained by the bedside of the patient using light-weight equipment, rather than in the radiography department. The quality of portable radiographs is usually limited due to compromises in the equipment design to keep it light and movable, which is necessary when patients cannot easily be moved (for example, on intensive therapy units).
