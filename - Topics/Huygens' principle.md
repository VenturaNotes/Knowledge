## Source [^1]
- A principle that explains, for example, diffraction, a phenomenon by which waves traveling in straight lines bend around corners; it is named after the Dutch astronomer Christian Huygens. It states that each point on a wavefront can be considered as a source that generates further waves which propagate from the source in all directions. The concept is important in the understanding of radio reception in built-up and mountainous areas.
