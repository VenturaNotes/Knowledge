## Source [^1]
- (equity share capital) 
- (1) Generally, the part of the share capital of a company owned by ordinary shareholders. For certain purposes, such as pre-emption rights, other classes of shareholders may be deemed to share in the equity capital and therefore be entitled to share in the profits of the company or any surplus assets on winding up. See also A SHARES. 
- (2) The market value of a company's issued ordinary shares.
