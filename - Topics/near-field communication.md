## Source [^1]
- (NFC) Wireless technology that enables two devices to communicate when within 4 cm of each other. The devices require loop antennae and use electromagnetic induction to connect. They operate at a frequency of 13.56 MHz and can transfer data at rates from 106k to 424k bits/second. The main applications are in contactless payments systems and access control. Most smartphones are now equipped with NFC capabilities and can run applications that emulate NFC cards.
