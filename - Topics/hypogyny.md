## Source [^1]
- A floral arrangement in which the ovary is superior, i.e. it arises from the receptacle above the sepals, petals, and stamens (see illustration). The perianth and stamens are said to be hypogynous with respect to the ovary, as seen in the tulip. Compare EPIGYNY; PERIGYNY.
