## Source [^1]
- $n$. A form of speech act that does not convey information like a statement but functions as an action in itself. For example, a request, a threat, a welcome, a warning, or an apology cannot be considered as true or false like a statement, but such utterances none the less bring about new psychological or social realities$\textemdash$saying is equivalent to doing. Compare LOCUTIONARY ACT, PERLOCUTIONARY ACT. \[From in- into + Latin locutio an utterance, from loqui to speak]
