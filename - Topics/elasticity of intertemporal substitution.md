## Source [^1]
- A measure of the willingness of a consumer to move consumption between time periods. The elasticity of intertemporal substitution, $\varepsilon_{s}$, is defined by$$\varepsilon_{s}=-\left(\frac{d\left(c_{i} / c_{j}\right)}{c_{i} / c_{j}}\right) /\left(\frac{d\left(U_{c_{i}} / U_{c_{j}}\right)}{U_{c_{i}} / U_{c_{j}}}\right)=-\frac{d \ln \left(c_{i} / c_{j}\right)}{d \ln \left(U_{c_{i}} / U_{c_{j}}\right)}$$where $c_{i}$ denotes consumption in time period $i$ and $U_{c_i}$ is the marginal utility of consumption in period $i$.
