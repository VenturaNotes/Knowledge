## Source [^1]
- The integration of a function of two inputs with respect to both of those inputs. If $f$ is a function of $x$ and $y$, and $R$ is a two-dimensional region defined by a range of values for $x$ and $y$, then the double integral of $f$ over region $R$ is written as$$\iint_{R} f(x, y) \, dx\,dy$$
	- #question Which integral is the $R$ the lower-bound for?
- A double integral is not the same in general as two single integrals, although for many well-behaved functions they give the same result. The notion can be generalized to functions with more than two inputs, such as the triple integral for functions of the form $f(x,y,z)$.
