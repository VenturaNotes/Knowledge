## Source [^1]
- Let $\circ$ be a binary operation on a set $S$. The cancellation laws are said to hold if, for all $a, b$, and $c$ in $S$,
	- (i) if $a \circ b=a \circ c$, then $b=c$,
	- (ii) if $b \circ a=c \circ a$, then $b=c$.
- It can be shown, for example, that in a group the cancellation laws hold.
