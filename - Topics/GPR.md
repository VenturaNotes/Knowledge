## Source [^1]
- (ground-penetrating radar) Radar designed to send pulses through the ground in order to detect the presence of layers or objects whose composition differs from that of their surroundings. The radio frequencies used are in the 16 Mhz to 2.6 Ghz range; while lower frequencies can penetrate as deep as 30 m, the higher frequencies can resolve much smaller objects. Applications range over detecting rebar in concrete, archaeological surveying, and geological profiling.
