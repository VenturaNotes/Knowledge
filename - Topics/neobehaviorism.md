## Source [^1]
- $n$. Any form of behaviorism coming after that of the founding father, the US psychologist John B(roadus) Watson (1878-1958), the most influential being that of the US psychologist B(urrhus) F(rederic) Skinner (1904-90). Most neobehaviorists, while agreeing with Watson that psychology should be restricted to the study of overt behavior, have accepted the existence of hidden psychological processes that can be inferred from behavior. US neobehaviorism. neobehaviorist or US neobehaviorist adj. $n$. (Of or relating to) one who advocates or practises neobehaviorism.
