## Source [^1]
- $n$. the female counterpart of the penis, which contains erectile tissue (see CORPUS CAVERNOSUM) but is unconnected with the urethra. Like the penis it becomes erect under conditions of sexual stimulation, to which it is very sensitive.
## Source[^2]
- An erectile rod of tissue in female mammals (and also some reptiles and birds) that is the equivalent of the male penis. It lies in front of the urethra and vagina.
## Source[^3]
- $n$. An elongated erotically sensitive erectile organ visible near the front of a woman's vulva and extending far into her body. It is much larger than is generally realized and as is shown in standard diagrams, and it is homologous with the male penis.
