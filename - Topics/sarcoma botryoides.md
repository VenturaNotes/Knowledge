## Source [^1]
- the most common tumor of the cervix and vagina in children and adolescents under the age of 16; $90 \%$ occur in children under five years. Symptoms are vaginal bleeding and a bloody discharge; in young girls the tumor may protrude from the cervix. It is a highly malignant rhabdomyosarcoma with the appearance of a bunch of grapes. Treatment is with triple chemotherapy with or without radiotherapy prior to hysterectomy and vaginectomy.
