## Source[^2]
- The theory of heredity that forms the basis of classical genetics, proposed by Gregor Mendel in 1866 and formulated in two laws (see MENDEL's LAWS; PARTICULATE inHERITANCE). Mendel suggested that individual characteristics were determined by inherited 'factors', and when improved microscopes revealed details of cell structure the behavior of Mendel's factors could be related to the behavior of chromosomes during meiosis.
