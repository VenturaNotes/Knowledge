## Source [^1]
- A needle-shaped crystal of insoluble calcium oxalate found in certain plants as a means of defence against browsing animals. The raphides occur in specialized cells (idioblasts) and in some cases may be forcefully expelled when the cell is disrupted, causing irritation to the mouth and digestive tract of the consumer.
