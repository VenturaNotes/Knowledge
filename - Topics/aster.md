## Source [^1]
- $n$. a star-shaped object in a cell that surrounds the centrosome during mitosis and meiosis and is concerned with the formation of the spindle.
## Source[^2]
- A starlike arrangement of microtubules radiating from a centrosome. Asters become conspicuous in animal cells at the ends of the spindle when cell division starts. The astral microtubules help to locate the spindle in relation to the cell's boundaries and to position the cleavage furrow of the cytoplasm when nuclear division is completed.
