## Source [^1]
- n. 
- (1) the slow injection of a substance, usually into a vein (intravenous (IV) infusion). This is a common method for replacing water, electrolytes, and blood products and is also used for the continuous administration of drugs (e.g. antibiotics, painkillers) or nutrition. See also DRIP. 
- (2) the process whereby the active principles are extracted from plant material by steeping it in water that has been heated to boiling point (as in the making of tea). 
- (3) the solution produced by this process.
