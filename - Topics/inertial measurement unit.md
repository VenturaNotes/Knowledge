## Source [^1]
- (IMU) A sensor system that measures acceleration and rotation by using a combination of gyroscopic sensors and accelerometers, usually having three of each. IMUs are chiefly used in inertial navigational systems designed to determine global location and velocity. As all their sensors can be of solid-state manufacture, they are commonly found in smartphones and tablet computers. They are also used in various gaming sensors, and to support automatic stabilization for consumer and commercial drones.
