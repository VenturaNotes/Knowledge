## Source [^1]
- $n$. an interventional radiology technique in which a sclerosant is used to obliterate vascular malformations, recurrent cysts, or varicose veins. This is a substance that damages the lining of the structure into which it is injected, hardening the tissue and eventually obliterating the vessel or cavity. Sodium tetradecyl sulphate, alcohol, ethanolamine, bleomycin, and poldocanol are commonly used sclerosants.
