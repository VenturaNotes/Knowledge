## Source [^1]
- (SLAR) A radar mounted on an aircraft so that it looks sideways (left or right), rather than straight ahead or behind. It is typically also angled downwards so that it scans the ground off to one side. SLAR has the advantage of working through clouds, and its main application is ground surveying. See also SYNTHETIC APERTURE RADAR.
