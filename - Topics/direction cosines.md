## Source [^1]
- In 3-dimensional space, a direction can be specified as follows. Take a point $P$ such that $\overset\longrightarrow{OP}$ has the given direction and $|OP| = 1$. Let $\alpha, \beta \text{ and } \gamma$ be the three angles $\angle xOP, \angle yOP \text{ and } \angle z OP$, measured in radians with $0 \le \alpha, \beta, \gamma \le \pi$. Then $\cos \alpha, \cos \beta, \text{ and } \cos \gamma$ are the direction cosines of $\overset\longrightarrow{OP}$. Point $P$ has coordinates $(\cos \alpha, \cos \beta, \cos \gamma)$, and so $\cos^2\alpha + \cos^2\beta + \cos^2\gamma = 1$.
