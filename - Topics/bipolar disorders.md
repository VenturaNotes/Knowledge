## Source [^1]
- $n$. A class of mood disorders characterized by manic episodes or mixed episodes and usually, but not necessarily, also major depressive episodes. Some classifications, including DSM-5, also distinguish bipolar I disorders, characterized by major depressive episodes together with manic episodes, from bipolar II disorders with major depressive episodes together with hypomanic episodes rather than manic episodes. The third major form of bipolar disorder is cyclothymic disorder. Bipolar disorders are also called manic-depressive psychoses . See also antimanic, Borna disease virus, mood stabilizer. Compare cyclothymia, DISRUPTIVE MOOD DYSREGULATION DISORDER. \[From Latin bis twice + polaris of or relating to a pole, from polus a pole]
