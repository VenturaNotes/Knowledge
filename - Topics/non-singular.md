## Source [^1]
- (curve) A curve (or geometric object more generally) is non-singular if it has no singular points. So a parabola is non-singular, but the curve with equation $y^2 = x^3$ is not, having a singular point (a cusp) at the origin.
---
- (matrix) A square matrix $\mathbf{A}$ is non-singular if it is not singular; that is, if $\det \mathbf{A} \neq 0$, where $\det \mathbf{A}$ is the determinant of $\mathbf{A}$. See also INVERSE MATRIX.
