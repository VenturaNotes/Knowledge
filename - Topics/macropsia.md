## Source [^1]
- $n$. a condition in which objects appear larger than they really are. It is usually due to disease of the retina affecting the macula but may also occur in spasm of accommodation.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. Visual perception of objects as larger than they really are, usually resulting from damage to the retinas, epilepsy, or a somatic symptom disorder. Also called macropsy. Compare micropsia. \[From Greek makros large + opsis sight, from ops an eye + -ia indicating a condition or quality]
