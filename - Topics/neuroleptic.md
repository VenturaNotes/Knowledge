## Source [^1]
- $n$. 
- (1) Any of the dopamine antagonist drugs, including any of the phenothiazines, butyrophenones, dibenzodiazepines, or thioxanthenes, that are used in the treatment of psychoses. Also called an antipsychotic drug or a major tranquillizer. See also atypical ANTIPSYCHOTIC, FENTANYL, NEUROLEPTIC-INDUCED. adj. 
- (2) Capable of affecting the brain or nervous system, especially by reducing the activity of neurons. \[Adopted at the Second International Congress of Psychiatry in 1957, from Greek neuron a nerve + lepsis a taking hold or seizure + -ikos of, relating to, or resembling]
