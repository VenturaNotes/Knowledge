## Source [^1]
- A technique for giving three-dimensional coordinates of a point based on its distance from a fixed origin and two angles relative to two perpendicular axes through that origin. The radial distance coordinate $r$ can be interpreted as a move up the vertical $z$-axis, which is then followed by a rotation by polar angle $\theta$ down towards the horizontal $x$-axis, and finally a rotation by the azimuth angle $\varphi$ about the $z$-axis towards the $y$-axis. There is no fixed convention as to the Greek symbols used for the two angles, so care is needed when using them. See also CYLINDRICAL POLAR COORDINATES; POLAR COORDINATES.
