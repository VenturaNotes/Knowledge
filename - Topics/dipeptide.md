## Source [^1]
- $n$. a compound consisting of two amino acids joined together by a peptide bond (e.g. glycylalanine, a combination of the amino acids glycine and alanine). See DIPEPTIDASE.
## Source[^2]
- A compound consisting of two amino acid units joined at the amino $\left(-\mathrm{NH}_{2}\right)$ end of one and the carboxyl $(-\mathrm{COOH})$ end of the other. This peptide bond (see PEPTIDE) is formed by a condensation reaction that involves the removal of one molecule of water.
