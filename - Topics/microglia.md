## Source [^1]
- $n$. one of the two basic classes of glia (the non-nervous cells of the central nervous system), having a mainly scavenging function (see MACROPHAGE). Compare MACROGLIA.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. A type of neuroglial cell found in the central nervous system, more abundant in grey matter than in white matter, functioning as a phagocyte, scavenging dead or damaged cells or encircling them, and proliferating when the brain is injured or infected. See also Glia. Compare macrogla. \[From Greek mikros small + glia glue]
