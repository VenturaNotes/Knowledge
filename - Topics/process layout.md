## Source [^1]
- (process-oriented layout) A factory, office, or other workplace layout in which work stations and transforming resources are grouped together. The resources to be transformed move from process to process and each product or service may follow a completely unique route, according to its needs. Typical process layouts include small engineering workshops that produce medium to high volumes of similar items. See also BATCH PRODUCTION; PROCESS CHOICE. Compare PRODUCT LAYOUT.
