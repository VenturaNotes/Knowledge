## Source [^1]
- $n$. any tumor of non-nervous cells (glia) in the nervous system. The term is sometimes also used for all tumors that arise in the central nervous system, including astrocytomas and glioblastomas. Tumors of low-grade malignancy produce symptoms by pressure on surrounding structures; those of high-grade malignancy may be infiltrative.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. A tumor affecting the neuroglia (usually astrocytes) in the brain or spinal cord. Symptoms include headache, drowsiness, and vomiting, together with other site-dependent signs, such as motor weakness on one side of the body caused by a cerebral glioma on the opposite side, or clumsiness on one side caused by a cerebellar glioma on the same side. Compare Glioblastoma. \[From Greek glia glue + -oma indicating a tumor]
