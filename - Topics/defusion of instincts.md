## Source [^1]
- $n$. In psychoanalysis, the separation of Eros and Thanatos, so that they pursue independent instinctual aims. Sigmund Freud (1856-1939), in his book The Ego and the Id (1923), cited the ambivalence of obsessional neurosis (obsessive-compulsive disorder) as one of the clearest examples of this phenomenon (Standard Edition, XIX, pp. 12-66, at p. 42). Compare FUSION OF INSTINCTS.
