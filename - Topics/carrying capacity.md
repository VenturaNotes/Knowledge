## Source[^2]
- Symbol $K$. The maximum population of a particular species that can be supported indefinitely by a given habitat or area without damage to the environment. It can be manipulated by human intervention. For example, the carrying capacity for grazing mammals could be increased by boosting the yield of their grassland habitat by the application of fertilizer. See also K SELECTION.
## Source[^3]
- $n$. In ecology (1) and the theory of evolution, the maximum number of individuals that a habitat or area can support indefinitely, the limit usually being determined by the available food supply.
