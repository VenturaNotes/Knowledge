## Source [^1]
- (Poynting vector) (symbol: $S$ ) Of an electromagnetic wave, the power passing through unit area carried by the wave. If the electric and magnetic fields in the wave are $E$ and $H$ respectively (both vector quantities), then the power density is equal to the vector product of the fields:$$S = E \times H$$It is measured in watts per square metre. For a harmonic wave (with $E$ and $H$ both harmonic), a more often quoted value is the average power density, $S_{\mathrm{av}}$, which is given by$$S_{\mathrm{av}} = \frac{1}{2} \mathfrak{R} (E \times H^*)$$where $\mathfrak{R}$ indicates the real rather than imaginary value, and $H^*$ is the complex conjugate of $H$.
