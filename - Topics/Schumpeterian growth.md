## Source [^1]
- (endogenous growth) Economic growth that is driven by innovation and governed by a process of creative destruction, as described by the Austrian economist Joseph Schumpeter (1883-1950). The late 1980s and 1990s saw a major revival of interest in Schumpeter's ideas among economists, business people, and policy makers.
