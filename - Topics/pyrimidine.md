## Source [^1]
- $n$. a nitrogen-containing compound with a ring molecular structure. The commonest pyrimidines are cytosine, thymine, and uracil, which form the nucleotides of nucleic acids.
## Source[^2]
- An organic nitrogenous base, sparingly soluble in water, that gives rise to a group of biologically important derivatives, notably uracil, thymine, and cytosine, which occur in nucleotides and nucleic acids (DNA and RNA).
