## Source [^1]
- n. (pl. papillae) any small nipple-shaped protuberance. Several different kinds of papillae occur on the tongue, in association with the taste buds. The optic papilla is an alternative name for the optic disc.
## Source[^2]
- (pl. papillae) Any cone-shaped protuberance projecting from the surface of an organ or organism. Papillae occur, for example, on the tongue, in the kidneys, and, in plants, on the surface of many petals.
## Source[^3]
- $n$. A small nipple-shaped protuberance, including any of the lingual papillae situated chiefly within pits in the surface of the tongue but also on the soft palate, throat, pharynx, and insides of the cheeks, most papillae being studded with taste buds around their bases. Approximately 12 circumvallate papillae are concentrated at the back of the tongue, numerous fungiform papillae and filoform papillae in the middle and front of the tongue, and pairs of foliate papillae on the sides of the back part of the tongue, the only ones without taste buds being filoform papillae. papillae pl. \[From Latin papilla a nipple, diminutive of papula a pimple]
