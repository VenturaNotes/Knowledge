## Source [^1]
- Able to be known without experience, i.e. the truth or otherwise can be argued by logic from agreed definitions. For example, the probabilities of the score when tossing a fair die are deduced from the principle of equally likely outcomes

## Source[^2]
- Literally translated from Latin: from what is before. In the economic literature it is used to indicate that a claim is true on the basis of earlier reasoning rather than empirical evidence.
