## Source [^1]
- adj. In psychoanalysis, of, relating to, or characterized by strong emotional dependence on another person, exemplified by the behavior of an infant at the mother's breast. Sigmund Freud (1856-1939) introduced the concept in 1905 in his book Three Essays on the Theory of Sexuality (Standard Edition, VII, pp. 130-243, at pp. 181-5). See also anaclitic DEPRESSION, ANACLITIC OBJECT-CHOICE. anaclisis $n$. The state or condition of being anaclitic. \[From Greek anaklitos leaning up against, from ana up + klinein to lean + -ikos of or relating to]
