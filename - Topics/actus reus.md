## Source [^1]
- $n$. A legal term denoting a criminal act in contradistinction to a criminal intent and knowledge of the wrongness of the criminal act, which in turn is called mens rea. For a defendant to be convicted of a crime, both an actus reus and a mens rea must normally be proved. \[From Latin actus act + reus guilty]
