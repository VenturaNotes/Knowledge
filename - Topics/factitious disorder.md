## Source [^1]
- $n$. A mental disorder in DSM-5 characterized by a persistent pattern of feigned symptoms and/or self-inflicted injuries to simulate disorder, in the absence of a confirmed physical or mental condition, and without any economic or other external incentive for the behavior, the motive being to receive medical treatment, to gain admission to hospital, or to assume the sick role, multiple surgical operations often being eagerly sought. Also called Munchausen syndrome, pathomimicry, or (colloquially) hospital hopper syndrome. See also factitious disOnder IMPOSEd ON ANOTHER, GANSER SYNDROME. Compare BORDERLINE PERSONALITY DISORDER, COMPENSATION NEUROSIS, CONVERSION DISORDER, MALINGERING, PSEUDOLOGIA FANTASTICA, SOMATIC SYMPTOM DISORDER. \[From Latin facticius artificial or made, from facere to make]
