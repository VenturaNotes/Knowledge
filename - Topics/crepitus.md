## Source [^1]
- n. 
- (1) a crackling sound or grating feeling produced by bone rubbing on bone or roughened cartilage, detected by palpation on movement of an arthritic joint. Crepitus in the knee joint is a common sign of chondromalacia patellae in the young and osteoarthritis in the elderly. 
- (2) a similar sound heard with a stethoscope over an inflamed lung when the patient breathes in. 
- (3) a similar sound heard over an inflamed extensor tendon in the hand in scleroderma caused by thickening of the skin, or over a tendon injured by repetitive use in de Quervain's tendovaginitis.
