## Source [^1]
- $n$. An umbrella term for abnormal physiological or psychological reactions associated with sleep or the transition between wakefulness and sleep, causing significant distress or impairment in social, occupational, or other important areas of functioning. In DSM-5 they form a subcategory of sleep-wake disorders, including non-rapid eye movement sleep arousal disorder, nightmare disorder, rapid eye movement sleep behaviour disorder, and restless legs syndrome. See also BRUXISM, CENTRAL SLEEP APNOEA, OBSTRUCTIVE SLEEP APNOEA HYPOPNOEA, PAVOR DIURNUS, PAVOR NOCTURNUS, SEXSOMNIA, SLEEP APNOEA. Compare DYSSOMNIAS. \[From Greek para beside or beyond + somnus sleep]
