## Source [^1]
- (Latin) Short for ex post facto: after the event. This abbreviation is used, for example, to refer to the collection of financial data for transactions after they have been effected. Compare EX ANTE.
## Source[^2]
- Literally translated from Latin: from after. The value of a variable, or of a decision made, as it appears after the outcome of randomness has been realized, that is, what actually occurred. Ex post is contrasted with ex ante, which means looking at things before the event.
