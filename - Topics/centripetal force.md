## Source [^1]
- Suppose that a particle $P$ of mass $m$ is moving with constant speed $v$ in a circular path, with center at the origin $O$ and radius $r_0$. Let $P$ have polar coordinates $(r_0,\theta).$ (See circular motion.) Then $v=r_0\dot{\theta}$ and the acceleration of the particle is in the direction towards $O$ and has magnitude $r_0\dot{\theta^2}.$ It follows that, if $P$ is acted on by a force $\mathbf{F}$, this force is in the direction towards $O$ and has magnitude $mv^2/r_0$. It is called the centripetal force.
