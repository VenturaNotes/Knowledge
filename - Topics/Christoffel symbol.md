## Source [^1]
- For a parameterized surface $\mathbf{r}(u, v)$ the Christoffel symbols $\Gamma_{ij}^k$ are defined by$$\mathbf{r}_{uu} = \Gamma_{11}^1 \mathbf{r}_u + \Gamma_{11}^2 \mathbf{r}_v + Ln,$$$$\mathbf{r}_{uv} = \Gamma_{12}^1 \mathbf{r}_u + \Gamma_{12}^2 \mathbf{r}_v + Mn,$$$$\mathbf{r}_{vv} = \Gamma_{22}^1 \mathbf{r}_u + \Gamma_{22}^2 \mathbf{r}_v + Nn,$$where $\mathbf{n}$ denotes a unit normal and $L, M, N$ are the coefficients of the second fundamental form. The Christoffel symbols are intrinsic (and so may be expressed in terms of the first fundamental form), and a curve $\mathbf{r}(u(s), v(s))$ is a geodesic parametrized by arc length $s$ if$$\ddot{u} + \Gamma_{11}^1 \dot{u}^2 + 2\Gamma_{12}^1 \dot{u}\dot{v} + \Gamma_{22}^1 \dot{v}^2 = 0,$$$$\ddot{v} + \Gamma_{11}^2 \dot{u}^2 + 2\Gamma_{12}^2 \dot{u}\dot{v} + \Gamma_{22}^2 \dot{v}^2 = 0.$$
