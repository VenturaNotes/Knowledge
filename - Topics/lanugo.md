## Source [^1]
- $n$. fine hair covering the body and limbs of the human fetus. It is most profuse at about the 28th week of gestation and is shed around 40 weeks. Lanugo is often found in teratomas.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. Fine hair or down, especially the woolly covering of a human foetus at birth and the fluffy hair that often appears on the faces of people with anorexia nervosa. \[From Latin lanugo down, from lana wool]
