## Source [^1]
- A microscopic fibre. Plant cell walls contain microfibrils, about 5 nm in diameter, each consisting of some 50-60 parallel cellulose chains that are associated together to form a rod or a flat ribbon. Cellulose microfibrils are arranged in layers at right angles to each other.
