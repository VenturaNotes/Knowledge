## Source [^1]
- A unit of measurement for angles; one radian corresponds to the angle whose arc length on a unit circuit is itself 1. A single radian equals approximately 57.3 degrees and full rotation corresponds to $2\pi$ radians. It is the natural unit for angles, and the one required for the simplest forms of trigonometric equations to hold.
