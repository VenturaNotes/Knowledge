## Source [^1]
- $n$. insatiable overeating. This symptom may be psychogenic, occurring, for example, as a phase of anorexia nervosa (bulimia nervosa or the binge-purge syndrome); or it may be due to neurological causes, such as a lesion of the hypothalamus.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. 
- (1) Pathologically voracious appetite, including cases caused by a brain lesion. Also called hyperorexia, hyperphagia, or loosely speaking polyphagia.
- (2) A shortened name for bulimia nervosa. \[From Greek boulimia bulimia, from bous ox + limos hunger + -ia indicating a condition or quality, indicating the condition of someone who is hungry enough to eat an ox, not (as often stated) one who is as hungry as an ox, the ox being a creature not known for its voracity]
