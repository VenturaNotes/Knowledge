## Source [^1]
- The movement of charge carriers in a metal or semiconductor under the influence of an electric field. The resulting average velocity of the charge carriers is known as the drift velocity, $\nu$ , which is linearly related to the electric field by the parameter drift mobility, $\mu$:$$v = \mu E$$The drift current, $J$, is then the product of the carrier charge density and their average velocity:$$J = n e v = n e \mu E$$which is an expression of Ohm's law. The parameter $n e \mu$ is the conductivity of the material.
