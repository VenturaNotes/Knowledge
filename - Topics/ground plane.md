## Source [^1]
- (earth plane) A sheet of conducting material that is adjacent to a circuit. It may be used to provide a low-impedance earth at any point in the circuit. For example, one side of a double-sided printed circuit board may be used as the ground plane. Connections to it from the printed circuit on the other side of the board are easily effected through the board from any desired point in the circuit.
