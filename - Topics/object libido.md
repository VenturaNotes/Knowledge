## Source [^1]
- $n$. In psychoanalysis, libido involved in the cathexis of an external instinctual object, in contradistinction to ego libido, which is libido involved in the cathexis of oneself. According to Sigmund Freud (1856-1939), object libido decreases as ego libido increases, and vice versa. Freud expounded these ideas in 1914 in his article 'On Narcissism: An Introduction' (Standard Edition, XIV, pp. 73-102, at pp. 77-81). See also NARCISSISM.
