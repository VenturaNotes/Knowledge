## Source [^1]
- (DRO) A microwave transistor oscillator that uses a ceramic dielectric of high Q factor for the resonator element. Dielectric resonators (DRs) are low-loss, temperature-stable ceramics and resonate in various modes, depending upon the detailed composition and dimensions. The DR is usually a solid cylindrical puck, and in practice this is placed close to the microstrip transmission line to enable magnetic coupling between them; the DR behaves like a high-Q cavity resonator at the oscillation frequency.
