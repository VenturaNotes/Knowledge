## Source [^1]
- $n$. A philosophical method of enquiry, introduced in 1901 by the German philosopher Edmund (Gustav Albert) Husserl (1859-1938), that concentrates on the detailed description of conscious experience while suspending or bracketing all preconceptions, interpretations, and explanations; more generally a qualitative research method in psychology that concentrates on the analysis of mental experience rather than behavior. See also act psychology, Eigenwelt, epoche, existentialism, intentionality (1), introspectionism, Mitwelt, sociology of knowledge, Umwelt. \[From Greek phainomenon a thing shown, from phainein to show + logos word, discourse, or reason]
