## Source [^1]
- $n$. According to a doctrine propounded by the US historian and philosopher of science Thomas S(amuel) Kuhn (1922-96) in his book The Structure of Scientific Revolutions (1962), a rapid change, marking the culmination of a scientific revolution, from one paradigm (1) to another, such as occurred in physics when Einstein's theory replaced Newton's in 1916. See also Kuhnian, normal science.
