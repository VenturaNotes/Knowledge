## Source [^1]
- An amplifier that has two inputs and produces an output signal that is a function of the difference between the inputs. An ideal differential amplifier produces an output signal of zero when the inputs are identical. In practice a small positive or negative signal may occur. The common-mode rejection ratio is a measure of the ability of a differential amplifier to produce a zero output for like inputs.
