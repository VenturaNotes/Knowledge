## Source [^1]
- The impedance presented to the input terminals of an electronic circuit or device by any source of electrical energy. The source impedance of an ideal voltage source is zero (i.e. $\mathrm{d}V_{\mathrm{s}} / \mathrm{d}I_{\mathrm{s}} = 0$) whereas that of an ideal current source is infinity (i.e. $\mathrm{d}V_{\mathrm{s}} / \mathrm{d}I_{\mathrm{s}} = \infty$).
