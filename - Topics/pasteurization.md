## Source[^2]
- The treatment of milk to destroy disease-causing bacteria, such as those of tuberculosis, typhoid, and brucellosis. Milk is heated to $65^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$ for 30 minutes or to $72^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$ for 15 minutes followed by rapid cooling to below $10^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$. The method was devised by the French microbiologist Louis Pasteur (1822-95). See also FOOD PRESERVATION.
