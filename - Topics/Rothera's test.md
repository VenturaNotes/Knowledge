## Source [^1]
- a method of testing urine for the presence of acetone or acetoacetic acid: a sign of diabetes mellitus. Strong ammonia is added to a sample of urine saturated with ammonium sulphate crystals and containing a small quantity of sodium nitroprusside. A purple color confirms the presence of acetone or acetoacetic acid. \[A. C. H. Rothera (1880-1915), Australian biochemist]
