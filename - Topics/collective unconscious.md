## Source [^1]
- (in Jungian psychology) structures of the unconscious mind shared by all humans. Jung considered the collective unconscious to exist collectively amongst humans in addition to the personal unconscious mind postulated by Freud. He argued that the collective unconscious, populated by instincts and archetypes, had a profound influence on the lives of individuals through symbols, rituals, and shared narratives.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. In analytical psychology, a part of the unconscious (2) additional to the personal unconscious, containing memories, instincts, and experiences that are shared by all people. According to Carl Gustav Jung (1875-1961), these mental elements are inherited and often organized into archetypes (2), and they become manifest in dreams and in fairy tales, myths, religions, and other cultural phenomena. Also called the objective psyche, racial memory, or racial unconscious . See also ANIMA, ANIMUS (3), ARCHETYPAL FORM, INNATE IDEA, PSYCHOID.
