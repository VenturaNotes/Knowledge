## Source [^1]
- A method of restricting importation to a country, in which the importer is required to deposit a sum of money with the customs and excise authorities when goods are imported. It is sometimes used as an alternative to raising import duties.
## Source[^2]
- A requirement to place a blocked deposit in advance with the central bank as the condition for obtaining foreign currency to pay for imports. Such a deposit has the effect both of imposing a tax on imports, and of reducing the money supply in circulation.
