## Source [^1]
- A fertile male in a colony of social bees, especially the honeybee (Apis mellifera). The drones die after mating with the queen bee as the male reproductive organs explode within the female.
## Source[^2]
- An unmanned aircraft that is controlled over a radio link. Drones range from large winged aircraft used for surveying or military applications to small multiple-rotor helicopters used by hobbyists for both indoor and outdoor use. The growth in the recreational use of drones has led to aviation authorities introducing rules for their safe use, particularly near airports. See also REMOTELY OPERATED VEHICLE.
