## Source [^1]
- (1) A clause in a contract that restricts the freedom of one of the parties in some way. Employment contracts, for example, sometimes include a clause in which an employee agrees not to compete with the employer for a specified period after leaving the employment. Such clauses may not be enforceable in law. See RESTRAINT OF TRADE; RESTRICTIVE TRADE PRACTICES. 
- (2) A clause in a contract affecting the use of land. See COVENANT.
