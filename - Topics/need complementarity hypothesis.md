## Source [^1]
- $n$. The proposition that people tend to be attracted to others who have complementary needs to their own, a person with a high need for dominance, for example, being attracted to a person with a high need for submission, and vice versa. The theory was proposed in 1958 by the US sociologist Robert Francis Winch (1911-77) in a book entitled Mate-Selection: A Study of Complementary Needs. Despite its superficial plausibility, the theory has received only weak and inconsistent empirical support. Also called the complementarity hypothesis. Compare ATTITUDE SIMILARITY HYPOTHESIS.
