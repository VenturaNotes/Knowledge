## Source [^1]
- (balance method) An accurate method of measurement in which the quantity being measured, such as resistance or capacitance, is balanced by another of a similar kind: voltages in different circuits are adjusted so that the response of an indicating instrument falls to zero. The best known example of the null method is in the Wheatstone bridge although it is used in many other bridge circuits.
