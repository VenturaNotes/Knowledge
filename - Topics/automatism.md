## Source [^1]
- $n$. behavior that may be associated with epilepsy, in which the patient performs well-organized movements or tasks while unaware of doing so. The movements may be simple and repetitive, such as hand clapping and lip smacking, or they may be so complex as to mimic a person's normal conscious activities.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. Behavior executed without conscious awareness or control, as for example in sleepwalking, hypnosis, fugue states, and certain forms of epilepsy. Also called automaticity. See also dissociation THEORY.
