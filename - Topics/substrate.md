## Source [^1]
- $n$. the specific substance or substances on which a given enzyme acts. For example, starch is the substrate for salivary amylase; RNA is the substrate for ribonuclease.
## Source[^2]
- (1) (in biochemistry) The substance upon which an enzyme acts in biochemical reactions. 
- (2) (in biology) The material on which a sedentary organism (such as a barnacle or a plant) lives or grows. The substrate may provide nutrients for the organism or it may simply act as a support.
## Source[^3]
- A single body of material on or in which circuit elements or integrated circuits are fabricated. The substrate may be passive, as with a printed circuit board, or active, as with a bulk semiconductor.
