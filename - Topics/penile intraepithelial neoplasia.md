## Source [^1]
- (PIN) cellular changes affecting the glans, prepuce, or penile shaft that precede the invasive stages of cancer of the penis. There are three stages (PIN I, II, or III) based on the degree of dysplasia. The pathological features of PIN III indicate carcinoma in situ, known as erythroplasia of Queyrat and Bowen's disease.
