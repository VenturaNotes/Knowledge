## Source [^1]
- $n$. Amitriptyline hydrochloride, one of the most commonly prescribed of the tricyclic antidepressant drugs, used in the treatment of depression, and of anorexia nervosa and bulimia nervosa when accompanied by depression, and as an adjunctive treatment of neurogenic pain. It acts by blocking the reuptake mechanism of amine neurotransmitters, allowing them to accumulate at synapses (1) in the central nervous system. Also called Amitril, Elavil, Endep, Laroxyl, Saroten, and Triptafen (trademarks). Formula: $\mathrm{C}_{20} \mathrm{H}_{23} \mathrm{~N}$.
