## Source [^1]
- A number defining one end of an interval on the real line. Each of the bounded intervals $[a,b], (a,b), [a,b), \text { and } (a,b]$ has two end-points, $a$ and $b$. Each of the unbounded intervals $[a, \infty)$, $(a, \infty)$, $(-\infty, a]$, and $(-\infty, a)$ has one end-point, $a$.
