## Source [^1]
- A tax directly linked to the level of carbon dioxide $\left(\mathrm{CO}_{2}\right)$ emissions. This is an example of a Pigouvian tax designed to correct a negative externality, in this case the excess accumulation of $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ in the atmosphere which may have negative effects on the environment. In the UK the national tax on fossil fuels used in electricity generation, referred to as the carbon price support, was introduced in April 2013.
