## Source [^1]
- $n$. A therapeutic method based on cognitive behavior therapy, incorporating ideas associated with mindfulness, designed to prevent recurrence of episodes of major depressive disorder. The mindfulness element trains clients to notice automatic cognitive processes that have triggered depressive episodes in the past and to alter their reactions to be more reflective. It was developed by the Polish-born Canadian psychologist Zindel V(ictor) Segal (born 1956) and the British clinical psychologists J(ohn) Mark G(ruffydd) Williams (born 1952) and John D(ouglas) Teasdale (born 1944) and published in a book entitled Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy for Depression in 2002 and an article in the Journal of Consulting and Clinical Psychology in 2004. MBCT abbrev.
