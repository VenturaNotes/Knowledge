## Source [^1]
- The condition in which a heterozygous organism has greater fitness than the corresponding homozygotes. The hypothesis is that the heterozygous state leads to enhanced gene expression at a particular locus or set of loci relative to either homozygous alternative; this is proposed as an explanation of hybrid vigor.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. A phenomenon associated with certain heritable traits in which a heterozygote has a phenotype that is more extreme on some measurable dimension than either of the corresponding homozygotes. Also called superdominance. See also dominance (GENETIC). Compare filial REGRESSION, REGRESSION TOWARDS THE MEAN.
