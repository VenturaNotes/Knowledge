## Source [^1]
- $n$. A type of aftereffect that results from selective adaptation to specific stimulus features, the resultant effect being contingent on the presence in the visual field of one of these features, the classic example being the McCollough effect in which illusory colors are contingent on the presence of particular grating patterns. See also tilt AFTEREFFECT.
