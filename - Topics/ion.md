## Source [^1]
- When an atom has a charge, it's called an ion
	- Positively charged: [[Cation]] (fewer electrons)
	- Negatively charged: [[Anion]] (more electrons)
## Source[^2]
- $n$. an atom or group of atoms that has lost one or more electrons, making it electrically charged and therefore more chemically active. See ANION; CATION; ELECTROLYTE; IONIZATION.
## Source[^3]
- An atom or group of atoms that has either lost one or more electrons, making it positively charged (a cation), or gained one or more electrons, making it negatively charged (an anion).
