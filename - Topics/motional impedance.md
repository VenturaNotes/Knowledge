## Source [^1]
- (driving impedance; driving-point impedance) A component of the impedance of an electromechanical or acoustic transducer that results from the counter e.m.f. generated by the motion of the transducer. It is equal to the vector difference between the input impedance measured under specified load conditions and the blocked impedance.
