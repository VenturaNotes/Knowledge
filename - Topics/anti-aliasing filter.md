## Source [^1]
- A low-pass filter used to ensure that the maximum frequency of an analogue signal input to or output from a digital sampling system does not exceed half the sampling frequency being used, or the Nyquist frequency, in order to avoid aliasing. The cut-off frequency of an anti-aliasing filter is usually set to be just less than the Nyquist frequency.
