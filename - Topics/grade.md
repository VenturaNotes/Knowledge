## Source [^1]
- An angular measure which is one-hundredth of a right triangle
## Source[^2]
- n. 
- (1) the severity of a malignant tumour according to its degree of differentiation. Low-grade tumours (grade 1) closely resemble normal tissues, are well differentiated, and have a good prognosis. High-grade tumours (grade 3) show a poor resemblance to normal tissues, are poorly differentiated, and have a poor prognosis. Benign tumours are not graded. 
- (2) the severity of a non-neoplastic disease.
