## Source [^1]
- (epitaxial growth) Growth of a layer of one substance on a single crystal of another, such that the crystal structure in the layer is the same as that in the substrate. It is used in making semiconductor devices.
## Source[^2]
- (epitaxial growth) A method of growing a thin layer of material upon a single-crystal substrate, such as silicon, so that the crystal structure of the layer is identical to that of the substrate. The material, which may be the same as the substrate or a different one, is usually deposited from a gaseous mixture (see VAPOR PHASE EPITAXY). The technique is extensively used in semiconductor technology when a layer (the epitaxial layer) of different conductivity to the substrate is required. See also LIQUID PHASE EPITAXY; MOLECULAR BEAM EPITAXY.
