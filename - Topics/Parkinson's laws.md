## Source [^1]
- The propositions made by the British writer Cyril Northcote Parkinson (1909-93) in his book Parkinson's Law. Facetious but often confirmed in practice, they include such aphorisms as: work expands to fill the time available in which to do it; expenditure rises to meet income; and subordinates multiply at a fixed rate that is independent of the amount of work produced. They were devised with large organizations in mind and it is to such organizations that they principally apply$\textemdash$unless managers take steps to ensure that they do not. See also Dilbert PRINCIPLE; PETER PRINCIPLE.
