## Source [^1]
- (DFM) Design techniques that take into account the ease and/or cost with which a product can be manufactured. This recognizes that there is a trade-off between a design that gives some optimal performance but is very hard or expensive to construct and one that is easy to build but is perhaps suboptimal in other ways. Key aspects of DFM include modular design, the use of standard components, reducing the number of parts, and having multipurpose parts.
