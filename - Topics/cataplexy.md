## Source [^1]
- $n$. a sudden onset of muscle weakness that may be precipitated by excitement or emotion. There may be total loss of muscle tone, resulting in collapse, or simply jaw dropping or head nodding. It occurs in 60-90% of patients with narcolepsy.
## Source[^2]
- n. 
- (1) A sudden loss of muscle tonus, without loss of consciousness, leading to collapse, immobility, and a state closely resembling REM sleep without loss of consciousness, induced by laughter or strong emotions such as fear, anger, or surprise, sometimes confused with catalepsy. A propensity for cataplexy is genetic in origin, and strains of cataplexic dogs have been bred. Compare narcolepsy. 
- (2) A death-like state of immobility adopted by some animals to deter predators. cataplectic or cataplexic adj.
