## Source [^1]
- (near instantaneously compounded audio multiplex) A system used by the BBC and ITV whereby an extra digital code is added to the TV signal to carry digital stereo sound, so improving sound quality. To receive the signal, a TV or video cassette recorder has to have a NICAM decoder, and also a stereo amplifier and a pair of loudspeakers.
