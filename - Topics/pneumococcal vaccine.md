## Source [^1]
- a vaccine that protects against infection by the bacterium Streptococcus pneumoniae, which can cause serious diseases, including pneumonia, septicaemia, and meningitis. Children under 2 years of age and adults over 65 years are at particular risk. Since September 2006, the pneumococcal vaccine has become part of the childhood immunization programme. It is also offered to people over the age of 65 as a single vaccination that will protect for life.
