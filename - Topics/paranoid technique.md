## Source [^1]
- $n$. In psychoanalysis, one of the four defensive techniques proposed by the Scottish psychoanalyst W. Ronald D. Fairbairn (1889-1964). It involves internalization of the good object and externalization of the bad object, causing the paranoid person to feel persecuted by external forces. See also Splitting OF THE ObJect. Compare HYSTERICAL TECHNIQUE, OBSESSIONAL TECHNIQUE, PHOBIC TECHNIQUE.
