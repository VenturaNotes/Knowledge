## Source [^1]
- $n$. a condition in which pairs of homologous chromosomes fail to separate during meiosis or a chromosome fails to divide at anaphase of mitosis or meiosis. It results in a cell with an abnormal number of chromosomes (see MONOSOMY; TRISOMY).
## Source[^2]
- The phenomenon that occurs when a pair of homologous chromosomes do not separate in meiosis but migrate to the same pole of the cell, resulting in an uneven number of chromosomes being present in the daughter cells. Klinefelter's syndrome results from nondisjunction of the sex chromosomes.
