## Source [^1]
- If $a<b$ are two positive real numbers, let $a_{0}=a, b_{0}=$ $b$ and $a_{n+1}=\frac{a_{n}+b_{n}}{2}, b_{n+1}=\sqrt{a_{n} b_{n}}$ for $n \geq 0$. Then $\left(a_{n}\right)$ is increasing and $\left(b_{n}\right)$ is decreasing and $\lim _{n \rightarrow \infty}\left(a_{n}\right)=\lim _{n \rightarrow \infty}\left(b_{n}\right)$ is the arithmetic-geometric mean.
