## Source [^1]
- The velocity at which an equiphase surface of a traveling wave is propagated through a medium, i.e. the velocity at which the crests and troughs travel. It is equal to $\lambda / T$ where $\lambda$ is the wavelength and $T$ the period of the wave. If the frequency is $f$ and the number of wavelengths per unit distance is $\sigma$ (the wavenumber) then$$\frac{\lambda}{T} = \frac{f}{\sigma}$$
