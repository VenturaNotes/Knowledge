## Source [^1]
- The centralizer $C_{G}(a)$ of an element $a$ in a group $G$ is$$C_{G}(a)=\{g \in G: \mathrm{ga}=\mathrm{ag}\}$$It is a subgroup of $G$ and$$C_{G}(a) |\times|C(a)|=|G|$$where $C(a)$ denotes the conjugacy class of $a$. More generally, for a subset $S \subseteq G$, then$$C_{\mathrm{G}}(S)=\{g \in G: \mathrm{gs}=\mathrm{sg} \text { for all } s \in S\}$$Compare NORMALIZER.
