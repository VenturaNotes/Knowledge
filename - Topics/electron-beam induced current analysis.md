## Source [^1]
- (EBIC) A technique that is used to detect crystal defects in a semiconductor slice. The technique requires the presence of a p-n junction, Schottky barrier, or MOS capacitor to be successful. An electron beam is scanned across the sample, which leads to the generation of electron-hole pairs. The charges generated are collected by the diode and the resulting current detected. Any defect or inhomogeneity that affects the production or recombination of the electron-hole pairs will also affect the current detected. Use of a cathode-ray tube synchronized with the scanning electron beam produces an image of the area scanned. EBIC can be easily added to many scanning electron microscopes.
