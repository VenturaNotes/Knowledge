## Source [^1]
- (1) A market that emerged in the 1950s for financing international trade. Its principal participants are commercial banks, large companies, and central banks. Its main business is in eurobonds, eurocommercial paper, euronotes, and euroequities issued in eurocurrencies. The largest euromarket is in London, but there are smaller ones in Paris, Brussels, and Frankfurt. 
- (2) The European Union, regarded as one large market for goods.
