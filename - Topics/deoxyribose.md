## Source [^1]
- (2-deoxyribose) A pentose (five-carbon) sugar, a derivative of ribose, that is a component of the nucleotides (deoxyribonucleotides) that form the building blocks of DNA.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. Any of a group of sugars similar to ribose but having one of its alcoholic hydroxyl groups replaced by hydrogen, especially the sugar that forms part of the backbone of DNA and emerges as a product of the hydrolysis of DNA. \[From deoxy- indicating less oxygen + ribose]
