## Source [^1]
- For any set X, a permutation of X is a one-to-one onto mapping from X to X. The set of all of these, with composition as the operation, forms a group called the symmetric group of X, denoted Sym(X). If X has $n$ elements, there are $n!$ permutations of X and the symmetric group is denoted $S_n$. See alternating group, Cayley's Representation Theorem
