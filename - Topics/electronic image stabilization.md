## Source [^1]
- (EIS) A technique to reduce the effects of jitter or shaking during video capture; it works by comparing successive image frames and making small adjustments in the relative positions of features. The resolution of the video is reduced slightly, owing to the need to treat some of the image pixels as a buffer over which an image may need to be moved. See also IMAGE STABILIZATION.
