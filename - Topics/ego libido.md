## Source [^1]
- $n$. In psychoanalysis, libido involved in the cathexis of oneself, in contradistinction to object libido, which is libido involved in the cathexis of an external instinctual object. Sigmund Freud (1856-1939) discussed this distinction in 1914 in his article 'On Narcissism: An Introduction' (Standard Edition, XIV, pp. 73-102, at pp. 77-81), where he theorized that an energy balance exists, ego libido decreasing as object libido increases, and vice versa. Also called narcissistic libido. See also NARCISSISM.
