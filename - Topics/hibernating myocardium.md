## Source [^1]
- an area of heart muscle subject to critical coronary ischaemia sufficient to cause reversible impairment of function but insufficient to result in death of the muscle. Also known as viable myocardium, it is detected by MRI scanning of the heart. The clinical importance is that restoration of normal coronary blood flow may improve heart muscle contraction.
