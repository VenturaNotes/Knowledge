## Source [^1]
- (1) n. the recumbent position. 
- (2) adj. describing a radiograph taken with the patient lying on his or her side and the X-ray beam traveling horizontally. Such films reveal free gas in the peritoneal cavity following perforation of a hollow organ and fluid levels in the bowel when it is obstructed. They are commonly used in barium enema examinations. Compare ANTEROPOSTERIOR; POSTEROANTERIOR.
