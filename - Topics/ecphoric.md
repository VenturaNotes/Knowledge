## Source [^1]
- adj. Pertaining to a mental state, such as an emotion or a memory, evoked or revived by means of a stimulus. The term was introduced by the German biologist Richard Semon (1859-1918) in his treatise De Mneme (1912). See also redintegration. ecphorically adv. ecphore vb. \[From German ekphorieren to revive, from Greek ekphorein carrying or bringing forth, producing, or disclosing, from $e k$ out of + -phoros carrying, from pherein to bear + -ikos of or relating to]
