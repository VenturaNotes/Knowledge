## Source [^1]
- (1) Any financial position that is not perfectly offset. 
- (2) A floating-rate note in which the coupon is paid monthly but the interest rate paid is that applicable to a note of longer maturity. 
- (3) The situation in which the assets (loans made) and liabilities (deposits taken) of a bank are not matched, typically because the bank borrows short-term and lends long-term. This is usually acceptable as long as deposits keep coming in. See ASSET-LIABILITY MANAGEMENT; GAP.
