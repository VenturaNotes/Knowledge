## Source [^1]
- A person appointed by a court after the presentation of a winding-up petition on a company (see COMPULSORY LIQUIDATION). The provisional liquidator, whose powers are limited, has to protect the interests of all the parties involved until the winding-up order is made. The official receiver is normally appointed to this post. See also LIQUIDATOR.
