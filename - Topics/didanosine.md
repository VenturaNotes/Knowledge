## Source [^1]
- (ddI) $n$. an antiretroviral drug that interferes with the action of the enzyme reverse transcriptase, by means of which HIV, the cause of AIDS, is able to incorporate itself into the human host cell. In combination with other antiretroviral drugs, it is used to treat HIV infection and AIDS. Possible side-effects include damage to nerves, severe pancreatitis, nausea, vomiting, and headache.
