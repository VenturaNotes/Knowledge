## Source [^1]
- (magnetic induction) (symbol: $\pmb{B}$; unit: tesla (weber/meter$^2$)) The fundamental force vector in magnetism; the magnetic analogue of the electric field $\pmb{E}$. Both a magnet and a current-carrying coil exert forces on other coils or magnets. The magnetic flux density produced by such magnets or coils is a vector quantity and lines of flux can be drawn whose direction at any point is the direction of magnetic flux density. The value of $\pmb{B}$ is given by the number of lines of flux per unit area and is expressed by the equation$$d \boldsymbol {F} = I (\mathrm {d s} \times \boldsymbol {B})$$where $\mathrm{d}\boldsymbol{F}$ is the force exerted due to $\boldsymbol{B}$ on an element of length ds of wire carrying a current $I$. This defines the unit of magnetic flux density as that which exerts a force of one newton on a wire of length one metre carrying a current of one ampere.
