## Source [^1]
- $n$. Any of a number of theories according to which the perception of objects proceeds by recognizing individual features (1), such as a pair of wheels, a pair of pedals, a handlebar, a saddle, and an M-shaped metal frame, and assembling them to form a coherent pattern, in this example a bicycle, which is then identified as such. Also called feature abstraction theory. Compare feature integration THEORY, TOP-DOWN PROCESSING.
