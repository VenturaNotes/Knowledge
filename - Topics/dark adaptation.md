## Source [^1]
- The changes that need to take place in the eye when an animal moves from a brightly lit environment to a relatively dark one to enable objects to be seen clearly. On moving to a darker environment, the pupils dilate and rhodopsin—the pigment in the rod cells that is broken down in bright light—is regenerated from its constituents.
