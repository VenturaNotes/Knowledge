## Source [^1]
- The intrinsic angular momentum possessed by all elementary particles, including the electron and proton. An atomic electron also possesses angular momentum as a result of its orbital motion. Spin is a quantized quantity, the spin quantum number (or spin), $s$, of particles having either a half integral or integral value. Both electron and proton have spin.
- The magnetic fields produced by an atomic electron's spin and orbital motion interact in such a way that the electron can exist in either of two closely spaced energy levels. The spin in one level is $+$ and in the other it is $-$. Because of its spin an electron has an intrinsic magnetic moment.
