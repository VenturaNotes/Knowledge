## Source [^1]
- $n$. In the interdependence theory of the US social psychologists Harold H(arding) Kelley (1921-2003) and John W(alter) Thibaut (1917-86), expounded in their book The Social Psychology of Groups (1959) and developed further in Interpersonal Relations: A Theory of Interdependence (1978), an interpersonal situation involving two people, $A$ and $B$, in which ' $A$ 's control over $B$ 's outcomes is contingent on what $B$ does; therefore, they jointly affect B's outcomes' (1978, p. 31).
