## Source [^1]
- (1) A method of transmitting mains electrical power at constant voltage around the country on the national grid. Alternating-current transmission allows the use of transformers for step-up and step-down of the voltage. 
- (2) A method of transmission used in television in which the direct-current component of the luminance signal is not transmitted. A direct-current restorer must be used in this form of transmission. Compare DIRECT-CURRENT TRANSMISSION.
