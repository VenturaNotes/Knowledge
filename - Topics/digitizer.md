## Source [^1]
- (1) A device that produces a digital representation of an object. Digitizers use various technologies$\textemdash$optics, acoustics, magnetostriction, electromagnetics, resistance, capacitance, etc.$\textemdash$to calculate the position of the stylus or puck that is used to input the data values. A digitizer can be two- or three-dimensional. A common application is to capture information related to schematic line drawings. 
- (2) Another name for quantizer, but generally implying that the output is encoded into binary numbers.
