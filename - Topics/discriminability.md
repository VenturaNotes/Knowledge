## Source [^1]
- $n$. The extent to which stimuli can be distinguished from one another or discriminated. In signal detection theory, it is more specifically an index of the extent to which a signal can be detected or distinguished from noise, and it is also called $d$ prime or $d$ '. discriminable adj. \[From Latin discriminare to divide, from discrimen a division, from discernere to divide, from dis apart + cernere to separate + -abilitas capacity, from habilis able]
