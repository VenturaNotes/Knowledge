## Source [^1]
- $n$. 
- (1) the skin. 
- (2) a membrane or layer of tissue covering any organ of the body.
## Source[^2]
- (1) The outermost body layer of an animal, characteristically comprising a layer of living cells$\textemdash$the epidermis$\textemdash$together with a superficial protective coat, which may be a secreted hardened cuticle, as in arthropods, or dead keratinized cells, as in vertebrates (see SKIN). 
- (2) The outer protective covering of a plant ovule. It is perforated by a small pore, the micropyle. Usually two integuments are present in angiosperms and one in gymnosperms. After fertilization the integuments form the testa of the seed.
