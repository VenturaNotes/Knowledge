## Source [^1]
- $n$. (in medicine) 
- (1) a strong reason for believing that a particular course of action is desirable. In a wounded patient, the loss of blood, which would lead to circulatory collapse, is an indication for blood transfusion. 
- (2) any of the conditions for which a particular drug treatment may be prescribed, as defined by its licence. Compare CONTRAINDICATION.
