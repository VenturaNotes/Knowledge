## Source [^1]
- $n$. a technique for identifying antigenic fractions in a serum. The components of the serum are separated by electrophoresis and allowed to diffuse through agar gel towards a particular antiserum. Where the antibody meets its antigen, a band of precipitation occurs. See also PRECIPITIN.
## Source[^2]
- An analytical technique used to identify antigens that have been separated by electrophoresis. The gel medium used for the electrophoresis is irrigated with specific antibodies for the required antigens: when an antigen meets its corresponding antibody a precipitate forms, which can be seen and isolated.
