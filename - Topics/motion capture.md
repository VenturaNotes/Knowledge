## Source [^1]
- $n$. A phenomenon of apparent movement in which a target image moving in one direction against a background of randomly moving elements causes those elements to appear to be moving in the same direction as the target.
## Source[^2]
- Techniques to record details of the motion of physical objects. Most involve visible markers of some form, which are attached to various points of interest on the physical object, such as extremities or joints; the images of these markers are then tracked in successive frames of a video stream. More recent techniques use image processing to identify object parts and then track their motion. A significant use of motion capture is in determining natural human motion for video games or films with CGI characters.
