## Source [^1]
- $n$. In psychometrics, the extent to which the items of a test are appropriate to the content domain of the test. For example, a test used for employment selection has high content validity if the test items cover the abilities, skills, or competencies required to perform the job and do not cover other irrelevant attributes. See also DOMAIN-REFERENCED TEST, DOMAIN SAMPLING.
