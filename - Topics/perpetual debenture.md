## Source [^1]
- (perpetual bond) 
- (1) A bond or debenture that can never be redeemed, i.e. the issuer has neither the right nor the obligation to repay the principal. Usually interest is paid at a constant rate, or at a fixed margin over a benchmark rate. See IRREDEEMABLE SECURITIES.
- (2) A bond or debenture that cannot be redeemed on demand.
