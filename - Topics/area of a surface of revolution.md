## Source [^1]
- Let $y = f(x)$ be the graph of a function $f$ such that $f'$ is continuous on $[a,b]$ and $f(x) \ge 0$ for all x in $[a,b]$. The area of the surface obtained by rotating, through one revolution about the x-axis, the arc of the curve $y = f(x)$ between $x=a$ and $x=b$, equals $$2\pi \int_a^b f(x)\sqrt{1 +(f'(x))^2}\space dx.$$
### Parametric Form
- For the curve $x = x(t)$, $y= y(t)\space (t \in [\alpha, \beta])$, the surface area equals $$2 \pi \int_{\alpha}^{\beta}y\sqrt{(\frac{dx}{dt})^2+ (\frac{dy}{dt})^2}\space dt.$$
### Polar Form
- For the curve $r = r(\theta)\space (\alpha \le \theta \le \beta)$, the surface area equals $$2\pi \int_{\epsilon}^{\beta} rsin\theta\sqrt{r^2+(\frac{dr}{d\theta})^2}\space d \theta$$
