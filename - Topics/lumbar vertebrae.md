## Source [^1]
- the five bones of the backbone that are situated between the thoracic vertebrae and the sacrum, in the lower part of the back. They are the largest of the unfused vertebrae and have stout processes for attachment of the strong muscles of the lower back. See also VERTEBRA.
## Source[^2]
- The vertebrae in the region of the lower back. They occur below the thoracic vertebrae and above the sacral vertebrae. In mammals they bear processes for the attachment of back muscles.
