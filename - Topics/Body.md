## Source [^1]
- (payload) The part of a cell or packet in a network that holds the information supplied by the end-user for transmission from the sender to the receiver.
## Source[^2]
- An object in the real world idealized in a mathematical model as a particle, a rigid body or an elastic body, for example.
## Source[^3]
- n. 
- (1) an entire animal organism. 
- (2) the trunk of an individual, excluding the limbs. 
- (3) the main or largest part of an organ (such as the stomach or uterus). 
- (4) a solid discrete mass of tissue; e.g. the carotid body. See also CORPUS.
