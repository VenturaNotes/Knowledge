## Source [^1]
- (electronic learning) Any form of education or training that uses computer technology as an essential part of the teaching process. The term covers a broad range of goals and methodologies, but two features frequently found are that computerized data and programs substitute partly or completely for the human teacher; and that students engage with all or part of the course remotely, for example over the Internet from their homes.

## Source[^2]
- eLearning refers to the process by which learning occurs electronically. It can involve something as simple as watching a YouTube video or taking a professional development webinar. It can also be as intense as taking an accredited class to get a college degree. eLearning has become an extremely popular way of engaging in necessary professional development initiatives.
## Source[^3]
- (electronic learning) Any form of training or education that uses computer technology as an essential part of the teaching process. Typically, computerized data and programs substitute partly or completely for the human teacher and students engage with all or part of the course remotely (e.g. over the Internet).
