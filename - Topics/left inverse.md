## Source [^1]
- A left inverse $g: Y \to X$ of a map $f: X \to Y$ satisfies $g(f(x)) = x$ for all $x$ in $X$. If $X$ is non-empty, the existence of a left inverse is equivalent to $f$ being one-to-one. See INVERSE, FUNCTION, RIGHT INVERSE
