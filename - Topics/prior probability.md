## Source [^1]
- The probability attached to an event before certain data are obtained. The posterior probability is the probability attached to it after the data are obtained. The posterior probability may be calculated by using Bayes' Theorem. It may then be considered as a new prior probability before further data are obtained.
## Source[^2]
- $n$. 
- (1) The base rate or relative frequency of an event or characteristic in a population. 
- (2) In Bayesian inference, the probability assigned to an event before taking empirical evidence into account. Also called an a priori probability or simply a prior. See also insufficient REASON. Compare POSTERIOR PROBABILITY.
