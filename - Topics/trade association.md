## Source [^1]
- An association of companies in the same trade, formed to represent them in negotiations with governments, unions, other trade associations, etc., and to keep members informed of new developments affecting the trade. Trade associations also frequently draw up contracts for their members to use and provide arbitration procedures to settle disputes between members.
## Source[^2]
- A voluntary body representing the firms engaged in a particular type of business. Trade associations promote the collection and exchange of information and discussion of technical standards; they also lobby government concerning legislation affecting the industry, and possible subsidies to it.
