## Source [^1]
- A type of correlation diagram in which the molecular energy levels are drawn as a function of bond angles rather than as a function of intermolecular distance. These diagrams were first constructed by Arthur Donald Walsh (1916-77) in 1953, building on earlier work by Robert Mulliken. The conclusions he drew are known as Walshâ€™s rules. Walsh diagrams have been used extensively to predict molecular shapes. As with VSEPR theory, the valence electrons determine the molecular shape.
