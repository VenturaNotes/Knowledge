## Source [^1]
- (time delay) The time that elapses between operation of a circuit-breaker, relay, or similar apparatus and the response of the current in the main circuit. A time lag may be deliberately introduced into a particular system. A definite time lag is a predetermined interval, sometimes adjustable, that is independent of the magnitude of the electrical quantity causing the operation. An inverse time lag is a delay time that is an inverse function of that quantity. See also OVERCURRENT RELEASE.
