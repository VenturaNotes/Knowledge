- (1) An American buys an entertainment system that was manufactured in China. How does the U.S. national income accounts treat this transaction?
	- Net exports and GDP both fall
		- GDP is the sum of [[consumption]], investment, government spending, and net exports (in equation form: GDP = C + I + G + X).
		- In this case, as the American is buying something made in China, net exports would fall, causing GDP to fall
- (2) Which of the following would be included in U.S. GDP calculations?
	- A worker donating $200 to the [[Red Cross]]
		- For a transaction to be captured in GDP calculations, it needs to be recorded and reported to the government
		- Countries such as the [[United Kingdom]] and [[Germany]] are looking at ways to include [[parallel market]] transactions as part of their GDP
		- Not included in U.S. GDP calculations
			- An auto mechanic fixing his dentist's car for a filling
			- A stay-at-home dad providing childcare for his children
			- High school students spending their Saturdays building homes for the homeless
			- A college student paying another student $50 in cash for a used laptop
