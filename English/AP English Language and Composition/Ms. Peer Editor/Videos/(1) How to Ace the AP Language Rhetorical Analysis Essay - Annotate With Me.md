## The prompt
- Rhetorical Analysis Essay
	- Given a passage and asked to analyze the rhetorical devices used by the speaker or author of the passage
	- 40 minutes to complete essay
		- Annotate & Plan: 5-8min
		- Write Essay: 32-35 min
	- This question counts for one-third of the total essay section score.
- The passage below is the <mark style="background: #FFB8EBA6;">opening</mark> to a speech made in 1960 by <mark style="background: #FF5582A6;">American journalist and politician</mark> Clare Boothe Luce <mark style="background: #ABF7F7A6;">to journalists at the Women's National Press Club.</mark>  In this speech, Luce went on to <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">criticize</mark> the tendency of the American press to <mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;">sacrifice journalistic integrity in favor of the perceived public demand for sensationalist stories.</mark> Read the passage carefully. Then, in a well-developed essay, analyze how Luce uses this introduction to prepare the audience for her message. Support your analysis of her rhetoric with specific references to the text. 
	- <mark style="background: #FFB8EBA6;">Important to notice that we're not reading the whole speech. Just the introduction/opening</mark>
	- <mark style="background: #FF5582A6;">Speaker</mark>
	- <mark style="background: #ABF7F7A6;">Audience</mark>
	- <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">Tone of speech will be critical</mark>
	- <mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;">Introduction is about how the American press tends to exaggerate and not tell the truth about new stories because they are looking for sensationalist news. Will analyze how the speaker uses the introduction to prepare the audience for her message. That is the purpose of introduction in total</mark>

## Steps
- Introduction
	- 2-3 sentences
		- Explain the purpose of the excerpt
	- Thesis
- Body Paragraphs
	- Topic Sentence (TS): refer to specific rhetorical device used by speaker
	- include examples from the text
	- Explain the effect of each example on audience
	- Tie back to overall purpose
- Conclusion
	- Reiterate thesis
	- Describe how the author succeeds in relaying the message
