	- Spanning Vectors
	- Checking if the set of vectors span the space $\mathbb{R}^2$ 
	- What does [[span]] mean?
		- It means that we should be able to write any arbitrary element of $\mathbb{R}^2$ 
		- Using generic vector $\begin{bmatrix}a \\ b \end{bmatrix}$
		- Can we weight $v_1$ and $v_2$ to yield the vector  $\begin{bmatrix}a \\ b \end{bmatrix}$? 
			- So can I write any vector I choose $\begin{bmatrix}a \\ b \end{bmatrix}$ as a weighted combination of $v_1$ and $v_2$. This is what it means to span $\mathbb{R}^2$ if any vector in $\mathbb{R}^2$ can be written as a linear combination of $v_1$ and $v_2$ 
			- Vector formulation of equation
				- $x_1 \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}+ x_2 \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}a \\ b \end{bmatrix}$
			- Matrix formulation of equation
				-  $\begin{bmatrix}1 & 1 \\ 1 & -2\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}a \\ b \end{bmatrix}$
		- These vectors do span $\mathbb{R}^2$ as we are able to find solutions to the equation
