	- Conjecture - An educated guess based on what is known
	- Inductive Reasoning - Process of using a number of specific examples to arrive at a conjecture
	- Counterexample - example that is not true, or false
	- Although many things can be shown to be true with inductive reasoning, some conjectures can actually be false. This is why deductive reasoning is more sound than inductive reasoning
	- Deductive Reasoning - uses facts, definitions, properties, and laws to reach a conclusion
