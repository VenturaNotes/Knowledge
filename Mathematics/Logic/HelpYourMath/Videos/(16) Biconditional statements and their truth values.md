- Biconditional statements
	- Recall that a compound statement is a biconditional is "if and only if" or "iff" with notation $\iff$
		- p $\iff$ q
	- It's called biconditional because it's the conjunction of two conditional statements
		- $p \implies q \land q \implies p$
- Truth Table for a biconditional statement
	- Let's consider when the biconditional is true
		- The biconditional is true when p and q have the same truth value
