	- The [[Converse|converse]] of a statement $p \implies q$ is the statement $q \implies p$ 
		- Not logically equivalent (truth table will be different)
	- If it's a dog, then it's a mammal (True)
		- <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">Converse</mark>: If it's a mammal, then it's a dog (False)
	- The [[inverse]] of a statement $p \implies q$ is the statement $\lnot p \implies \lnot q$
		- First statement equivalent to $\lnot q \implies \lnot p$
		- Second statement is the converse of the <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">contrapositive</mark>
	- Statement and contrapositive are logically equivalent
	- <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">Inverse</mark> is logically equivalent to the <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">converse</mark>
	- If it's a dog, then it's a mammal (True)
		- Inverse: If it's not a dog, then it's not a mammal (False)
	- Logical statement and inverse are not logically equivlaent
