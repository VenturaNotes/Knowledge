	- Additive and Multiplicative Principles
		- For your college interview, you must wear a tie. You own 5 regular (boring) ties and 3 (cool) bow ties
		- How many choices do you have for your neck-wear?
			- 8 choices 
			- Additive principle example
		- You realize that the interview is for clown college, so you should probably wear both a regular tie and a bow tie. How many choices do you have now?
			- 15 choices
			- Multiplicative principle example
		- For the rest of your outfit, you have 8 shirts, 3 skirts, 5 pants, and 4 dresses. You want to select either a shirt to wear with a skirt or pants, or just a dress. How many outfits do you have to choose from? 
			- Mixture of additive and multiplicative principles
