	- Less common [[Laws of logic]]
		- These laws are used to make solving complex logical questions simpler
		- Remember the definitions of tautology and contradiction:
			- [[Tautology]]: A logical statement that is always true
			- [[Contradiction]]: A logical statement that is always false
		- For the rest of this video, assume T is a tautology and F is a contradiction
	- (1) [[Distributive Law]]
		- $p \land (q \lor r) \leftrightarrow$ $(p \land q) \lor (p \land r)$
		- $p \lor (q \land r) \leftrightarrow$ $(p \lor q) \land (p \lor r)$
	- (2) [[Absorption Laws]] 
		- $p \land (p \lor q) \leftrightarrow p$
		- $p \lor (p \land q) \leftrightarrow p$
	- (3) [[Commutativity Laws]]
		- $p \land q \leftrightarrow q \land p$
		- $p \lor q \leftrightarrow q \lor p$
	- (4) [[Associativity Law]]
		- $p \land (q \land r) \leftrightarrow (p \land q) \land r$
	- (5) [[Inverse Laws]]
		- $p \land \lnot p \leftrightarrow F$
		- $p \lor \lnot p \leftrightarrow T$
	- (6) [[Conditional Law]]
		- $p \to q \leftrightarrow \lnot p \lor q$

	- Practice
		- Using logic laws to simplify the following
		- [[Double negation]]
			- $\lnot \lnot p$ is p
		- [[Identity laws]]
			- $p \lor F$ is p
