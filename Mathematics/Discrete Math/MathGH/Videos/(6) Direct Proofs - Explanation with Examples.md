	- [[direct proof|direct proofs]]: Introduction and Practice
		- Direct proofs: if p, then q
		- Most straightforward type of proof, simply assume p is true then try to arrive at the conclusion that q must be true
	- Practice
		- (1) Prove: If n is odd, then $n^2$ is odd
		- (2) Let a, b, and c be integers with a $\ne$ 0 and b $\ne$ 0. Assume that a|b and b|c. Prove that a|c
			- Multiplying two integers is always going to be another integer
		- (3) Prove: Every odd integer is a difference of two squares
