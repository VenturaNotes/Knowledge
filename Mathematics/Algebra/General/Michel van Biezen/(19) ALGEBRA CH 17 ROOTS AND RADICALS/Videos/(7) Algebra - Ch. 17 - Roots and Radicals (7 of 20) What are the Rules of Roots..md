	- i is an imaginary number
	- the square root of -1 is an imaginary number
	- By definition, i squared is negative 1
	- In the real number set, there is no such thing as multiplying a number by itself and getting a negative result. Can never get a negative number by multiplying 2 numbers together
		- If they're both positive, you get a positive result
		- If they're both negative, you get a negative result
	- Imaginary number was created so that when you multiply a number by itself, you can get a negative result.
	- $6i$ 
		- We like to write the "number" first and "i" second in algebra
		- In electrical engineering, we like to write the "i" first and then "6" ($i6$)
			- Order is reversed for whatever reason.
