	- When thinking of imaginary numbers think like this:
		- $i^5$ = $\sqrt{-1}$  $\sqrt{-1}$  $\sqrt{-1}$  $\sqrt{-1}$  $\sqrt{-1}$ 
			- = (-1)(-1)(-1)
			- = -1
	- Memorize these imaginary numbers (these values will keep repeating on forever)
		- $i = \sqrt{-1}$
		- $i^2$ = -1
		- $i^3 = -i$
		- $i^4$ = 1
	- Beginning of repeating values
		- $i^5$ = $\sqrt{-1}$
	- There is no equivalence of the 4th root of -1
		- No solution
		- Not a real number
