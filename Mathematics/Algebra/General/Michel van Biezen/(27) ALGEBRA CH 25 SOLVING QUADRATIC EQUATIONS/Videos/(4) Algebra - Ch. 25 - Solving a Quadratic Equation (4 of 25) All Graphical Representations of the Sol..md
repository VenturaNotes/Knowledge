- ![[Screenshot 2023-02-18 at 4.09.01 AM.png]]
	- All possible representations of the way solutions can come out when trying to solve quadratic equations.
	- Can have the parabola open upward (top graphs) or have the parabola open downward (bottom graphs)
	- Parabola opens <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">upward</mark>
		- If <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">lowest</mark> point is <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">below</mark> the x-axis, will have 2 solutions
		- If <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">lowest</mark> point is <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">on</mark> the x-axis, will have 1 solution
		- If <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">lowest</mark> point is <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">above</mark> the x-axis, will have no solutions (or 2 imaginary solutions)
	- Parabola opens <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">downward</mark>
		- If <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">highest</mark> point is <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">above</mark> the x-axis, will have 2 solutions
		- If <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">highest</mark> point is <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">on</mark> the x-axis, will have 1 solution
		- If <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">highest</mark> point is <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">below</mark> the x-axis, will have no solutions (or 2 imaginary solutions)
