- Propositional Connectives
	- “[[Conjunction]]” ([Lal, 2017, p. 2](zotero://select/library/items/D5DDVLY8)) ([pdf](zotero://open-pdf/library/items/ED4BVI3C?page=17&annotation=9NUBTR8B))
		- Propositional Connective 'and' conjoins two statements
		- P and Q are statements
		- $P \land Q$ denotes the conjunction P and Q
		- Example
			- “Charu is a brave girl, and Garima is an honest girl.” ([Lal, 2017, p. 1](zotero://select/library/items/D5DDVLY8)) ([pdf](zotero://open-pdf/library/items/ED4BVI3C?page=16&annotation=22T7WYIP))

		- "or"
		- $P \lor Q$ denotes disjunction of P or Q
		- Example
			- "Sun rises from the east or sun rises from the west.” ([Lal, 2017, p. 1](zotero://select/library/items/D5DDVLY8)) ([pdf](zotero://open-pdf/library/items/ED4BVI3C?page=16&annotation=C47INZY4))
	- “[[Negation]]” ([Lal, 2017, p. 2](zotero://select/library/items/D5DDVLY8)) ([pdf](zotero://open-pdf/library/items/ED4BVI3C?page=17&annotation=2CY8KNFN))
		- not
		- $\lnot P$
		- Example
			- Shipra will go to school
			- $\lnot$(Shipra will go to school)
	- “[[Conditional statement]]” ([Lal, 2017, p. 2](zotero://select/library/items/D5DDVLY8)) ([pdf](zotero://open-pdf/library/items/ED4BVI3C?page=17&annotation=X8GRUURF))
		- If P, then Q
			- P is the [[antecedent]] or [[hypothesis]]
			- Q is the [[consequent]] or [[conclusion]]
		- Example
			- “If Gaurav works hard, then he will pass.” ([Lal, 2017, p. 1](zotero://select/library/items/D5DDVLY8)) ([pdf](zotero://open-pdf/library/items/ED4BVI3C?page=16&annotation=RXHLCQZI))
		- Alternatives
			- If P, then Q
			- Q is a necessary condition for P
			- P is a sufficient condition for Q
	- “[[Implication]]” ([Lal, 2017, p. 2](zotero://select/library/items/D5DDVLY8)) ([pdf](zotero://open-pdf/library/items/ED4BVI3C?page=17&annotation=25T73CZ7))

			- Q is the <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">consequent</mark> or <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">conclusion</mark>

### (1.3) Quantifiers
- [[Universal Quantifier]]
	- $\forall$: For all
		- Called the universal quantifier
	- “For every river, there is an origin.” ([Lal, 2017, p. 1](zotero://select/library/items/D5DDVLY8)) ([pdf](zotero://open-pdf/library/items/ED4BVI3C?page=16&annotation=7W7A9KC2))
		- For every x, 'x is a river' implies 'x has an origin'.
		- 'For every x, P(x)' where P(x) is a valid statement involving x
		- May be represented as
			- $\forall$x('x is a river' $\implies$ 'x has an origin')
- [[Existential Quantifier]]
	- $\exists$x: there exists
		- 'There exists x, P(x)', where 'P(x)' is a statement involving x
	- Example: There is a man who is immortal
		- “‘$\exists$x,‘x is a man’ and ‘x is immortal’.’.” ([Lal, 2017, p. 7](zotero://select/library/items/D5DDVLY8)) ([pdf](zotero://open-pdf/library/items/ED4BVI3C?page=22&annotation=LQ2A9LC4))
	- Parenthesis $()$ and brackets $[]$ will be used to limit scope of propositional connectives and quantifiers
- <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">Negation of a statement formula involving Quantifiers</mark>
	- "Every river has an origin"
		- Universal quantifier: $\forall$x('x is a river' $\implies$ 'x has an origin')
			- Statement false iff there is a river which has no origin
	- "Every man is mortal"
		- Universal Quantifier: $\forall x$('x is a man' $\implies$ 'x is mortal')
			- Statement false iff there is a man who is immortal
	- "There is a river which has no origin"
		- Existential Quantifier: “‘$\exists$x, (‘x is a river’ and ‘x has no origin’.’.
			- Statement false iff "Every river has an origin"

### (1.4) Tautology and Logical Equivalences
- “A statement formula is called a [[tautology]] if its [[truth value]] is always T irrespective of the truth values of its [[atomic statement variables]].” ([Lal, 2017, p. 8](zotero://select/library/items/D5DDVLY8)) ([pdf](zotero://open-pdf/library/items/ED4BVI3C?page=23&annotation=M9NX3LGU))
- “statement formula is called a [[contradiction]] if its truth value is always F irrespective of the truth values of its atomic statement variables.” ([Lal, 2017, p. 8](zotero://select/library/items/D5DDVLY8)) ([pdf](zotero://open-pdf/library/items/ED4BVI3C?page=23&annotation=5I98KG6F))
	- Negation of tautology is contradiction and negation of contradiction is tautology
- “examples in Sect. 1.2 except the Example 1.2.2 are tautologies.” ([Lal, 2017, p. 8](zotero://select/library/items/D5DDVLY8)) ([pdf](zotero://open-pdf/library/items/ED4BVI3C?page=23&annotation=6CS9IDU3))
	- $P \land \lnot P$
		- This is a contradiction
