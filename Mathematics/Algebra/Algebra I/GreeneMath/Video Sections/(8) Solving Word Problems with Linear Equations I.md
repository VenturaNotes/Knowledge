- Solving a [[word Problem]]: ^6be7c4
	- (1) Read the problem carefully and determine what you are asked to find
	- (2) Assign a variable to represent the unknown
	- (3) Write out an equation
	- (4) Solve the equation
	- (5) State the answer in terms of the problem.
	- (6) Check the result (make sure it is reasonable)

## Sums of Quantities
- These problems involve finding two individual amounts, when the sum is known

### Examples
- The Phantoms won 8 more games than the Aztecs last year. Their combined wins were 32. Find the number of games won by each team last year.
	- ![[Screenshot 2023-02-06 at 3.09.05 PM.png|400]]
		- It doesn't matter which one you assign the x to. The answer here is that the <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">Phantoms won 20 games</mark> and the <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">Aztecs won 12 games</mark> last year
- There are three girls in a family: Jessica, Jennifer, and Ashley. Ashley is 3 years older than Jessica. Jennifer is 5 years younger than Jessica. Their combined age is 34. Find the age of each girl
	- ![[Screenshot 2023-02-06 at 3.15.56 PM.png]]
		- Can check if the ages add up to 34

## Consecutive Integer Problems:
- When two integers differ by 1, they are called [[consecutive integers]]
	- 2, 3
	- 14, 15
	- 123, 124

### Examples
- Two pages that face each other in a pamphlet have 33 as the sum of the page numbers. What are the page numbers?
